Webby Awards 2000 - A Step Towards Webby Awards 2001
by: Francis Mark Santos

They say failures make you work hard and do better the next time. Well, we sure do. After failing to bag that elusive Webby Awards for Education trophy for the second time, we are not giving up yet.

Like any local award-giving bodies, the Philippine Webby Awards is marred by controversies. There were questions raised on free e-mail accounts being used to vote for the People's Choice, the security of the organizer's servers and the proper categorizing of the nominees. Our web site fell prey on the categories issue, where we were previously up for the School category where we would contend for the title with the other schools' web sites. On the last minute, the School Category and we were included in the Education Category with schools, online magazines, and publications. To add insult to injury, DLSU's web site and even other universities' or schools' web sites were not included among the finalists for the merged category. Oh well, can't win 'em all! We're thinking of joining another award-giving body. At least I got a shirt this time.

Even though we lost the Webby last year, we already know that our web site is a "winner" in its own right. And maybe in the next awards night, we can find ourselves standing in the podium and making our speeches.

From Double Dare to DOCU Dare
by: Francis Mark Santos

The Systems Management Office together with the SITE students organized a game show patterned after Nickelodeon's Double Dare for a TGIF Activity. The game show is a question and answer type of game where the contestants of one team can dare the other team for the answer if they were stumped by the question or if they want to rack up on points easily.
