An Invitation!!!

This coming January 26, SMO's practicum students from CCS together with Mr. Mark Rufino of SMO will be offering another fun-filled Friday activity that you surely would not want to miss!

Do you guys remember ever watching NICKELODEON? Well, if you do, I'm sure you'll remember the game DOUBLE DARE! The game is played by two groups who are pitted against each other in answering a series of questions. If one group is unable to answer the question thrown their way, they have the option to DARE the other group who will be given the chance to answer it. If the other group is still at a loss, then they can DOUBLE DARE the first group. The group is then given a choice: answer the question or face the PHYSICAL CHALLENGE!

Interested? If you are, then be ready to join DOUBLE DARE- ITC style! This Friday activity will start at 5 PM and the venue will be the ITC conference room. See you guys there!

Getting to know the DOC Group
by: Charleston Young

The DOC Group is composed of Pamela Garcia, Aimee delos Santos, Clarice Tantuco, and Charleston Young. We feel very relaxed and at home when working with the ITC personnel. This is because we know that the ITC personnel are very friendly and are very helpful to us. They are happy people and are fun to be with. Every gathering or meeting we have attended so far have been ok and is actually fun-filled.

We feel that the workload is just right but we know that it won't be easy. In such a short span of time, we were able to learn a lot of new things that we could not have learned in our other subjects. We are all thankful to the ITC personnel and we hope to have more fun working with them. Projects assigned to the DOC group as of January 19, 2000 are:

  • Stat Counter - Pam and Charles will research and then give a report of the pro's and con's of the stat counters available on the Internet. Later, the group will recommend whether it is better to outsource or create DLSU's stat counter. Some of the major features of the stat counter are the tracking of unique visitors, the referring site, the search keywords used by the visitors, and the country/continent where the visitors belong to.
  • Message Board - Aimee and Clarice will research and look at the features of existing message boards. The group will give their advice on what features are needed or necessary for DLSU to have. The group will then be able to create a message board for DLSU. The message board will serve as a means of communication for all DLSU faculty members and students and will improve communication among them.
  • Work Instructions for System Investigation Guideline Forms - The work instructions documentation will serve as sample instructions and will teach the users on how to fill out the forms used in the system investigation guideline properly.
  • Websites -
    • OJT students Home Page -The OJT students website is all about us! It will contain pictures and some personal information. Assigned to do this are Aimee and Clarice.
    • La Salle Newsletter -The La Salle Newsletter will be converted to an Avantgo downloadable webpage for the Palm Pilot. Assigned to do this are Pam and Charles.
  • Maintenance Documents -
    • Perfective (Pam)
    • Preventive (Charles)
    • Adaptive (Clarice)
    • Corrective (Aimee)

SMO's Winning the 5S Jingle Contest during the ITC Christmas Party
by: Aimee delos Santos

Last year's Christmas party was truly an eventful one. The food was great, the music was excellent and who can forget the jingle sung by the SMO staffers. This group not only showed their wit and skill off the stage, but also "on stage" as well.

The rules were simple. Each group was to prepare a jingle relating to the theme "5S". The criteria for judging are as follows:

Content 40%
Audience Impact 20%
Timing 20%
Creativity 20%
Total: -----
  100%

SMO won the very much-coveted award with a total score of 92%.

"Ang 5S Theme" captured the hearts of the audience even before the song was finished. Sung to the tune of Jingle Bells, this Tagalog song not only promoted the Christmas spirit within but it also highlighted the team's creativity by applying the 5S theme into the said song.

Everyone went wild when the song "Five to One" was sung. Composed by SMO's self-declared "crush ng bayan", this song not only hit the song charts several times but it also helped discover SMO's version of Ryan Cayabyab.

To the SMO team, CONGRATULATIONS!
