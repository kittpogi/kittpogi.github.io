De La Salle University Launches Portal
 by: Clarice Tantuco, Edited by: Francis Mark Santos

After launching its wireless information services last year, De La Salle University (Manila and PSI) now launches its own Internet portal dubbed "My.LaSalle". The Information Technology Center (ITC) of DLSU conceptualized the portal from a single idea - to give each De La Salle Internet account holder access to the University's different information services anytime, anywhere. This portal is a one-stop system that enables an active account holder to use the different information services, like subject availability, subject schedules, campus calendar, campus news and events, and e-mail using Outlook Web Access.

The system recognizes its users if they are active students or members of the faculty and will display the appropriate features or settings which their user level access allows them to have. Students can check subjects available during enrollment or inquire about their academic status without going to the Registrar's Office. They can also inquire on campus news and activities. On the other hand, faculty members can take a look at their class schedules or download class lists for their use. Eventually, the system will allow students to enroll online.

ITC is continuously developing the portal to keep up with the growing needs of its customers and the amazing pace of technology. It is hoped that soon, everyone in the DLSU Community can say that the My.LaSalle System is truly his/her own.

First in Wireless Information Service
by: Francis Mark Santos

De La Salle University (DLSU) asserts its position as the forerunner among the country's top higher education institutions through its De La Salle University Wireless Information Services or DLSU WISe. This was developed by DLSU's Information Technology Center (ITC). It delivers University-related information to its students, faculty, and alumni through the use of cellular phones. DLSU is the first University in the country to use this technology to disseminate information along with print media and the Internet.

This all started with the objective of making information available to DLSU's customers and a simple idea on how to maximize the potential of cellular phones. ITC realized that the technology was there but there was no existing application to harness it. Seeing that many use cellular phones, ITC developed its own system to facilitate the information in its database to be read selectively and be sent to a user's cellular phone through Short Messaging System (SMS) or text messaging. This system is available to both Globe and Smart subscribers.

Since September 11, 2000, DLSU has been providing wireless information services, such as DLSU announcements/news, availability of subjects during enrollment, dial-upnumbers, final exams schedule, course card distribution schedule, and DLSU contact information. There have been over 60,000 hits. The most recent application is a query facility for the results of the DLSU Freshman Entrance Exam. It is another step forward since DLSU started to use its web site four years ago to post Entrance Exam results over the Internet web.

To ensure that students are guided in the use of this technology, instructions are posted on the DLSU web site at http://www.dlsu.edu.ph. To know the available DLSU WISe keywords, simply send "DLSU KEYWORD" or "DLSU HELP" to 2333 (for Globe subscribers) or 211 (for Smart subscribers).

It is a bright future ahead of us and there will be more exciting possibilities as the technology develops further.
