New Site Students

The System Management Office warmly welcomes its newest batch of Students of Information Technology Enrichment Program (SITE) from the College of Computer Studies.

These SITE students, as they are more fondly called, have been assigned to develop two systems. The Computer Laboratory and Operations Information System (CIS) is being developed by a bevy of "stars" namely Edda Omandac, Sharon Pineda, Ycon Saberon, and Osang Torredondo. On the other hand, the spirited group of Ron Salamat, Kate Tinio, Nelwin Uy, and Roj Varandmal, are handling the Extra-Curricular Information System (XIS).

For the entire duration of their practicum (roughly late May to early September), they will be treated like regular employees of ITC. In other words, they will be expected to log-in and log-out, attend SMO meetings, and of course, participate in our Friday night gimmicks and monthly "food fests."

It is our fervent hope that these senior students not only gain a better understanding of the software development life cycle but also have a lot of fun during their regretfully brief stay with the ITC family.

Brian O. Co 06/14/2000

Computer Laboratory and Operations Information System (CIS)

Computer Laboratory and Operations Information System is an IS that keeps track of information with regards to Student Assistants of computer laboratory in DLSU. It includes report generation, tracking of offenses made, and computation of SA salary. Moreover, it is a software designed to deliver useful and accurate information with regards to student assistant record and billing. It also monitors laboratory services and personnel all over the University. Even if CIS covers only those student assistants of DLSU Taft, the system can also be utilized by other branch of the said University. In addition, the proponent assumes that an IT personnel will be the one in-charge of the maintenance of the program.

Project Leader: Ms. Pineda
Members: Ms. Omandac, Ms. Saberon, Ms. Torredondo

Sharon Mae Pineda 05/30/2000

Extracurricular Information System (XIS)

The Extracurricular Information System (XIS) emphasizes on the monitoring and updating of vital information regarding a student’s college activities other than those related to academics. This information would then be distributed to the different offices under the Office of the Dean of Student Affairs which would use this information to help maximize the each student’s potential. The system would also cater to the individual needs of the aforementioned offices in order to provide the university with accurate, fast, and reliable data.
