    SMO Updates
 

First System Study

The monitoring of the Credit Union System (CUS), a system used to facilitate the operations of the De La Salle Credit Cooperative Office, was turned over last February 7, 2000 by Rowena A. Santos to Loveleah V. Malig in the presence of Vivian C. Sy. To ease the turn-over, Ms. Santos continued to support Ms. Malig as System Monitor until the end of 3rd Trimester, SY 1999-2000.

As the new System Monitor, Ms. Malig was given a week dedicated solely for studying the system in preparation for monitoring the system. It was the first time that a System Study was conducted in SMO. The System Study was conceived in relation to observations of Harold Paulo D. Bautista that new System Monitors were not ready to handle systems assigned to them because they are not given enough time to study a system before they actually monitor these.  Hence, the System Study was initiated in the case of Ms. Malig, as new System Monitor of the Credit Union System.

After her System Study, Ms. Malig presented her understanding of the system to CUS Project Leader James C. Sy at the DAO Office last April 13, 2000.

According to Ms. Malig, “Studying the system gave me sufficient understanding of what CUS does. This will help me in performing my function as System Monitor. Aside from that, I also learned a lot of new programming techniques from reading the codes of the CUS programs.”

With the positive feedback on the System Study, new System Monitors will now be enforced to study systems newly assigned to them. This activity will ensure that new System Monitors will know how their systems work before they actually handle these.

Loveleah V. Malig  06/01/2000

Operations Meeting

Last May 9, 2000, the first Operations Meeting was held between SMO, represented by Rhaniel Bingcang and Vivian del Castillo-Sy, and Albert Uy and Leo Magsisi of CFO. The meeting was conceived to improve the coordination between the two offices regarding operations related to application systems being maintained by ITC.

Among the improvements in current procedures that were discussed during the meeting are: 

  • The inclusion of users in the mailing list by the Project Leaders, so that the users will know that their report requests have been queued for printing by CFO.
  • The addition of a Remarks column in the Reports File to contain information regarding delays in the printing of reports.
  • The indication of an * in the pitch column to specify that a report was printed using a laser printer, for documentation and costing purposes.
  • The separation of small (11" X 9-1/2") and large (11" X 14") reports to facilitate checking for completeness against the Transmittal Slips.

In the ITC Planning Workshop, it was discussed that the Operations Meeting will meet at least once a school year. It is hoped that this venue will provide more opportunities for SMO and CFO to improve their operations in meeting their users' needs.

Vivian del Castillo-Sy 06/13/2000

New SITE Coordinator

Brian Co was recently appointed as the new coordinator for the SITE and ETP students. He replaced erstwhile coordinator Harold Bautista. Mr. Co is currently handling four groups with a total of 16 students: two groups composed of SITE students and the rest composed of ETP students.

The SITE students are having their practicum under the wing of the Systems Management Office (SMO). The ETP students, on the other hand are having their extended thesis program by developing systems for SMO.

Mr. Co's term as coordinator would run for the entire duration of SY 2000-2001.
