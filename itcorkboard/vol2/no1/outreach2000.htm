    Outreach 2000
 

To promote camaraderie through social activities, the ITC recently had their outreach activity with the children of Hospicio de San Jose in Manila. The institution is HOME to more than three hundred people of varied ages and needs, from cradle to the later years.

ITC organized a short program for the children from 5 to 10 years old. Funds for the activity were raised through generous donations from a group of ITPs who received their longevity pay and the rest were contributions (cash and in kind) from other ITPs and SITE students.

On the afternoon of May 23, 2000, the volunteers were welcomed by about 40 energized but well-behaved children of the institution. Each ITP volunteer handled at least two children and acted as guide throughout the program. Patricia and Joel took charge of the games (Paper Dance, Candy Relay and Trip to Jerusalem) which the children truly enjoyed. An additional game of Stop Dance was also held per request of the participating children. Prizes were given to the winners of each game.

At around 3 p.m., food was served under the supervision of Agnes, JC, and Vivian. All the children enjoyed the spaghetti, hotdogs, bread slices, Jelly-Ace and orange juice that were prepared for the afternoon delight. The volunteers also joined together to enjoy the luscious meal.

Small gifts were distributed to the children after the merienda. All the 40 children received toys, crayons and coloring books, among other goodies. Other donations such as bath and laundry soaps, toothpaste, boxes of instant noodles, and others were deposited with the institution's social workers.

The program ended with a heartfelt "Thank You!" song from the children, and it was, I must say, a song that made the DAY of everyone involved. Due credit should go to the following people - Vivian, Leo, Agnes, Joel, Tricia and yours truly - who patiently coordinated the activity.

Julio Ceres R. Ibay 06/05/2000
