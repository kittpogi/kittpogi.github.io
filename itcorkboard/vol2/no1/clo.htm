Win2K Powered Computer Labs

Last month, 3 computer laboratories had a major Operating Systems (OS) upgrade alongside with the hardware upgrade from Pentium Celeron to Pentium III, 500 MHz. The computer labs namely: LS320, G302 and G404 are now operational and are currently being observed for critical errors. Visual software and other programming languages are now being tested, with the assistance of SMO, to determine whether it can support lower software versions.

Why Win2K? Windows 2000 is the total solution to our computer lab dilemmas. With its user friendly command buttons and it’s special functions and sophisticated features such as software deployment combined with unmatched Administrator Support, any user complaint or mischief is history. But as far as system security is concerned, since it is built on Win NT Technology, it is expected to be reliable in all imaginable situation. With the problems encountered before with Fortres, a security software, it is hoped that any related concern will be lessened, if not eradicated.

And lastly, if all goes well, the other computer labs will soon follow their lead and will become Win2k powered as well.

CLO Welcome Three Bachelors

With the entry of the first school year this millennium, 3 new faces are added to the CLO Staff List--the following are Jeffrey Montano a.k.a. Jeff, Christopher Madrona a.k.a. Boyet and Gary Bangud a.k.a. Gary B. They are the so-called Lab Assistants whose main task is to provide assistance to the manpower needs of CLO whether technical or a function similar to the help desk inside the computer laboratories.

Of course, this should not be seen as the end of the glorious days of the Student Assistants. They are, in fact, the necessary force that should beckon the SAs to be more proactive in their duties. That they may be able to inspire the SAs to become more assertive in dealing with faculty members and student alike. And also, to mingle with them and work with them hand in hand to provide quality service.

In addition, the dynamic trio is also expected to strengthen the basketball lineup of the office. With heights ranging from 5 feet 6 inches to 6 feet and with basketball experience they are sure to provide the team additional fire power on both sides of the court whether offense or defense.

So, whether at work or at play, these guys can surely be counted on. Let’s all wish them luck in their work and hope that they enjoy the camaraderie that we have here in the office as much as they enjoy their stay here at DLSU.

Glen D. Bertulfo 06/06/2000
