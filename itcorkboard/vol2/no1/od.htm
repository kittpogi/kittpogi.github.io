ISTC to ITC

Since it was decided by the IT System Committee to carry the same name for all the IT units of the System Schools, ISTC is now called Information Technology Center or ITC. This will be the fifth time the Center has changed its name over its 30 years of existence. As a result, the newly designed ISTC logo has to be changed, and the new one is applicable for all the IT Center of the System Schools.  The elements used in the new logo was adopted from the old ISTC logo, hence the same symbolism, the same servitude, under a new name. To remind us of what our logo speaks about the Center and what each ITP should embody, here’s another run-down of the logo elements and their meaning:

The main element is the acronym ITC – which of course stands for "Information Technology Center".

The five-pointed star at the upper left-corner of the acronym is the Signum Fidei star of DLSU. It is the sign of faith and is the symbol of the La Salle Brothers (Fraternum Scholarum Christianarum). It symbolizes ITC’s commitment to the Lasallian values Religio, Mores and Cultura. The star also means hope.

The radial lines inside the text signify ITC’s vision to shine and to be involved with cross-functional and cross organizational teams in the DLS System and other educational communities. Graphically, it gives an illusion of movement that suggests vitality in service.

The clockwise rotary orbit (isometrical) in vibrant green tone illustrates that of a computer network around the DLSU system, community, and the world with a high-speed information system at work. It also suggests a revolutionary in information technology and communication advancement through worldwide networking.

The slanting concept of the acronym ‘ITC’ into an oval shape orbit (as if it were high up in space) gives it a more dynamic profile – simulates the feeling of ascent; summarizing a fast and reliable service, management and human resource development towards the next millennium.
