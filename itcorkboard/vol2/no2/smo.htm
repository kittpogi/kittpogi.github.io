Oracle Financials

By October 30, 2000, the Accounting Office of the Manila campus should be using only Oracle Financials for its Accounts Receivables (AR), Accounts Payables (AP), and General Ledger (GL) sections.  Targeting October 27 as the date when all AR, AP and GL transactions for SY 2000-2001 should have been entered, the Manila team hopes to have abandoned parallel implementation of its 3 modules on both Oracle Financials and the DLS-FAST systems.

It was October of last year when the Manila team first attempted to implement the Oracle Financials system for AR, AP and GL.  Having encountered difficulties in being “simultaneously live” on all 3 modules, the Manila team decided to try to implement the system for the second and last time.  The set-up of all 3 modules had to be redone, and the new starting date was established – June 1, 2000.  Data from June 1, 2000 have since been entered for all 3 modules.  As of October 5, 2000, the GL section was finishing data entry of its June and August transactions; the AP section finished its June and August transactions, and was finishing its July transactions; the AR section was just starting on its June transactions.  The AR section has since employed additional contractual staff to enter the remaining transactions so these could be entered by the agreed target date.

Meanwhile, activities for the Payroll module have already commenced.  Training on the module was already conducted with all system school representatives in attendance.  Currently, the Manila team is setting up its test environment.  Target date for parallel implementation in the Manila campus is February 2001.

Sammy Mallare is the Project Manager of the Oracle Financials System.  The Manila team is composed of Kay Bondoc (GL, Payroll), Noel Santiago (GL), Feny Garcia (GL), Glo Bacani (GL), Ting Palmes (AP), June Fajardo (AP), Fritzie Vergara (AR), Janet Silva (AR), and Jo Buena (Payroll).  Support staff from ITC include James Sy (GL, AR, Payroll), Weng Agustin (GL, Payroll), Harold Bautista (AP), Ampel Balanza (AR), Rhaniel Bingcang (GL), and Vivian Sy (AP, AR, Payroll).  Oracle counterparts include Mayet Mendoza (outgoing Project Manager), Tito del Rosario (incoming Project Manager), Grace Ogena (GL, AR, AP consultant), and Mike Hallare (Payroll consultant).

Vivian C. Sy  10/13/2000

 

SMO Assists CFO in Customizing Clientele Help Desk Software

Clientele Help Desk version 7.0 is finally up. As the name implies, this software is primarily for the use of CFO’s help desk support staff. Some of the more notable features of Clientele are:
  • Forms 
    The manual filling out of forms for the following services will be virtually eliminated:
      • Service maintenance request
      • Equipment Acknowledgement
      • Service equipment request
  • Networked Database
    • A central database temporarily installed in the CFO application server will allow Daisy, Dette, and Jean to concurrently accept and enter caller data. This means that all calls handled by the helpdesk team are recorded and stored in memory.
  • Knowledge Base
    • Very soon, Clientele will also be able to answer simple and frequently-encountered problems via a “smart” knowledge base. This “expert system” of sorts will allow our helpdesk staff to provide instant solutions to common problems. 
  • Asset Management Modules and Reports  
    A module that will keep an electronic inventory of all hardware and software in campus is in the works. Before long, all service maintenance requests will have as a standard print the hardware specifications of the person or office calling for service.      

SMO personnel who helped prepare Clientele for CFO use include Weng, Lorna, Vivian, and Brian.

SRM-Actual Count

Last adjustment period for 1st Term SY 2000-2001, a web-based Actual Count program was made available for use by the students who were adjusting at the North Wing of the Conservatory.  However, the program was not as popular as during the adjustment period for 2nd Term SY 2000-2001, when the program was made available to the College Assistants on their assigned terminals, and then to the students on other available terminals.

The web-based Actual Count program is part of the Student Records Management System. Its purpose is to allow users to check, real-time, whether a section is already closed for a particular subject, or an open section with less than 5 slots left, or an open section with 5 or more slots. The subject and the section are colored red if this is already a Closed Section, blue if it is an Open Section with 5 slots left, and green if it is an Open Section with more than 5 slots. A section is considered closed if it has already 40 students enrolled in it.

To access SRM-Actual Count, just go to URL http://enrolla.dlsu.edu.ph:81/dlsu/actual_count, then start searching for a subject/section.

Rhaniel C. Bingcang  10/12/2000

 

EVA of ITC

Evaluation System, otherwise known as EVA, was designed as the integration of existing faculty evaluation systems -- Faculty Evaluation System (FES) of DLSU-Main campus and the Student Instructional Report (SIR) of the College of Saint Benilde. The objective of the system is to maintain a systematic operational procedure for processing evaluation data of faculty, laboratory, coaches, athletes, counselors, etc.; and to provide adequate reports to management to support its decision-making processes regarding its resources. EVA is structured in a generic way so that it will be easily adaptable to the needs of other system schools which might want to use the system.

The development of EVA was assigned to the SITE students namely Grace, Don, Paul and Irvin during their practicum here at Information Technology Center, particularly Systems Management Office (ITC-SMO). Though they were not able to finish the system, they completed a considerable number of modules. They turned-over the system to SMO and the continuation of the development of system was passed on to the EVA Project Leader, Harold, and to the EVA System Monitor, Lorna. After several months of revisions on the programs by the SITE students, as well as development of unfinished modules, EVA is finally completed, ready for testing by the PL. Hopefully this term, EVA will be implemented and run parallel to the existing FES and SIR systems.

Way to go, EVA!

Lorna P. Acorda  10/12/2000
