    ITC Personnel joins Hobby Clubs
 

A number of ITC personnel have joined in activities conducted by the Institutional Faculty Development (IFD) through the hobby clubs.  The concluded and ongoing club activities include:

  • Arts and Crafts Club:         
    Souvenir and Give-Aways
    Christmas Decor
  • Culinary Arts Club:          
    Chinese Dimsum Cooking
    Cheesecake and Ham Making
  • Sports Club:            
    Swimming
    Ballroom Dancing

Those who joined the swimming were interested in learning or reviewing the rudiments of the sports and keeping fit at the same time.  I heard they were so satisfied that even though the last lessons were long over, they still continue wading in the gym’s pool.  Those who joined Ballroom Dancing are currently enjoying the rhythm, the steps and their partners' company (?) 

Those who attended the culinary arts club activities were those who like ‘cooking and eating’.  They are now excitedly trying out the new learnings in their own kitchens (a lot of ITC personnel are willing to do some taste tests!).  Meanwhile, those who attended the crafts club activities were the artistic type.  Probably, their hands are now busy making Christmas decors (samples, please!).

Gone are the days when IT people are only seen in the confines of their cubicles, in the computer/terminal rooms, or in front of their monitors.  Like in ITC, slowly the people are realizing that rest and recreation are equally important as food is to the body.  Rest and recreation recharge us and keep our energies high to face more work and challenges ahead.  I hope we could continue with this attitude despite our increasing workload and to pursue ITC’s Mission-Vision of spearheading the pervasive application of information technology not only with excellent service and innovative leadership but also with a holistic human resource development.

 

 

Celine Rivera, 10/18/2000
