    Viewpoint
 

Who's that girl?

The DLSU web development team has spent three gruesome months in rehashing the DLSU website.  It started from an exhausting brainstorming on how the site can be presented to surfers considering their various interests. After the brainstorming, the layout was designed, information gathered, and then the transfer started.  Transfer did not go uncomplicated since script hitches and content scrapes arise one after the other.  Fume even steamed-up inevitably among the team members as they feel the pressure of the deadline and the stress of darning numerous slip-ups.  

After the uphill struggle with time and transformation complexities, the Web Development Team, headed by Ms. Celine Rivera, finally blurted out a big sigh of relief and proudly presented the new face of DLSU on the web.  But no 24-hours had passed when the mailboxes of the team was filled to the brim.  Congratulations, violent reactions, suggestions, corrections and whatever "-tions" have you, paraded in the e-mails of the Docu staff.  What the team thought as conclusion of the project proved to be only a halfway mark down the finish line.  And to make things a little 'stiff' for the team, reactions do not go in unison (well, of course it's natural).  Others were very much delighted with the controversial splash page, others were not so much pleased.  Truly, after having the new DLSU site on-line, another series of meetings, discussions, and editings crammed the schedules of the Documentation Office... until today.  

On the contrary, this article was not written to gripe and express disappointment, in fact, the Team wanted to acknowledge those who gave their feedbacks since it is only through these will we know that we are able to consider the varied interests of surfers, check on the flaws, and reconsider other matters to really make the site a reflection of a world-class university that is DLSU.

Oh!  About the title?  Well, it is the number one question that flooded the office when the new DLSU site went on-line... the graduating girl on the Splash page.  If you were not able to take a look at her, well, here she is again.  FYI, she has already been replaced-by a true-blue Lasallian.  But, whoever she is, only Von knows!
