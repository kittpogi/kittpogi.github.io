New Multimedia Setup for Computer Lab

Recently, two classrooms, one computer lab under the IT Center, and another belonging to Behavioral Science Department, had one interesting new getup. The multimedia projectors of these rooms had been uniquely set up on the ceiling.

Placing the projectors above gives it a more space-saving, convenient and not to mention secured, perspective. This also gives the faculty enough space to work around the room; and the students, a much clearer picture, since they don’t have to worry about not seeing the projection especially if the speaker blocks the projector.

With the successful implementation of the project, not only will the other Computer Labs have this kind of setup but also the Conference Rooms within the campus, and it is all just a matter of time. So to all who took part in this project, (you all know who you are) keep up the good work! 

 

New Cellphone Regulation



In the light of several queries presented to the Computer Lab Operations by some concerned students on the use of cell phones inside the Computer Labs, it was agreed that a revised policy be implemented. 

Below is the revised policy on the use of cell phones:

  1. Cell phones should be set to silent mode upon entering the lab.
  2. No cell phone on top of tables. It is better to keep them inside your pockets or keep the units under the table. Doing so will also minimize theft of the said units.
  3. Voice calls will be done outside. All incoming or outgoing voice calls should be entertained outside the Computer Lab Rooms to avoid disturbing the other users.

*However, the following only applies during free slot period. It is understood that if the lab is used as a classroom or when there are classes going on, similar to non-computer classrooms, it is mandatory to turn-off all cell phones.

Sanctions:

Pursuant to Section 13.3.2.2 and Section 13.3.2.14 of the Student Handbook, any offense or violation of the above mentioned items will be awarded a Minor Offense.  Likewise, any succeeding offense of the same nature will be dealt with in accordance with the provisions of the Student Handbook
