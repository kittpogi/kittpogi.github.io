Room 309 Chronicles
by: Precious Anne L. Taliwaga

Drip… drip… drip… drip… Water droplets fall from the ceiling to the CPU…

Drip… drip… drip… Water droplets fall from the ceiling to the keyboard…

Drip… drip… A pool of water settles near an office cubicle…

A lady slowly moves around the room. Carefully, she goes from one point to another looking UP to see if water falls from just one area, and DOWN so she will not slip and fall. A quick survey reveals that there are 2 more computer units that are in danger of getting wet.

She calls the attention of the men in the room. They ignore her. She tries to remedy the situation by getting covers for all the units. She assessed that unless these are moved to a safe, dry place, it will get more wet.

Again, she calls the attention of the men in the room. This time, out of curiosity, they look at the objects of her concern. True enough, they agreed that unless the computers and their peripherals are moved, they might cause short circuit. They unhooked all the computers and found a place where they will stay dry.

As they leave the building, a strong wind whistle like it can barely contain its strength. The dark sky pours torrents of rain.

Nope, this is not the opening scene from a mystery/thriller/horror movie. This was the scenario inside the different offices of the IT Center and outside Gokongwei Building.

May 27, Tuesday (1:30 p.m.) - DLSU-Manila announced the suspension of classes and offices as it deemed necessary. Students and employees alike filled the hallways, corridors and entrance/exit points in the different buildings within the campus deciding on braving the storm and going home or letting the rains subside a little.

Most ITPs opted to stay in their workstations. Most ITPs were still staring at their respective monitors. A few others loitered around curiously observing what was keeping the other guys busy.

The SMO/PRIO and DAO/DOCU Rooms were hazard areas, so to speak. What kept us puzzled was, as there is still a floor above us, where did all this water come from?

People in the rooms good-humoredly informed me that this was not the first time it happened. They were even surprised because these were not the ceiling areas that were wet in the previous years.

It was a good thing there was a spare umbrella in the office. As Sir James’ cabinet is locked, we just placed it on top so his computer or his things would have gotten wet. It was also good that there were a number of empty trash cans to collect he drippings.

May 28, Wednesday (7:00 a.m.) - A number of ITPs received text messages from caring officemates who were wise enough to inquire regarding office suspension for the day before preparing for the office. And who said texting is all but a waste of time?

Sally had a different story for the day. Who will ever question her dedication to her work? Since she hails from the City of Malabon (we all know how high the waters rise in this place during heavy rains), she got out of bed early enough to be in the office before 8:00 a.m. (Was it true that Sir Sammy also dropped by the office in his immaculate polo barong that same morning?)

May 29, Wednesday - Waking up to a clear sky brought about the feeling of being in the Twilight Zone. Many of us wanted to go back to Dreamland and stay there until noontime again. A number still tried calling up the trunkline just to check if classes and offices will really resume on that day. In vain, of course.

Walking to my cubicle in my usual manner I suddenly stepped on something wet. I looked down my feet and see practically half of the room flooded with water. I showed it to Sally and she contacted a janitor at once. Soon, Albert arrived. He smilingly asked why I was staying in Joie’s cubicle and found the answer to the question himself. Yup, yup. He stepped on the puddle of water too.

And then Francis arrives. He found that his workstation is the hardest hit with his computers neatly placed in the conference room.

During lunch break, as the ceiling was still dripping wet, a patch of it fell when we were inside the room. It was a good thing nobody got hurt!

In our room alone, one complete computer set - CPU, monitor and keyboard, and one colored printer were collected by the CFO for blow-drying.

The musty stench of soiled wood and dirty water filled the room and persisted for two days. Most of us covered our computer units as we leave for home because we were unsure of the weather at night.

We informed the Physical Facilities Office that they need to send someone over to repair the damages in our room. In addition to the computers, five of our fluorescent lights went out. They told us that people were already going around the whole building to check affected areas.

It has been a week now since we have yet to see the men going around. The joke here is, “Maybe that is why it is not being fixed. Maybe the men were doing just that… “GOING AROUND.” Should we just wait around until the next storm hits us and the whole ceiling collapses?
