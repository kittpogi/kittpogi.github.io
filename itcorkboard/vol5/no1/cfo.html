Computer Facilities and Operations : Updates

Security System for all Computer Laboratories under ITC and ITC Offices
by: Irwin S. Jurilla

To enhance the security of our computer laboratories and ITC offices, a door access system will be implemented by using biometrics technology. Every access to any laboratory or office at any given time will require a finger print(s) and an ID or password authentication for additional security confirmation. Thus, the identity of the person who accessed the laboratory or office is captured by the security system. This will eliminate “inside job” theft theories and will give better security accountability and management.

Only authorized staff/officers can have access to the laboratories or offices. Any unauthorized access to the security system will be denied. All access are logged and recorded in a secured computer system. Only authorized officers can access and modify its security policies.

The installation of this security system is scheduled this month of July. If completed, DLSU may claim, among other universities in the Philippines, to be the first to implement the biometrics technology in its computer laboratories and ITC offices for security purposes. A total of 21 doors are to be installed with the door access system.

Storage Area Network (SAN) Implementation
by: Irwin S. Jurilla

Information has grown in value to become a primary asset of almost all businesses today. The ITC, in its service to make information always available and completely shareable, with consistent fast access, complete protection from loss, highly secure and cost-effective centralized management, will implement the Storage Area Network (SAN) this month of July.

A SAN is a dedicated storage network designed specifically to connect storage, backup devices, and servers by using fibre channels. This will give high bandwidth connectivity among the storage network. By consolidating storage in one location, we benefit from efficiencies of management,
utilization, and reliability.

The storage size of the SAN figures to a capacity of Terabytes of data which shall address our storage requirement for two or more years. The keys to its effective implementation are planning, monitoring/projection of storage requirement, good system administration and hardware support.

To provide continuity of power supply during brownouts or power interruption, the SAN and the mission-critical (vital) servers will be supported by the 12kVA APC Symmetra Uninterruptible Power Supply (UPS). This will guarantee the availability of data at all times during abnormal power
operations.

Yuchengco Room 602 New Computer Laboratory
by: Irwin S. Jurilla

A new computer laboratory has been setup for the College of Education during the last period of the 3rd term. It is installed with 42 brand new Dell Desktop Computers and equipped with computer accessories like headsets and web cameras. An overhead projector will also be installed to provide better presentation of lectures to students. Presently, the laboratory has internet access which can be utilized during classroom instructions.

New Internet Service Provider
by: Irwin S. Jurilla

Last June, ITC acquired two new Internet Service Providers (ISP) to provide internet access services. The ISPs are the Pacific Internet and Meridian Telecoms. These companies are reliable ISPs that have 24-hour service and monitoring supports. Their IT infrastructures are robust of which we are guaranteed 99.99% internet access availability.

For this School Year 2003-04, 7 x E1 Lines were acquired from these two ISPs to serve the main campus and its system schools. This approximately sums up to a bandwidth of 14.28Mbps.

Within the school year, ITC will be upgrading its network infrastructure by employing additional switches and wireless access points, and completing all structured cabling in the campus. Also, additional security solutions like Intrusion Detection System/Intrusion Prevention System (IDS/IPS) and firewall redundancy will be realized within the school year.
