Christine R. Tapican
I just started with my new job last January 5, 2004 – it’s at De La Salle University –Professional School Inc. at Taft Avenue, Manila. It’s 45 minutes travel time from my place.
First week at work was okay, I got to know the staff and my colleagues in Process Review and Improvement Office (PRIO) team. Everybody just came from a long holiday, yet surprisingly, the things I’ll need to start my work seemed prepared. I was pleased when Sally led me to a tidy workstation - a dust-free table, chair and cabinets, and a computer ready for setting up. Effortlessly, I received a set of office supplies from Janet and with that I felt welcomed. It was late afternoon when I got the chance to thank Jojo for preparing and setting up my PC.
Selflessly, Joel offered me a tour to the university offices and facilities, as he imparted to me the community culture and how things work here in DLSU. Moreover, he introduced to me the university websites, which gave me a glimpse not only of the school’s policies but also its people. Everyone I met welcomed me including Sir Sam, in which I am grateful. I am now a month old in Information Technology Center (ITC) with new sets of friends and tasks, and hopes for more happy days to come.

Francis R. Salvador
"Among all the companies I've worked for, La Salle ITC is the best organization I have ever joined. This is in terms of company's well-defined job contract, rules and regulations, systems, and most of all, the rapport I have gained easily with all the staff. Everyone is so warm and accommodating that a newcomer really feels so welcome and inspired to work comfortably with dedication and sincerity."
