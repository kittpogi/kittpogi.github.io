Our Challenge: Taking Control of Intervening Projects

How do we prepare for the future?

Planning sessions are opportunities for us to chart our future – the immediate future (i.e., one school year) and the long-term future (i.e., 10 years). The daily challenge of implementing current projects and solving current problems is accompanied by numerous initiatives to create our future. We simultaneously introduce new things – new technologies, process changes, and innovations. Some of these activities may be seen as unnecessary since many of us are already overloaded or seem to be overloaded. However, we need start to building our future as early as “now”, if not “yesterday”, even in bits and pieces. We also need to be comfortable with change – either expected or unexpected. That’s easier said than done but it is doable. We can do that, right? I know you’ll agree because we need to set a good example to our customers about accepting changes.

How often should we have planning sessions? Each meeting, where we’re involved in, could be seen as a planning session. We discuss matters about “today” and “tomorrow”. Each item contributes to the improvement of bigger plans, the bigger picture, the vision. Perhaps it is in being of aware what we can do about our present and our future that we can see implications of our actions and decisions…and how all these can improve our present and our future…how all these can help us achieve our vision.

2nd-hand computers

Why buy a Benz if you only need a Toyota? It’s the same thing for computers. For typical use, my office PC (Celeron 800MHz) uses less than 40% of the CPU power and around 180 MB of 256 MB memory. My office PC runs Windows XP and about five application programs. If you budget is tight, you might want to opt for a 2nd-hand PC/notebook. Visit www.bidshot.com or www.ebili.com to bid for 2nd hand computers (among other things). Please do this outside your residency hours. Let’s not turn ITC into an auction hall! There are other shops doing selling 2nd-hand stuff. Instead of bidding, you can contact the seller directly and buy the item. The disadvantage of buying 2nd-hand computers is the warranty period – typically three to six months, if not zero. And there’s risk of “bad” hardware. That’s the source of the “discount”.

Graduate studies

It’s a strange feeling being a freshman again. I’m enrolled in the MBA program of the GSB. It’s part of lifelong learning. (Is that a cliché?) It’s also part of going out of my comfort zone. Knowing that we have several MBA graduates and students in ITC, it gives me comfort to think that I have people to ask about class lessons. The sub-group where I’m in (we’re four) was supposed to meet last Saturday for a case study. The agreement prior to that was to be ready with individual papers that the group can consolidate. The meeting ended with people not yet reading the case study (what more finishing the paper!) and meeting next Saturday. I could see those sad stories about MBA group work becoming a reality.

I do hope that all of us have our learning initiatives. This could be in the form of short training, reading books/magazines, or enrolling in graduate school. For those taking graduate studies, please remember to give updates (i.e., your grades) to Daisy so these courses you’ve taken can be credited to you or used in your favor when the Promotion Board convenes.
