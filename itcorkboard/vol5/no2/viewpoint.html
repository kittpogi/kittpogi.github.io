Secure or Not Secure?
by: Agnes Bulay

Personal security nowadays has to be tightened… by each one of us if we want to live longer…

More than a week ago, a mishap occurred to one of our colleagues here in ITC. On a Friday afternoon around 4pm, our officemate Amy and her son, Lambert, were held-up by a couple while walking along the sidewalks of Taft Avenue. Amy and her son were on their way to the North Gate of DLSU for their usual merienda when one of the alleged malefactors, a female pedestrian, pointed a pen, which apparently was a cutter blade, on Amy’s side. The male companion of the female felon obviously, took charge of Lambert. Amy, notwithstanding the fear and threat of the situation especially that it involved the life of her son, screamed for help and shouted at his son to run. But unfortunately, even though a crowd has already started to build up in the area, Amy still suffered stabs on her arm, near the wrist area, while her son too suffered stabs in his arm and bruises at his back. Apparently, Lambert was able to escape and called one of the security guards in the DLSU North gate but to Amy’s dismay, while blood profusely flowed from her arm, the Security Guard disregarded the incident and went back to his post. The reason being that, he could not recognize the wrong doer from the victim. Even when Amy and Lambert reached North Gate and asked for help, it still took a yelling command from a concerned male faculty that the security guards started to assist quickly and go after the criminals. Thank God, the offenders were caught and brought to the police while our officemate and her son were immediately brought to Ospital ng Maynila for proper treatment and medico legal procedures.

On hearing this story, I felt sorry for Amy and her son. I definitely felt very bad about the initial, insensitive actions of the security guard. Whatever happened to his sworn duty to help and protect our students and personnel from the evil elements of our society? Whatever happened to our human instinct to help each other at the sight of the crime but do not instead simply because we are afraid the offenders might go after us? I often hear this from friends and even from my parents, to take extra care before helping out when it comes to actual crime scenes so as not to unnecessarily be involved with it. I understand the basis but what about the cause for unity we always try to speak about to help fight violence in this country? Are we just going to remain bystanders letting the crime go on unsolved or apathetically watch the victims endure the violence?…. Perhaps that is one of the reasons why justice is unreachable in this country. Simply because, we are not brave enough to take a selfless stand, to unite and fight the evil in our society. Are we just hoping against hope that someday, someone will initiate to make a stand and come to our aid during the perilous seconds of our life? Will it always be the cliché “my safety first before others”? Then I guess there’s no reason for us to be where we are right now as being educated and intuitive human beings. No man is an island? Or so I thought.

I just hope and pray, we would soon realize that all of these crimes are just but a part of a vicious cycle that one day could happen to each one of us if we will not do anything about it. Yes, it could happen to you, to me and to our children.
