Computer Facilities and Operations : Updates

Upgrade of Computer Lab G302 and G306
The PC desktop units at G302 and G306 have been upgraded to branded units with Intel Pentium 4 2.0GHz processors having a memory of 256MB and 40GB hard disk. These units have dual boot feature. When the classroom instruction requires a Windows environment, the user can boot to Windows 2000 and Red Hat Linux for Unix environment. This dual-boot feature gives the teacher and student more resources and flexibility.

The total number of upgraded units is 112. With better hardware reliability and support services, better service is extended to the users.

Internet Link for DLSU-Araneta and Canlubang
De La Salle Araneta and Canlubang are new system schools that were provided with Internet links. These System schools are connected to ITC for their Internet requirements. De La Salle Araneta is allocated with 256kbps of Internet bandwidth while 126kbps is assigned for De La Salle Canlubang. ITC provides Internet service to all De La Salle system schools.

In-House Maintenance Support Services
Last May, the Service Maintenance Contract of ITC for the repair and maintenance of its computer desktops and peripherals has ended. These services will now be provided directly by ITC to give better support services to the academic community. With the new setup, PC/Engineers were hired to augment the manpower requirement to deliver the said services. New internal procedures were adopted to efficiently address requirements and deliver support services to the academic community.

New Hardware Solutions
Under the supervision of the ITC Director, CFO has identified projects to improve its IT infrastructure and network services. Implementation of which will secure IT resources and give better services. CFO scheduled or planned the following projects to be implemented on the second term:

1. Additional Branded Servers - New servers will be deployed as redundant units to existing servers. This will increase server availability thus providing efficient and continued service.

2. Upgrade of Wireless Network - For the enhancement of the present wireless network, additional Wireless Access Protocol (WAP) devices will be strategically installed in the university campus. This will give ease and flexibility to mobile users in connecting to the campus network. A total of 50 additional WAP devices will be deployed.

3. Upgrade of Network Switches - To address the growing number of users in the campus, new network switches will be installed. Other existing switches will be relocated to identified locations that
require additional network ports. Increasing the network ports will give users more access to the network and IT services.

4. Bandwidth Manager - Implementing a bandwidth manager will aid and provide better management of our internet services. With this setup, we will be able to allocate, prioritize or restrict the kind of internet services and bring forth efficient internet usage.

5. Ultrium2 LTO Tape Library - With ITC’s growing and mission critical data, a tape library will efficiently manage the backup system requirements. This will complement the existing SAN using the
implemented Veritas backup software. Ultrium2 tape drives provide a native capacity of 200GB (400GB compressed) per cartridge and a native data transfer of around 247GB per hour. A faster and
reliable tape library would address ITC’s backup requirements.
