Action Plan 2003-2004 : Updates
by: Precious Anne L. Taliwaga

“The greatest thing in this world is not so much where we are, but in what direction we are moving. Unless you set big, worthwhile goals, you’ll never move beyond your current abilities.” - Oliver Wendell Holmes

This best describes the idea that motivates the IT Center to come up with the Action Plan year after year.

So ITC is reminded of the goal of the bigger communities to which it belongs, the redefined Mission-Vision of DLSU-Manila and the existing Mission-Vision of the DLS System and the Professional Schools, Inc. are reflected in this year’s edition.

Each department has gone over their individual action plans for 2002-2003 and measured their performance against the specifics they defined and pushed themselves to achieve more goals and function better. There have been no changes in the major sections covered in the Action Plan since the last school year. These sections still remain as the individual department’s mission, vision, quality policy, status report of regular activities for the previous school year, macro-environmental changes, TOWS analysis, tedious tasks, recurring problems, TOWS summary, action plan summary, performance objective, regular activities and the action plan for the school year 2003-2004.

The Computer Facilities and Operations (CFO) Office status report on the regular activities for the school year 2002-2003 shows more than 100% growth in the number of activities they have accomplished in comparison to the previous school year. The report also reflects that the top factor in the economic macro-environmental changes that happened is the increased need for on-demand information. It was beefed up with the addition of increased awareness of out-sourcing.

In school year 2001-2002, the TOWS analysis showed that the top threat was potential security breach posed by the users. This just goes to show how the users have changed in the span of one (1) year. The tedious tasks mentioned in the CFO action plan are as numerous as last year’s but this year, these tasks were paralleled with strengths/weaknesses.
The action plan summary defines the individual objectives of the Help Desk group, the systems administration group and the department head. CFO opted to present it this way to improve clarity as to what is expected from each group. Their tabulated Action Plan for SY 2003-2004 is also more specific with the addition of the groupings of the topics into security, management, server room, software, document, and hardware for the Systems Administration Group, and CFO’s own set of plans for the PC network group.

For the Computer Laboratory Operations (CLO) Group, it is interesting to note that the additional threat to last year’s list is the misdemeanor of lab assistants due to lack of defined policies. It was noted that the presence of the Discipline Office hampers these activities and the conduct of investigation before deciding on issues presented also helped resolve this problem/threat (?).

For the Data Administration Office (DAO), this year’s TOWS analysis shows that DAO has eliminated the lack of available training on data warehousing, data analysis and ad hoc report generation. Added to the list of last school year’s tedious tasks is the need to follow up customers to verify data accuracy several times to strictly implement data controls for consistency. Considering the volume of update requests the DAO staff monitors, this task presents difficulties for the DAO to beat deadlines that involve report generation.

The number of recurring problems has been reduced to two (2) from the eight (8) posted last school year. These are the lack of customer’s adherence to standards and lack of available materials to prepare standards. The maintenance of databases including data, stored procedures and access rights was added to the regular activities. DAO have also identified the projected intervening projects.

The Documentation Office (Docu) has also defined the office’s projected intervening projects. They no longer see customer manual documentation as a tedious task. Recurring problems have been traced to lack of measurement to determine effectiveness of web design before launching, lack of customer documentations, and inability to predict the best timing for customer manual documentation.

Weaknesses have been reduced to more than half its previous number, from 25 to 10. Eliminated were lack of software to facilitate on-line web site creation/uploading for customers, lack of feedback on effectiveness of documentations, lack of policies on web hosting and access, and lack of skill to use/maximize software utilization. Docu also defined its intervening projects.

In its TOWS analysis, PRIO added the transition of process owners to last year’s threats, which was continuously reduced through skill in project management and flexibility. Tedious task is down to one (1) from last year’s three (3). Eliminated were transcribing interviews and minutes of meetings, gathering of materials and other information to be used for training modules and projects.

Items in the summary of action plans is grouped into strategy, structure, systems, style, skills, staff and shared values. An almost whole new set of performance objectives was documented with the performance of system study being given topmost priority.

Their current action plan is also presented in a more detailed manner with the conduct of system investigation integrated in the management of research for internal and external faculty members, undergraduate and graduate students.

The System Management Office’s (SMO) vision has been enhanced with the integration of University information added. The status report was also divided into the projects tackled by the operational section and the developmental section. These projects were further classified into academic, non-academic, employee systems, financial systems, integrating systems, support systems, ITC systems and external systems. The summary of action plan is grouped into manpower structure, skills, topics, systems, staff, style, and shared values.

The past year’s staff development summary and the projected targets are also tabulated at the latter part of the document, for the whole IT Center and per department.
