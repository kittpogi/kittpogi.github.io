Have you ever heard about tithing? When was the last time you were reminded of it? Do you practice it?

As a member of the Catholic community, we were taught and we should always be reminded about tithing. Even back in high school, we were already taught about it. But the only time that it started to sink in to me was when I became a member of the Singles For Christ Community. There were a lot of teachings about it more so on how to manage our finances. We are encouraged to practice it in order to help others and fund the projects of our community.

The following is a simple yet an enlightening article I got from the web. Reading it reminded me once more that I am nothing without Him.

 

 

Tithes and Offerings

(A Crash Course)

1) Is there a difference between `giving' and `tithing'?

Yes. Giving, as we will see in our study, is giving to God “as He has prospered us,” as many coin the phrase. Tithing, as we hope to show, is giving God “what is due Him.” If something is “due” or “owed,” it isn't giving. When you send you're payment for the electric bill this month, are you `giving” them your money, or “paying what is due?” You are “paying what is due.”

2) Define `tithing'.

Tithing is, in simple terms “paying 10% to God.” But for those who would prefer a Hebrew (Old Testament) definition, tithe is defined as follows:

1. dekatoo NT:1183, from dekatos, "tenth", in the active voice denotes "to take tithes of," Heb 7:6, RV, "hath taken (KJV, received) tithes"; in the passive, "to pay tithes," 7:9, RV, "hath paid (KJV, 'payed') tithes." In the Sept., Neh 10:37.

2. apodekatoo NT:586 denotes (a) "to tithe" (apo, "from," dekatos, "tenth"), Matt 23:23 (KJV, "pay tithe of"); Luke 11:42>; in Luke 18:12 (where the best texts have the alternative form apodekateuo), "I give tithes"; (b) "to exact tithes" from Heb 7:5.

3. apodekateuo NT:586 v, "to give tithes," in Luke 18:12 (some texts have No. 2). Vine's Expository Dictionary of Biblical Words, Copyright (c)1985, Thomas Nelson Publishers)

3) Why do we need to give our `money' to God? Does He need it?

He doesn't need it. He doesn't need us! But He can use us, and He can use what we give Him to further His Kingdom (i.e. “Bless the gift and the giver”). So why do we give? Yes, it should be for the purpose of “furthering the growth of the Kingdom” (the church,) but we give primarily to further our personal spiritual growth.
In this world of self-proclaimed independence, the church must be constantly aware of our need for dependence, dependence upon God and dependence upon one another.

Giving/Tithing:

  • Reminds us that what we possess really isn't ours
  • Reminds us that the Lord is God
  • Reminds us that God has given to us
  • Causes us to reflect upon God, the church, and our priorities
  • Helps to subdue the temptation to be selfish and covetous
  • Allows us to show our faith and love for the church and the Lord Jesus is good practice
  • Takes our eyes off of self

Denver Sizemore gives these basic “Reasons For Giving.”

A) Advance The Work of the Kingdom

Philippians 4:15-16, “And you yourselves also know, Philippians, that at the first preaching of the gospel, after I departed from Macedonia, no church shared with me in the matter of giving and receiving but you alone; 16 for even in Thessalonica you sent a gift more than once for my needs” (NAS).

2 Corinthians 11:9, “and when I was present with you and was in need, I was not a burden to anyone; for when the brethren came from Macedonia, they fully supplied my need, and in everything I kept myself from being a burden to you, and will continue to do so” (NAS).
When we write our check(s), do we reflect upon the growth and work of the Kingdom?

B) To Develop Spirituality In The Christian

2 Corinthians 9:11, “you will be enriched in everything for all liberality, which through us is producing thanksgiving to God” (NAS).

2 Corinthians 9:7, “Let each one do just as he has purposed in his heart; not grudgingly or under compulsion; for God loves a cheerful giver” (NAS).

Luke 16:10-12, “He who is faithful in a very little thing is faithful also in much; and he who is unrighteous in a very little thing is unrighteous also in much. 11 "If therefore you have not been faithful in the use of unrighteous mammon, who will entrust the true riches to you? 12 "And if you have not been faithful in the use of that which is another's, who will give you that which is your own?” (NAS).

We develop a Christ-like attitude in giving. Remember Matthew 6:21, “For where your treasure is, there will your heart be also” (NAS)? If we are not faithful in the stewardship of the material wealth that we have received how can we expect the TRUE riches of God to become ours? We develop a “right” attitude toward money. Recall the rich young ruler (Mark 10)?

Prayer Of Generosity
Saint Ignatius of Loyola (1491-1556)
Dearest Lord, teach me to be generous.
Teach me to serve You as You deserve;
to give and not to count the cost;
to fight and not to heed the wounds;
to toil and not to seek for rest;
to labor and not to ask for reward, save that of
knowing that I am doing Your will. Amen
