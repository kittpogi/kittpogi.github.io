Conquering Mt. Pulag: A Hesitant Climber’s Account: Day 3


Day 3, Aug 23, 2:00 am

I was rudely awakened by the cold. Now, when we laid down to sleep, we decided to put the earth pad first and spread the sleeping bags flat so that everyone can at least have something soft to lie on. But, now, I was sleeping on the floor. Apparently, the person sleeping beside me (you know who you are, J) pushed me clear of the sleeping bag.


Day 3, Aug 23, 6:00 am

To say that I didn’t get any sleep would be an understatement. I never knew that you could be so tired that all you wanted to do was sleep and yet not be able to sleep at all. Well, it was so cold that this was how almost all of us felt.

Thankfully, the warm breakfast helped energize us a little and gave us something to be cheerful about. Still, we couldn’t leave because typhoon Niña was raging outside.

We were all disappointed with the realization that we couldn’t risk continuing with the planned itinerary given the present weather condition. We all felt that we could have managed the somewhat short climb to the summit with relative ease. Who could blame us? Trekking for approximately six to seven hours in a cold, harsh, and wet environment gave us a reason to be smug.


Day 3, Aug 23, 10:00 am

The rain was easing up a little and we were all so eager to get home that we braved the cold and decided to start our journey back to Baguio. As anticipated, the trip down took us half the time when we were on our way up.

We would only realize much later that a mostly downhill climb is a “killer” on the knees. For the meantime, we were just grateful to have made our way back to the DENR office in one piece. After resting for several minutes, it was time to go back to Baguio.


Day 3, Aug 23, 7:00 pm

We were all hungry by the time we reached Baguio. Because we didn’t have the energy to look for another place to eat, we decided to dine at Jollibee. We also decided to stay at Teacher’s Camp for the night.

Some were already making plans to take the last trip to Manila while others were anxious to get to Teacher’s Camp to get a good night’s rest.
