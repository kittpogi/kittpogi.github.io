Conquering Mt. Pulag: A Hesitant Climber’s Account

Day 1, Aug 21, 9:58 pm

We all agreed to meet at the Victory Liner Terminal in Cubao. Harold decided to go ahead of the group in order to make sure that last-minute chance passengers do not claim our reserved seats. There were 20 people in the group. Nine from ITC: Harold, Lorna, Mark, Ryan, Joel, Precious Anne, Francis, Jojo, and myself; Three from AMCI (Ayala Mountaineering Club, Inc., including Johnny) and eight guests. >>>

 

Day 2, Aug 22, 4:15 am

After more than six hours of traveling, we finally arrived at Baguio City. Two more people were joining the group and we all agreed to meet at Chowking in Session Road. Harold also asked the jeeps which were taking us to Ambangeg to meet us there. >>>

 


Day 3, Aug 23, 7:00 pm

We were all hungry by the time we reached Baguio. Because we didn’t have the energy to look for another place to eat, we decided to dine at Jollibee. We also decided to stay at Teacher’s Camp for the night.

Some were already making plans to take the last trip to Manila while others were anxious to get to Teacher’s Camp to get a good night’s rest. >>>


Day 4, Aug. 24, 8:00 am

When I woke up, Pres and her friends have already gone back to Manila. Lorna, Jojo, Ryan, Joel, and Francis were all packing their things for their 10:20 am bus ride back to Manila while Harold, Tetet, and I were making plans for our extended vacation in Baguio. >>>

 

 


Post Script, Sep 26, 10:00 pm
Some of you may be wondering. Was it worth it? Do I regret having joined the group? Was climbing Mt. Pulag one of the hardest experiences of my life? Does Harold really not take a bath every day?

Allow me to answer this way. I have decided that mountain climbing is not for me and that as much as I can help it, I will only climb one mountain in my entire lifetime and that mountain is Mt. Pulag. I have also decided that no matter what happens, I will one day see the sun rise from Mt. Pulag’s peak.
