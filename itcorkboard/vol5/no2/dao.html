ePurse System
By James Sy



The DLSU ePurse system is designed to make use of the existing smart cards that we are currently using as ID cards for DLSU employees and students (ID-102 and above only). The DLSU ePurse system is an electronic method of carrying cash and making payments to eliminate the inconvenience of going to and from the cashier just to pay for some small-value transactions within the premises of the University. Some examples of such transactions are requests for transcript of records, payment for library dues, and use of laser printers (at the computer labs). As of this moment, only two stations have been set up- one at the Accounting Office to accept "deposits", and another at the Registrar's Office to accept electronic payments for transcript requests and other certifications. The library will have its own station soon.

Features of the DLSU ePurse system:
1. Since the ePurse system is only intended for small-value transactions, it only accepts a maximum load of 1,500.00.
2. The ePurse system forms part of the MLS account. In the MLS account, the card owner can change his PIN (personal identification number) and view his statement of account;
3. The card is protected by a default PIN consisting of 6 digits, which is assigned once the first "deposit" has been made. The card owner must log on to his MLS account to change his PIN, before he can use the card. The card will be locked after three wrong PIN entries;
4. Data on the card is encrypted;
5. All transactions can only be carried out with the PIN;
6. The stored value of a lost card will be carried over once the card owner receives his new ID card at the Registrar's Office.

Since its launching last August 8, 2003, two students have already availed of our ePurse system. We hope that the number will begin to rise once the library and the computer labs set up their own stations.


Online Update Request
By: JC Ibay

The online request for New School and Employee Updates is designed to speed up the process of maintaining the School and Employee master file. The online request page will feature the different request transactions that users require, and the fields for the information they need to add or update. Submitted data or information will be automatically forwarded and made available to the Data Administration Office. Users will be informed of the action taken on their request upon receiving their SRF back from the ITC.

Orientation of users on the new process of School and Employee update will be scheduled before the end of the 2nd term of SY 2003-04. After which, the online request system will immediately be implemented.

Introduction to SQL Server Training Conducted
by: Flor Tamonan

Ms. Florinda Tamonan of the Data Administration Office recently conducted a two-day Introduction to SQL Server training. The course covered the new features and enriched capabilities of SQL Server 2000. The 17 participants were composed of Systems Management Office and Data Administration Office staff. It was held at LS-212 last August 5 and 6, 2003 from 1:30 to 5:00 PM.
