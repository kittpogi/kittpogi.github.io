BOC Annual Summer Outing 2003 : Basco, Batanes

Our Batanes Adventure
by: Brian O. Co

Climbing the Banaue Rice Terraces (albeit a little forced), Eating Curacha at Pagudpud, Bumming in Boracay, Island Hopping in Palawan, and Exploring Sagada. Now I can add Being Stranded in Batanes to my list of travel adventures.

Although we often hear people associate stormy weather when someone mentions Batanes in conversation, this is hardly the case when you get there. You may think that I am contradicting myself as I just mentioned being stranded but wait till you hear the whole story.

Batanes is a delightful mixture of the old and the new. Old traditional stone houses with cogon roofs are built alongside newer structures made of cement and concrete. Traditional sari-sari stores are situated beside internet cafes and calling stations. Friendly and hospitable are the town folk who discuss the virtues of simple living one minute and then spout technical jargon such as LANs, satellite phones, and GPS the next.

To say that the Ivatans (the collective term for the people of Batanes) are a gracious bunch would be an understatement. Let’s just say that in Batanes, if you meet somebody on the street who doesn’t nod hello or throw you a smile, then he/she must also be from out of town.

They even have an establishment called the Honesty Café where nobody mans the shop and people who want to drink coffee simply mix themselves a cup and then leave the payment. I suppose that you can even give yourself change in case you only have large bills. I wonder what will happen if they implement a similar set up in Metro Manila. I may be cynical but I hesitate to even think that the roof will still be there the following business day.

Going back to Batanes, did I mention the food? We were treated to a sumptuous assortment of seafood and local delicacies such as flying fish, dorado, Batanes beef, and tatos (coconut crab). Mama Cata, Joel, Harold, Tetet, Boots, Stu, and I enjoyed our stay immensely, thanks to our hosts Lito and Emma - cousins of our ITC colleague, Lloyd, and his girlfriend Bhing.

Before I forget, there is still that matter of being stranded. As luck would have it, typhoon “Amang” decided to show up on the morning of our departure flight from Basco (capital of Batanes). Thus we were “forced” to extend our vacation for a few more days. But a little inconvenience didn’t dampen our spirits and can’t erase the fond memories that we will always have of Batanes. And did I mention the halo-halo? But that’s another story.
