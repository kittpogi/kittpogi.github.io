Excellence Award Results Just Like NBA Playoffs
by: Reynaldo R. Mendoza, Jr.

The recently concluded Excellence Awards was not far off from the NBA playoffs. This year, many teams, I mean candidates, were into clinching the championship, (the Excellence Award). The race for the award was anybody's ballgame as many contenders have emerged proving to everybody that they are all worthy of the Trophy.

The three-peat champion (Rhaniel, LA Lakers) looked like they were ready to be dethroned. The defending champion was not even in the forefront of the race as many were into the lead because of high evaluation results, a modest peer-to-peer survey rating and proverbial winners of the attendance awards got their advantage on sheer excellent attendance records (maybe this attendance award perennial winners are aliens, they never get sick!). Although the peer-to-peer survey consists only of the 10% of total points, it was not to be sneered upon. What was really remarkable was that the third place winner got only one vote! It only proves that anybody could win even if they are not that "popular", just work hard and be consistent in your work.

And as the race continues, the final blow on everyones' bid for the trophy was dependent upon the attendance records as it was the last piece of puzzle that we got to determine the winners.

And indeed, it was a telling blow as the mighty contenders got eliminated from the series as if it was a game seven for all candidates. Some strong candidates got knocked off by their leaves and tardiness. And these were the aspects that propelled the winners to the top as attendance records were determined.

And just like the NBA playoffs, two new candidates emerged as winners; Mary Jean Soria was awarded the second place winner and Francis Mark Santos clinched third place. But unlike the championship results in the NBA, no new champion was to be crowned as the reigning three-peat champion, Rhaniel C. Bingcang, propelled himself four-ward into the Hall of Fame by winning it again.
