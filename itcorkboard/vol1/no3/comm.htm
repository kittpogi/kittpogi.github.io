    Mastering the Art of Communication

Communication is the beginning point of all agreement. It is the "glue" of teamwork and the bridge of human relationship. Whether you are in a committed relationship, leading a management team, running a business, parenting children, or negotiating a deal, your ability to understand, to BE understood and to create harmony, depends on your ability to master some of the basic laws of communication.

KNOW YOUR PERSONAL POWER TO INFLUENCE
Communication itself is your true personal power. Our world is nothing but a mass of earth, air and chemicals - if it were not for human exchange and communication. All of our present day ideas, products, rules and rituals have been created through cooperation and relationship and that stems first from our communication. Someone, somewhere, beginning a thought and COMMUNICATING it with another, CREATES our world. So, to be your most powerful communicator that you are, know and BELIEVE that your communication itself is POWER. (and respect the "power" of others to create this way also) If you want to change ANYTHING in your life, to have more money, to have a great relationship, or to effect positive change around you, your COMMUNICATION can CREATE it. It can bring things into EXISTENCE. Mastering the sending and receiving (the exchange) elements of Communication lets you influence your world where no obstacle can ever block you from what you desire.

LISTEN... AND RECEIVE
Most people's greatest desire is simply...to be heard, to be understood, and to be accepted. Communication is a relay game. Too often, communication blocks happen when BOTH parties maintain a position that each needs to be heard FIRST. When you let go of that, the other person feels an instant increase in trust with you. By listening first before you talk, you are RECEIVING the communication they are sending and that "empties their cup" so that they can hear YOU. This does not mean to simply "let them finish". Rather, it means to clearly UNDERSTAND the other's position. Ask questions of them, acknowledge what is being said to you (don't comment on it but reflect it and acknowledge it) and show them you are getting their point. Remember that communication only happens when it is RECEIVED. Do what you need to do to receive it clearly and fully and you will open up the channel to even more productive exchange.

ACKNOWLEDGE COMMUNICATION
Receiving communication also means "signaling" that you received it as well. Communication... is a response! (if you speak in an empty forest and no one hears you it is NOT communication is it?). So, ACKNOWLEDGE what is being said to you before you respond to it (with your opinion or counter opinion). This can be as simple as nodding your head, uttering "yes", "uh huh" or "I can see what you mean" as the other person makes a point of what they are saying. (Mastering this one skill can make you a master of online chats as well! People rarely acknowledge communication in an online chat) This is also PARTICULARLY TRUE when people are upset. Especially when dealing with an irate customer. Think about the last time you received poor treatment in customer service and all you wanted was someone to listen to your issues. If the irate person is your customer, hear them out and ACKNOWLEDGE them before you defend your company's position - or even before you try to right the wrong. Since there is no greater desire than the desire to be heard (and it is rare that people take the time to really acknowledge what we think), when you do that for others you will gain immediate and tangible results in the form of trust and respect.

CLARIFY AND CONFIRM YOUR COMMUNICATION
In the same thinking, whenever you complete a significant business meeting or whenever you resolve an upset in a personal relationship, be proactive about CONFIRMING whether your communication was RECEIVED. Don't assume just because you said it (or...that you SENT it) that it was understood. ASK the other person if you can review what you both covered in the communication before you leave it. Example: "Okay, just so I understand this, we are going to resolve this (situation) this way, is that right? or..is it that way ?" Example: "So, you're going to pick up the kids at 5:00 and I am going to do the dinner. Is that right?" ( No one can say you didn't say what you did if you take the time to confirm like this) Sure, most times you can speak in shorthand with people you know very Well and you might not find this necessary. But, if you are finding that there are missed signals, and misunderstandings happening, it is usually because both parties are not clarifying their communication beforehand. You will be surprised when you do this how often what we ASSUME people understand is so very far from what they DO understand. Asking to review the communication will prevent upsets in the future as you both clarify what is needed.

IDENTIFY AND CONFRONT YOUR ASSUMPTIONS/BELIEFS
Too often today, in this "enlightened" "politically correct" world we live in, we think it is "bad" to judge or to make assumptions about people, their actions or intentions. But, assumptions are an important part of our daily real-world interactions. Without them, we would never get through the simplest tasks of team work and cooperation. Interpretation is a normal part of communication. The problems with judgements and beliefs comes when we don't recognize them as...just that, beliefs and interpretations. When we try to present them as facts to ourselves or others, we cloud communication. Let's take a simple example. Let's say that you send someone an email asking a question. Let's also assume that they don't answer it promptly and you notice they have seemed to avoid you in other respects as well. It would not be out of the ordinary for you to wonder about that. Now, you could assume, say, that the person is...shy...or that they are...insensitive...or that they, perhaps, just don't take a shine to you for some reason. Why knows? All assumptions are equal here. But if you need to have prompt communication with this person, it needs to be resolved, right? So, confront this by openly expressing your suspicions and ASKING the person if there is such a problem. Now, often a person asked is there a problem will respond "No, there is no problem." It is as automatic as "How are you?" "I am fine", etc. If they do answer that way, simply say (following this example), "Well, I have noticed you haven't been able to answer my communication and that it has happened like this before and I was even thinking ... (SHARE with them whatever you assume) ...am I correct?" This gives them a chance to acknowledge you, the situation, and what is really going on for them. Obviously this is a small and trite example but the principle holds for any kind of assumption belief. It is part of communication and all communication needs to be acknowledged.

KNOW and PRACTICE THE 3 CENTRAL SKILLS of RAPPORT BUILDING
Interview - asking questions with interest Acknowledge - recognition & confirmation of communication you receive Sharing - creating common ground through revealing your own background/story The 3 central skills are essential for online chatting as well as face to face conversation!

LEARN to CONFRONT in ORDER to AVOID CONFRONTATIONS
When there is a problem, confront it. Look up that word in the dictionary and it will show you a very different meaning than most of us assume. When faced with an issue to resolve with someone else, if we don't like confrontations we say, "I don't want a confrontation", etc. However, if you look at the word confront, it means to FACE something. (con-front to be in "front of") When we confront a situation, we actually DIFFUSE it through communication. NOT confronting it actually CAUSES CONFRONTATIONS. They happen because when the issues are real, the "facing of it" is inevitable. Unfortunately when it happens at a stress/breaking point and people lose control, we get a "confrontation and the situation gets much worse. Confront true issues openly and quickly to avoid confrontation.
