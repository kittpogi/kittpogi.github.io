    View Point

ISTC nagdidikta nga ba sa PRESS?

"ISTC Nagdidikta sa Press" - Plaridel.  Hmmm... that's a tough phrase to read.  The September 23, 1999 issue of Plaridel highlighted ISTC's attitude with the press as sort of spoon feeding the press of what to write about the Center.  The paper contempts ISTC's policy of "editing" the article first before it can be published. Well, let's start digging down for the truth in the issue by knowing what both sides have to say:

Plaridel: "...noong buwan ng Hulyo, nagbigay ng isang liham ang Plaridel na humihingi ng panayam sa kanila.  Sinagot nila ito sa pagsasabing kailangang magpadala ang Plaridel ng isang liham na nagsasaad na kailangang dumaan muna ang mga artikulo sa ISTC at mailalathala lamang kapag pumayag sila.  Kailangang nilagdaan ang liham ng punong patnugot ng Plaridel, direktor ng Student Publications Office, at dekana ng SPS ( o Student Affairs)..."

ISTC: ISTC Director explained the side of the Center in his e-mail addressed to the SPS Dean by saying that it is not the intention, inclination or plan of ISTC to dictate the press, the intention is to make sure that the truth is published.  The Director also stated that he does not mind if students publish negative aspects of ISTC's operations or services as long as it is the truth, since it is also one way of improving the performance of the Center.

He also said that the editing of the article was not intended to cover up the truth but plainly to review and correct the wrong.  This policy was formulated since there were a number of articles published in the past with wrong information about ISTC without any erratum made to correct the wrong information published.

As the Paper had put it, "mayroong right to information ang bawat Lasalyano," but ISTC just wanted to make sure that every information given to the Lasallians is the correct information.  Maybe it is just a matter of conveying not just the correct information but also the correct tone.

 

Joie Alvarez 10/18/1999
