Ruts

Have you ever fallen into a rut, the result of being a slave to certain daily routines that seem to have lost their luster? In my life, I found there can be a fine line between being faithful and dependable in the daily routine one abides by as wife and parent and being a prisoner, of sorts, to what one perceives as obligation. For example, as a young bride and new mother, I used to love to cook for my family. I took great pride knowing that after a hard day at work I could provide my husband with some well-deserved relaxation and pleasure merely by having a hot meal awaiting him. The whole routine set the mood for a pleasant evening with our children, my husband often reaching for his guitar as our toddlers pranced around the room enjoying the lively music and me attempting to sing.

Many years have passed and our babies are now teens. I have come to discover that not only my self, but our whole family has fallen into a rut. Meals are still prepared, but not with the great care as before. Pleasant dinner talk has progressed into squabbles with our children, their dad and I acting as referees. Too busy with work, my husband's guitar now sits in the closet gathering dust and, once supper has concluded, we all tend to wander off and do our own thing.

It is hard to pinpoint how or why this happened, but that really isn't significant right now. What IS important is that we all saw the problem, addressed it, and are making strong efforts to improve it.  Perhaps it is now easy to see how a minor problem can grow rapidly if unattended. The same holds true with our relationship with Christ. Minor diversions have the ability to push one further away from righteousness and, before too long, we may find ourselves deeply lost and embedded in a rut of sin. Being a prisoner of sin, one often feels there is no way out from such a pit. But have hope, there is! Your heavenly Father has not left your side, but has been waiting for you to reach up and call out to Him! Through the helping hand of God and His Son, Jesus Christ, we can be more than conquerors in all things!

Don't get stuck in a rut. Call on Christ to help you out.

 

From: Daily Wisdom, 17 April 1999
