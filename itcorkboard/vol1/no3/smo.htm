    SMO Updates

 

Identification Access System

IDA or Identification Access System is the software being developed by SMO’s Rowena Santos for use in the validation, updating, and usage reporting of identification cards (IDs) in campus. The use of bar-coded ID cards (which will be issued to all university personnel and students) and barcode readers (strategically placed in all entry points) will help curb the incidence of trespassing and will greatly increase our campus’ security level. This system is being developed in coordination with the Security, Registrar’s, and Accounting offices. The system is scheduled for implementation this 3rd term of SY 1999-2000.

 

 

Student Records Management Systems Migration

Because our system back-end has changed from DEC VAX RDB to Oracle, SMO is currently integrating all existing student records management systems (undergraduate, graduate, College of Saint Benilde) into one integrated SRM system (ISRM). This involves modifying existing RDB-COBOL programs into PRO-COBOL programs. As of last count, 39 out of 207 programs have been completed by the FIT (Fearless ISRM Team) composed of Arfeli "Wonder Woman" Balanza, Rhaniel "Wonder Boy" Bingcang, Brian "BBB" Co and Loveleah "Super Girl" Malig.

 

 

Frontline Service Management Seminar

Lichelle Legutan of SMO attended a seminar at the Bro. Connon Conference Room last August 25 on Frontline Service Management. The seminar focused on how to deal with different kinds of customers, specially the difficult ones. Lichelle shared that the key traits which a frontline service staff member should possess are patience, expertise in field of work, friendliness, neatness and being well-groomed, flexibility, approachability, and being always informed and up-to-date.

 

 

Data Flow Diagramming Guidelines

SMO has finished writing its guidelines or procedures on Data Flow Diagramming with the help of Ms. Celine Rivera of DAO. These guidelines will help standardize all DFDs produced by the office. Eventually, SMO hopes to share these rules with the rest of ISTC in order to further standardize outputs.

Data Flow Diagrams (DFDs) are diagrams used to describe high-level system design. They show how data is transformed as it moves from one system component to another. The primary use of DFDs is that it presents a situation from the viewpoint of the data, instead of from the viewpoint of any person or organization. The advantage of this approach is that the data sees the big picture, while the various people and machines and organizations that work on the data see only a portion of what happens.
