Prototyping

The Systems Management Office of ISTC has decided to incorporate the prototyping methodology in its software development lifecycle. The group of Harold Paulo Bautista, Brian Co and Lichelle Legutan of SMO conducted a presentation on prototyping last January 27. Different kinds of prototypes, their corresponding advantages, disadvantages, quality, and costs were discussed.

A prototype by common definition is an easily modifiable and extensible working model of a proposed system; however, it does not necessarily represent the whole system completely. The main benefit of using a prototype is that it serves as a communication tool between users and developers during the software design stage. This method of emphasizing the key parts of the system helps reduce the risks of incomplete and erroneous requirements definition. This is because the users are given a chance to experience specific examples of the system’s behavior, thereby helping them articulate their actual needs to the developer.

After the presentation, basic guidelines on prototyping were discussed. These guidelines were used by Catalina M. Quiogue in making the prototype for the Faculty Attendance Monitoring (FAM) System. The pilot prototype was presented to the users last February 10, 1999, and the actual system will be ready for their use come June 1999.

Lichelle B. Legutan 02/23/1999
