MSCL Now Under ISTC
A new computer laboratory has just been added to ISTC’s responsibility checklist. This time it is the computer lab located at the 3rd floor Room 316 of STRC known as MSCL. The Math Department formerly handled the lab but by December last year, the management of the lab was transferred to ISTC.

This computer lab is essentially for Math Majors and for faculty members who are involved with research work. However, since the lab provides other software commonly used by other colleges, the facility will now be shared with other students belonging to other colleges. Among the various software utilized or installed there are: Statistica, Mathematica, SAS, Visual C++ and several others. Moreover, the 36 computer units have been upgraded to Celeron 300A early February, making the lab one of the most advanced computer labs under ISTC. The lab also boasts of its several multimedia equipped PCs and other services like scanning and laser printing. Glen Bertulfo 02/19/1999

Proposed Prepaid Printing Scheme for Computer Laboratories

A new payment scheme for paid printing was proposed last January 6, 1999. It was during the first official Central Committee Meeting when a unanimous decision was reached to change the current process.

The proposed plan, whose main goal is to lessen problems related to payments for printing and other forms of Computer Lab services, was adopted from a system used by Simon Frasier University in Canada. The idea came from one of the Central Committee Members who went there a few months ago.

The process, which simulates a form of advance payment, allows the user to print as many print jobs equivalent to the amount he/she paid. The user simply gets a payment slip from any of the Computer Labs and pays a minimum amount (P25.00 = 20 pages) or whichever amount the SA foresees the user might consume. They are to bring the receipts to the lab where they are going to print and then have it duly signed by the SA-on-duty to certify that he/she had already printed a number of pages. If in case, the user needs to print more pages, he/she simply follows the same process.

One clear advantage of using this system is that, it eliminates chances of delinquency on the users part. They have to present the receipt first, otherwise, they would not be allowed to print. Another is, during the last shift, that is, from 6:00-9:00pm during weekdays and 1:00pm onwards on Saturdays, only those with receipts will be allowed to print. This is one way of instilling discipline to our customers. They have to foresee that they need to print and so, they have to pay in advance to be able to avail of the printing facilities inside the labs.

Sufficient information will be provided for the computer lab users before we implement this scheme. Information regarding the availability of the Accounting Office for receiving payments as well as the whole process itself, will be posted in every lab. We expect that in the near future, students and faculty alike will learn to appreciate the importance of this scheme. It is, primarily, for their convenience.

Glen Bertulfo 02/19/1999
