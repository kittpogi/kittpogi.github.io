    View Point


Online Query: A Bane or a Boon?

It’s so funny how people react when you give them a certain privilege, before you know it, they’re already claiming it as an inherent right doubled with some insatiable tendencies, making things more complicated. A typical case of offering them a hand and they grab your whole arm. More or less, that was the picture when the Online Query for qualified freshmen was put up in the Internet last January 5, 1999. It was actually intended as an extended service to those who took the Freshman Entrance Exam, especially to those who are already too jittery for the results. This query intends to deliver them the results the earliest possible time. To the chagrin of the Web Development Team and the other people behind this project, these were the things they had to say:

  • "I would just like to inquire if the freshmen applicant’s list on the Internet is official, and do you take out applicants from the list? Why? [snipped] I was on that list two weeks ago and now, it’s not posted anymore [snipped] "

  • "[snipped] I had quite a hard time looking for my fellow schoolmates’ results…can you send me a copy to this e-mail address?"

  • "Kung hindi niyo rin papakita o whatever ang results ng incoming freshmen, tanggalin niyo na lang yung sign sa webpage para hindi mag-expect ang taong tumingin." (when the e-mail source had a hard time accessing the web page).

  • "I’d like to ask if the Internet version of the DLSU qualified freshmen list is a complete version. [snipped] "

  • "I would like to know if the following freshmen applicants pass the entrance exams. These are the following names: [snipped]"

  • "[snipped] There are also some questions that my classmates would want to be answered. I hope you can help us. They just can’t understand some things they see in the web. What does my name’s appearing after typing my name imply? Does it mean I’m qualified?"

  • "I’m having a hard time accessing the on-line search for freshmen results. Could you please e-mail the file to this address [snipped]?"

  • "I am a bright student. I do not know why I cannot find my name on your list."

  • "Is it possible for you to provide a search by school?"

  • "Hey it’s really funny, we input the data for the search of the new freshmen for 1999-2000 but we were informed that a letter will be sent to the applicants on the 3rd week of January, what then is the purpose of the Online list of Freshmen??"

Diverse reactions and tough demands? Well, you cannot really please everybody, but these sometimes-annoying inquiries can be a useful input to improve next year’s online query. ‘Til next year.

Joie B. Alvarez 2/15/99
