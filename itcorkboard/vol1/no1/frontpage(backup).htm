A Decade and a Half…
(A History)

In the 1970's, the La Salle Computer Center (LSCC) was created. BASIC computer programming was taught to engineering students then.

In the 1980's, it became the Planning Information and Computer Science Center (PICSC). DLSU introduced the first Bachelor of Science Degree in Computer Science program (SY 1981-1982) in the Philippines. Four years later, PICSC cleaved into two units - the Computer Services Center (CSC) and the College of Computer Studies.

CSC was formally inaugurated as a separate unit 15 years ago on January 7, 1984. The Center was dedicated in honor of a philanthropist gentleman, Mr. Cristino Concepcion, Sr. whose marker is at the ISTC (G308). Mr. Concepcion, Sr. and his family helped, then De La Salle College acquire its very first computer system, the PDP 8/I, with a substantial donation from his family and Stanford Microsystems, Inc.

CSC, stationed at the St. Miguel Building (formerly St. Benilde Building), worked the decade in enhancing the Student Records Management information systems for the undergraduate students and other administrative information systems. The succeeding years of the 1980's saw the interphasing of microcomputer-based systems with minicomputer-based systems, development of a computerized registration system for the Graduate School of Business and Economics (GSBE, formerly GSB); and the campus network with the VAX 8350. Before the end of the decade, many of the University's systems were computerized.

The Millennium Bug
    The Millennium or Year 2000 (Y2K) Bug is generally a software (or program) limitation. It can cause a software error. In computer lingo, this is called a bug. There is a lot of talk about this in the IT (Information Technology) industry. Others are concerned about the ripple effects across the region/globe. The truth is no one really knows all the effects of this bug despite various speculations. However, we can take some steps to minimize, if not totally avoid, its ill effects.

On December 1990, Computer Services Center transferred to Gokongwei Hall (formerly Intellect Building). Taken in tow by the new decade is the Internet. The Internet link began through a dial-up Internet Service Provider in 1991 and a direct access on March 29, 1994 at exactly 10:59 am.

At the end of 1993, INSYST-DLSU (Information System Strategic Plan for DLSU), a technical committee aimed to formulate a comprehensive information systems plan for the University, recommended the creation of three new offices for CSC. These are the Documentation Office (DOCU), Process Review and Improvement Office (PRIO) and Research and Development Office (R&D). This is in addition to the four currently existing offices: Computer Facilities and Operations (CFO), Computer Laboratory Operations (CLO), Data Administration (DAO), and Systems Management (SMO). As a result of its expanding function, CSC has decided to change its name to Information Systems and Technology Center (ISTC) to reflect and emphasize the wider focus it has set to meet the dynamic and increasing Information Technology requirements of the University.

The Center busied this decade with activities focused into utilizing the Internet facility to the full advantage of the University. Among which are: world-class DLSU web site, Library Information System with its On-line Public Access Catalogue (OPAC), Dial-up Access, ATM network, Network Access Ports, Cybernooks, and the Intranet. On the other hand, the ISTC is likewise streamlining processes within the campus; developing integrated information systems; remodeling computer labs; and providing assistance to other La Salle schools.

ISTC has been through a decade and a half, through different names, through several Directors, but still with the same steadfastness in rendering the services it is expected to deliver… even better as it awaits the challenges of the new millennium.

Joie B. Alvarez 02/22/1999
