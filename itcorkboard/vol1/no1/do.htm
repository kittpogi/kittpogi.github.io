What's New In Our DLSU Home Page

Got a glimpse of the new look of the DLSU-Manila home page? It is not the same gray (or blue to other persons) thing that you see… it’s now green! Aesthetically speaking, it is better than the previous versions of the web page because of the new images, which are optimized for faster downloading, and the consistent look and feel of the web pages. Aside from these, it also uses navigation bars for easier site navigation and it projects interactivity with the user by using mouseover scripts on the links. The picture at the center of the page is changed periodically to make the web site look more dynamic.

The main page serves as a two-way entrance into the site, it has a navigation bar at the top, which brings you to the sections of the DLSU web site where more outside visitors are expected to go to, especially to the Academic Programs section. The navigation bar on the left side of the page is different from the one at the top. The links contained here leads to more specific details about the University, which are very informative for visitors inside or outside the University.

In the body of the main page itself, you can enter the DLSU Intranet through a link, provided that you are connected directly to the DLSU local area network. Aside from this, there is also a Headlines Section where you can see the latest happenings inside DLSU. This section is updated weekly and is linked to the online DLSU Newsletter, so you can really be sure that the information you read are up-to-date. The online list of accepted freshmen applicants can also be accessed through the main page, (See related story) which generated a lot of hits when it was uploaded last January 5, 1999.

A new feature in the DLSU home page is the Online Broadcast Section, where the suspension and resumption of classes in DLSU will be posted upon the announcement by the EVP. It was conceptualized as an additional way of disseminating information regarding suspension of classes. Before, you will have to call the University every time the weather is at its worst (where the possibility of your call being attended to is low because of an increased number of callers) or will have to listen to selected radio stations attentively, waiting for an announcement from the University ("Ano?! May pasok ang La Salle?"). Now, you can do it via the Internet.

In a few days, you’ll be seeing more changes in the DLSU home page. Next on the list is the reconstruction of the PINAS page (a link to other Philippine sites), and the DLSU campus vicinity map.
Francis Mark Santos 02/19/1999

Documentation Standards

In line with De La Salle University’s goal for ISO accreditation, the Information Systems and Technology Center (ISTC) formulated standards to be implemented in the documents used by the office.

This standard includes a pre-defined layout for every type of document, standard fonts, and margins and the appropriate use of the DLSU and ISTC logos.

Among the documents to be standardized are the forms, memos and postings released by ISTC. Also included are the documented processes, status reports, feedback forms, and manuals among other things.

This initiative is expected to keep the ball rolling for the whole University in trying to establish standards towards the making of a world-class university.
Francis Mark Santos 02/19/1999

Staff Development

Forms Design

The Docu, PRIO and SMO staff together with the representatives from the DAO and OD attended the Forms Design training conducted by Celine Rivera, Coordinator of DAO/DO last January 29. This training will enable them to make better-designed and more effective forms, which in turn would result in increased user satisfaction. A small exercise/workshop was given to the attendees as part of the training.
Francis Mark Santos 02/19/1999

Using Frontpage 98

The Docu, PRIO and DAO staff attended a report by Francis Mark D. Santos of Docu Office about the basic and advanced features of FrontPage 98 last January 27. FrontPage 98 is the software used by the Docu Office in maintaining the different home pages of DLSU (DLS System, DLSU-Manila and DLSU Intranet). A short hands-on exercise was given to a representative from PRIO as a culminating activity and to measure how much the attendees learned in general.
Francis Mark Santos 02/19/1999
