Reflections on Quality

"Tough" doesn’t mean beating up on people. It means sticking to the necessary policies and actions no matter how enticing the reasons for easing up.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

We all recognize that most of our problems are caused by management action, or lack of it.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Whenever management does something successful, or sorts out a complex situation, it too quickly forgets the whole thing and goes on to something else.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

A rule to live by: I won’t use anything I can’t explain in five minutes.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

When you decide to change a culture, you had better be sure you know exactly what you are going to change – and why.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

The problem is usually in the process.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Quality is a choice that management can make.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Communication, Example, and Participation are the three legs of the platform for Pride.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Many people do not need much success to take something off the problem list.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

It will take forever; but then, that’s just how long we have.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Not everyone recognizes change when he or she sees it.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Quality has to be caused, not controlled.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Executives determine what is going to be run; managers do the running.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

A manager’s ability to get the impossible done just means that even more impossible tasks will soon be on the way.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

It is not possible to fool people for very long. Once in a while they are wrong in judging those who are put over them, but they catch on very quickly.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

If anything is certain, it is that change is certain. The world we are planning for today will not exist in this form tomorrow.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

The business desert is layered with the bones of those who felt they understood completely, and stopped learning.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Making a wrong decision is understandable. Refusing to search continually for learning is not.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

The best reputation a leader can possess is for displaying ethical conduct in all things.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Subordinates establish their personal determination level based on what they see in their leader. Wimpy behavior produces wimpy results.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Enthusiasm is the result of an energetic person working on something that he or she finds keenly interesting. This means that a leader should not sign on to any project that does not give him or her a tingle deep down.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Nothing turns an organization ineffective so quickly as a continuing anxiety about what is going to please or displease the boss.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Running a corporation is too important a task to be left to the functional departments.

Philip B. Crosby

Relationships are where it all comes together or come apart. Nothing else can be made to happen if relationships do not exist.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Wars very rarely break out among those who have a common understanding of goals and purposes.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Quality is the result of a carefully constructed cultural environment. It has to be the fabric of the organization, not part of the fabric.

Philip B. Crosby
