Reflections on Quality

The cost of quality is the expense of doing things wrong.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

Quality is ballet, not hockey.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

The problem of quality management is not what people don’t know about it. It’s what they think they do know.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

The world’s business, whether a steel mill or a hotel, is conducted by people.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

Quality is hard to pin down, because each person thinks everyone else defines it the same way he or she does.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

To manage quality, you must define it as conformance to requirements.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

To have a meaningful discussion on such a complicated subject as quality, some erroneous assumptions must be examined and altered.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

It is erroneous to believe that quality means goodness, or luxury, or shininess, or weight.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

It is erroneous to think that quality is intangible, and therefore unmeasurable. It is precisely measurable by the oldest and most respected of means -- cold, hard cash.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

It is erroneous to think that there is an "economics" of quality.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

It is erroneous to think that all quality problems originate with the workers, particularly those in the manufacturing sector.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

It is erroneous to assume that quality comes from the "quality department."

 

Philip B. Crosby

A person in the "manufacturing ghetto" can’t contribute much to the prevention of problems, if all the planning and creation is done elsewhere.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

Once integrity has been compromised, it can never return to its original pristine state.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

The greater the distance between administrator and what’s being administered, the less efficient the administration becomes.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

 

If it’s going to be practical and achievable, quality management must start at the top.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Prevention is harder to sell than it is to do.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Worker performance corresponds to the attitude of management.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Most of us live by a dual standard: one for our personal lives and one for our work.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

What part of "Zero Defects" do you not understand?

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

People really don’t work for money.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

 

Motivation’s effect is only short-term.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

If you show no mercy for the people who supply your needs, why expect your customers to treat you differently?

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Quality has to be managed because nothing is simple anymore.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

One of the useless bits of management folklore: "If you have good in your heart, you will produce quality."

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

If you don’t produce one dead dragon each week, your license may be revoked.

 

Philip B. Crosby
