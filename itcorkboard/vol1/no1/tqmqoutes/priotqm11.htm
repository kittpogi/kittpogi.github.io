Reflections on Quality

It’s hard to snuggle up to numbers.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

The entire education process can be summarized in what I call "the six C’s": Comprehension, Commitment, Competence, Communication, Correction, and Continuance.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

 

Comprehension is the understanding of what is necessary, and the abandonment of the "conventional wisdom" way of thinking.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

 

Commitment is the expression of dedication, on the part of management first and of everyone else soon after.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

 

Competence is the implementation of the improvement process in a methodical way.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

 

Communication is the complete understanding and support of all people in the corporate society, including suppliers and customers.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

 

Correction is the eliminating of opportunities for error by identifying current problems and tracking them back to their basic cause.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

 

Continuance is the unyielding remembrance of how things used to be and the glorious vision of how they are going to be.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

 

Anything that is caused can be prevented.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

The outgoing new products or services normally contain deviations from the published, announced, or agreed-upon requirements.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

 

The companies that don’t see much improvement, even though they appear to be determined, have common characteristics:

  • The effort is called a program rather than a process.
  • All effort is aimed at the lower levels of the organization.
  • The quality-control people are cynical.
  • Training material is created by the training function.
  • Management is impatient for results.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

It has been known for generations that organizations that do what they promise to do, and take good care of their employees in the process, always come out on top.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

There is absolutely nothing more demotivating or demeaning to a budding executive than to have to go to meetings where the assigned role is to be a faithful listener.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Dissatisfaction with the final product or service of an organization is called "trouble with quality."

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

The typical management decision that is the result of a "hunch" will cause quality problems.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Having a large book of policies and practices never saved any company from disaster.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

The ecology of an organization is as delicate and vulnerable as that of a forest.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

The performance review, no matter how well the format has been designed, is a one-way street.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Staff meetings are the same everywhere. The boss talks about what he wants to talk about for as long as he wants to talk about it, and then the meeting is over.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

I never have felt that you could "motivate" anyone for more than a few days.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

The first absolute: The definition of quality is conformance to requirements.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

The second absolute: The system of quality is prevention.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

The third absolute: Performance standard is Zero Defects.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

The fourth absolute: The measurement of quality is the price of nonconformance.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

The quality improvement team requires a clear direction and leadership. Otherwise, people can get so involved with strategy and the selection of the team that they forget what the team is for.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

A great many people very rarely have exciting days at work.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

People don’t work for companies; they work for people.

Philip B. Crosby
