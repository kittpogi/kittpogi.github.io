Reflections on Quality

Slowness to change usually means fear of the new.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

You have to lead people gently toward what they already know is right.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

There is a lot more to golf than having the equipment and the intent.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

The people who have to put improvement programs of any kind into effect always feel that others are not for it.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

No one likes to get out front with too much, unless they’re absolutely certain it will be properly received.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Most things don’t work like they’re supposed to.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

The proof of understanding is the ability to explain it.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

No one is against preventing defects; it’s just that they don’t have the time right now.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

If we take care of the customers and the employees, everything else takes care of itself.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Executives cannot be sold into being serious about quality; they have to make the decision themselves, and in their own time.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

The primary measurement characteristic is how much money the company has made; the second is how long it has been around.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

I had always assumed that everyone else knew a lot more about everything than I did.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

We are all customers, and we are entitled to not be disappointed.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

The criterion of being a successful person is the ability to get something done.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

There are many cases where the apparently invincible organization becomes vulnerable overnight.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Being convinced one knows the whole story is the surest way to fail.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Debt begins to rise accounts receivable age faster; bright young people begin to drift off to other companies; a huge new headquarters building gets built; customers get neglected; housekeeping notably deteriorates; and management doesn’t seem to notice.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Most organizations have what appear to be suicidal tendencies.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Organizations don’t start out to be losers.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

People can’t last forever; the body eventually wears out. There is no way to prevent that from happening. But organizations have no need to die.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

When the employees and suppliers of an organization do things correctly on a routine basis; when customers are pleased that their needs are anticipated and met; when growth is internally generated, profitable, and planned; when change is welcomed and implemented to advantage; and when everyone enjoys working there – the foundation of an eternally successful organization exists.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Executives are more likely to be loyal to the company and its products than to the stockholders.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

I cannot recall a single meeting or conversation about doing something for the stockholders in all my years of working for large companies.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Executives know they can move on to another company and not even be missed after a few months.

 

Philip B. Crosby
