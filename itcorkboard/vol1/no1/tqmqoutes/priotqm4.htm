Reflections on Quality

In every operation there’s always one area that is more open to new ideas than the others.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

The purpose of policies is to settle arguments.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

One theory of human behavior is that people subconsciously retard their own intellectual growth.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

The bigoted, the narrow-minded, the stubborn, and the perpetually optimistic have all stopped learning.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

 

 

Customers deserve to receive exactly what we have promised to deliver.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

It’s always cheaper to do the job right the first time.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Many of the most frustrating and expensive problems we face today come from paperwork and similar "communications" devices.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Negative attitudes are more contagious than positive ones.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

You could spend an entire career and still not experience all the different things that can go wrong in business.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Just because corporate has "gotten religion" doesn’t mean anyone else has.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Anyone who considers quality improvement to be a motivation program will never attain Wisdom.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

You can get rich by preventing defects.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

An inspector is not a true inspector unless the inspection is independent and last.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

 

Once you put on a suit, no one tells you the truth anymore.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Good things happen only when planned; bad things happen on their own.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

The safety of a product isn’t a legal problem, it’s an ethical one.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

The basic cause of most product-safety problems is a lapse of integrity on the part of someone in management.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

The first thing you notice when management moves into Enlightenment is the relaxation of tension.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

If you don’t expect errors, and are truly astonished when they occur, then errors just don’t happen so much.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Imagine where your operation would be if you completely eliminated all costs related to failure.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

We are fragile and a vain group, we humans.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

The reason for having an independent, objective quality department is to limit the making of decisions by those who have nothing to gain from them.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

If something makes sense, is easy to understand, but still isn’t done consistently, there must be a reason for not doing it.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

You can make an excellent living actually doing the job of quality, as opposed to just auditing to find out why it wasn’t done.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

There is absolutely no reason for there to be errors or defects in any product or service.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Half of all rejections that occur are the fault of the customer.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

People really like being measured, when the measurement is fair and open.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

The only way senior management will let a quality team work on the future is if it is helping them to survive the present.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

People will only tell you the troubles that others cause for them. They will not reveal the troubles they make for themselves.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Auditing catches only the undedicated, bored, or careless.

 

Philip B. Crosby
