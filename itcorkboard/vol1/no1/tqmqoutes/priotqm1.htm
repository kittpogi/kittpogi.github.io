Reflections on Quality

The ideas I stand for are not mine. I borrowed them from Socrates. I swiped them from Chesterfield. I stole them from Jesus. And I put them in a book. If you don’t like their rules, whose would you use?

Dale Carnegie

Genuine recognition of performance is something people really appreciate. They will continue to support the program whether or not they, as individuals, participate in the awards.

Philip B. Crosby

Pick battles big enough to matter, small enough to win.

Jonathan Kozol

If quality isn’t ingrained in the organization, it will never happen.

Philip B. Crosby

Let us, then, be up and doing,
With a heart for any fate;
Still achieving, still pursuing,
Learn to labor and to wait.

Henry Wadsworth Longfellow

The executive’s problem in understanding and utilizing the labor force is compounded by the fact that people are not interested in doing something just because they have been told to do it.

Philip B. Crosby

The last of the human freedoms – to choose one’s attitude in any given set of circumstances, to choose one’s own way.

Viktor Frankl

It is difficult to think of any profession you would want to be part of that wouldn’t demand a great deal of integrity and compassion.

Philip B. Crosby

What we have to do is to be forever curiously testing new opinions and courting new impressions.

Walter Pater

When you make a mistake, admit it. If you don’t, you only make matters worse.

Ward Cleaver

If a pearl of wisdom drops from your lips when no one is around, it will have no value.

Philip B. Crosby

Honest criticism is hard to take, particularly from a relative, a friend, an acquaintance, or a stranger.

Franklin P. Jones

Being part of a team is not a natural human function; it is learned.

Philip B. Crosby

Being understood is hard work.

Philip B. Crosby

In the world of management, you can learn to be creative.

Philip B. Crosby

 You can create solutions to complicated problems by being the only one to break those complicated problems down to their basic causes.

Philip B. Crosby

The most important aspect of creativity for management style is not getting into the habit of stating your lack of creative ability.

Philip B. Crosby

The most valuable manager is one who can first create, then implement.

Philip B. Crosby

People don’t do for a living what they learned in college.

Philip B. Crosby

It is not possible to know what you need to learn.

Philip B. Crosby

Just about nothing is new, and almost everything used to be worse.

Philip B. Crosby

The leader who runs out of jobs for the led to do will soon be replaced by someone more interested in working hard.

Philip B. Crosby

If the leader is the only one who knows what game is being played, then obviously the leader is the only one who can win.

Philip B. Crosby
