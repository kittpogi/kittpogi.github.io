Reflections on Quality

There is a great deal of evidence showing that change can be accomplished without spending much money or inflicting a great deal of pain. The solution comes down to a few simple understandings.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Quality means conformance to requirements; management’s prime responsibility is to cause the right requirements to be created.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

All action must be oriented toward prevention.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Fortune 500 companies have several more zeroes at the end of their numbers, but their reporting and communicating requirements are very much the same as anybody else’s.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Expenses are the key to profitability. There is no revenue stream that cannot be outspent.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

It’s hard to find a company that failed because its expenses were too low.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

You don’t have to be noisy to be effective.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

This is a leader’s job description:

  • Create the right environment – on purpose.
  • Reduce complex issues to something each person can understand and learn to handle.
  • Concentrate on the objectives of the operation.
  • Relate to people at all times.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Everything is personal. People don’t want to separate home life and work life into two categories. You say it; they register it.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Don’t go around asking permission to do what you already have been commissioned to do.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Eliminating what is not wanted or needed is profitable in itself.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Successful people bred success.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

It’s okay to be a fanatic about something, but only if that something can be explained quickly.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

 

 

 

When it’s a pleasure to come to work because the requirements for quality are taken seriously and management is helpful, then attitudes change permanently.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

People should spend their time improving the quality process, rather than juggling it around to meet their feelings of the day.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Change should be a friend. It should happen by plan, not by accident.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

It’s hard to find an organization that both customers and employees regard with continuous affection and appreciation.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

In a true zero-defects approach, there are no unimportant items.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

The purpose of quality is not to accommodate the wrong things. It is to eliminate them, to prevent such situations.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

It’s hard to get people interested in improvement of any kind if they perceive it as a threat to their authority or lifestyle.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Never put development people in charge of production. They get too used to changing things whenever they feel it’s necessary.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

 

 

 

Quality is not a matter of having some superknowledge in some supergroup. It is a matter of managerial integrity. Either the requirements are taken seriously or they are not taken seriously.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

There is too much emphasis on the ineffective aspects of quality improvement. Many people still think it’s a technical problem, not a people problem.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

I used to get a lot of free lunches by betting general managers that I could find someone violating a safety rule during our tour of their facility. It was always the general manager!

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Requirements are answers to questions and the agreements that result from those answers.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

We need to keep "requirements" in the proper perspective. It is we who are masters, not the requirements. They serve to delineate an agreement between people, and should take whatever form is necessary. They must be respected and never altered, except by agreement among those who created them.

Philip B. Crosby
