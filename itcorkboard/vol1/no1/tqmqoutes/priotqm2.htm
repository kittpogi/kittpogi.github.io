Reflections on Quality

A good follower has to want the same results that the leader wants.

Philip B. Crosby

You never, never get away from being a follower.

Philip B. Crosby

Pretending all the time is a terrifying management style to adopt.

Philip B. Crosby

Listen. You can convey no greater honor then to actually hear what someone has to say.

Philip B. Crosby

Help. Let someone lean on you without expecting to lean back.

Philip B. Crosby

Create. Original solutions are the results of hard work in uncovering unoriginal problems.

Philip B. Crosby

Implement. There comes a time when someone has to actually get the job done.

Philip B. Crosby

Learn. When you have an answer for everything, you know you have stopped learning.

Philip B. Crosby

Lead. Leaders begin to fail when they begin to believe their own material.

Philip B. Crosby

Follow. You will never reach a stage when you aren’t working for someone, so learn to be good at it.

Philip B. Crosby

Pretend. If you’re going to be an actor, be a good one. But stay out of management.

Philip B. Crosby

"Zero Defects" is the battlecry of defect prevention. It means "do the job right the first time."

Philip B. Crosby

It is much less expensive to prevent errors than to rework, scrap, or service them.

Philip B. Crosby

Quality measurement is effective only when it is done in a manner that produces information that people can understand and use.

Philip B. Crosby

Most managers are so concerned with today, and with getting their own real and imagined problems settled, that they are incapable of planning corrective or positive actions more than a week or so ahead.

Philip B. Crosby

How you come across to others should not be left to chance.

Philip B. Crosby

Inside any organization, every employee has a customer.

Philip B. Crosby

There is no substitute for the words "Zero Defects." They are absolutely clear.

Philip B. Crosby

If you don’t know what the defect level is, how do you know when to get mad?

Philip B. Crosby

Many companies don’t have the slightest idea what happens to their product or their service once it leaves their hands.

Philip B. Crosby

Quality is free. It is not a gift, but it is free.

Philip B. Crosby

Why spend time finding, fixing, and fighting, when you can prevent the problem in the first place?

Philip B. Crosby

Without active management, there can be no quality.

Philip B. Crosby

People perform to the standards of their leaders. If management thinks people don’t care, then they won’t care.

Philip B. Crosby

Getting people to understand the need for and learn to have confidence in an improvement program doesn’t happen overnight.

Philip B. Crosby

The problem with using cash or financial awards to provide recognition is that they are not personal enough.

Philip B. Crosby
