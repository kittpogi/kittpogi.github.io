Reflections on Quality

The product is the organization, and the organization is the product.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

One of the most cynical statements imaginable is known as Murphy’s Law.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

I figure that every other person in service companies spends 100 percent of his or her time doing things over, chasing after data, or apologizing to someone.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

 

 

All requirements come from the customer in one form or another, because with no customers there is no business.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

The success of the quality improvement process does not depend on any "evangelical" powers possessed by the quality experts. It depends on education and implementation conducted in a serious and methodical way.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

It always bugged me that someone I didn’t choose got to make decisions about my career.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

The quality improvement process is progressive. One doesn’t just go from awful to wonderful in a single bound.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Each and every person in the organization must understand his or her personal role in making quality happen.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Satisfy the customer, first, last, and always.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

I start with two beliefs. First, those asking for help probably don’t know much about the problem or they would be fixing it themselves. Second, there is someone there who does know, and no one will listen to that person.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Quality has always been the most negotiable of the "schedule, cost, quality" triumvirate.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

"Zero Defects" means doing what we agreed to do when we agreed to do it. It means clear requirements, training, a positive attitude, and a plan.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

The thought of error being inevitable is a self-fulfilling prophecy. If you think it has to be that way, it will be that way.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

If we make our profit goals but don’t pay our bills, then we have not met our profit goals. If we deliver on time but the product has defects, we have not delivered on time. If we meet our safety objectives but damage somebody, we have not met our safety objectives.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Improving quality requires an overall culture change, not just a new diet.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Listen to everyone; seek out your customers and interrogate them. Don’t fall into ruts. Do what you do thoughtfully, but don’t go to sleep. Someone may be out in the garden digging up your treasure.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

No idea was ever accepted right out of the chute.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Good ideas, based on solid concepts, have great deal of difficulty being understood by those who make a living doing things the other way.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Trying to torpedo the competition should not be part of the business world. There is plenty of room for everyone.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Most real changes start somewhere in a company and spread around because they are worthy. They do not come out of the boardroom.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

 

It only takes one person, one division, one group to change the whole company.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Relationships are what business is all about, and there are only two that matter: the one with customers, and the one with employees.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Someone has to be responsible for everything... or nothing will get done.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

There are no failures in introducing quality improvement. No one ever gets worse, and no one ever doesn’t improve.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Success is a matter of return on effort.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

There is a Quality Revolution going on, and the quality people are missing it because they have their eyes on what goes out the back door, whether it’s a product or a service.

Philip B. Crosby
