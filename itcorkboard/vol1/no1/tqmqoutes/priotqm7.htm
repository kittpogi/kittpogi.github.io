Reflections on Quality

The eternally successful organization does not have to contend with uneven times, and is assured that survival is only an academic question.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Every business changes every day in some way.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Airlines are run by people who have never flown like normal folks.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

The outstanding teams are invariably said to have accomplished their victories by "execution".

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Very few of the great leaders ever get through their careers without failing, sometimes dramatically.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

We can spend our whole lives underachieving.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

The great discoveries are usually obvious.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Higher-ups have "mission statements" which sound good but are impossible to measure.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Selecting the right person for the right job is the largest part of coaching.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Just being honest is not enough. The essential ingredient is executive integrity.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

 

Strategy is one of the most overworked words in a manager’s vocabulary.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

Management has to lead, but in some cases it has to drag people along.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Every time I hear the word mission I think of big, complicated, political-sounding document that everyone nods about but no one takes seriously.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

 

 

Grow where the business is; don’t try to make a market where none exists.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Of course the customer comes first! Who doesn’t know that?

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Nobody’s customers stay put.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Today’s correct concepts are good only for today.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Top management should know some customers personally.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

No matter how it turns out, I’m looking forward to my brush with reality.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Look at change as being a friend instead of a pain in the neck.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Watch out for changes recommended eagerly by those who would profit from them.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Customers aren’t as forgiving as our bodies are.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

 

 

I would never send something to a customer that wasn’t what I had promised to send.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Staying out of the quicksand is much better than getting a good deal on a towing contract.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

It doesn’t take long before the people running a place become convinced that it is their own personality and personal charm that cause everything to hum along so smoothly.

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

White-collar workers are about 50 percent effective.

Philip B. Crosby
