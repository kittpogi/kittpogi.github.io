Reflections on Quality

Millions of products are produced every day that don’t wind up in court.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

People just want their rights – until you try to trample them. Then they want revenge.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Companies that truly want to solve problems must create a culture of openness that is imbued with the basic concepts of integrity and objectivity.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Every human conflict produces winners, losers, and observers.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Over 85 percent of all problems can be resolved at the first level of supervision they encounter.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Once or twice in your career you will encounter something for which there is no solution. That’s when you make a judgement call, accept the consequences, and get on with your life.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Specific problems require specific solutions.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

"Assembly" is just making big things out of little things.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Quality improvement has no chance unless individuals are ready to recognize that improvement is necessary.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Unless you know how you’re doing as you go along, you’ll never know when you’re done, or if you’ve succeeded.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Anything that tastes good is bound to be bad for you.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Authority can be delegated, but only after it has been packaged.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Debt is not a friend.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

One of the myths of management is that meetings are a gigantic waste of time. Only poorly managed meetings are not useful.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

The price of nonconformance in manufacturing is more than your pretax profit.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

We have traded Bob Cratchit’s quill pen for a computer, but the process is still the same.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

We think our customers want us to do things differently. We should find out for sure.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

It is very easy to become complacent and slip away from communication.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Some "purposeful conflict" is necessary for success, but it can waste a lot of time if the purposefulness is not properly contained.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Setting forth a list of commandments will not convince anyone that management is serious about integrity. After all, integrity is as integrity does.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

People like to think they shape events; in reality it’s the other way around.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

In the final analysis, their immediate supervisor is the person employees see as "the company."

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Quality is free, but no one is ever going to know it if there isn’t some sort of agreed-upon system of measurement.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

Half the people in most manufacturing plants never touch the product.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

We are all service people, except when we are blood donors. Then we are manufacturing plants.

 

Philip B. Crosby

 

 

 

The most difficult lesson for the quality crusader to learn is that real improvement just plain takes a while to accomplish.

 

Philip B. Crosby
