DLSU Gets ORACLE Financials

With consultation and coordination between System Finance, Accounting, Internal Auditor, and IT offices, De La Salle University System selected Oracle Financials/Payroll as its Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) system. This will take care of automating various operations in all five campuses. This will also solve all Y2K-related concerns for some computerized systems.

The Project covers General Ledger (GL), Accounts Receivable (AR), Accounts Payable (AP), Cash Management (CM), Fixed Assets (FA), Inventory, Purchasing, and Payroll. [Payroll is actually a module of Oracle's Human Resource (HR) package.]

One good thing is that these modules only use a single user interface -- the Netscape Communicator web browser.

The ERP system will initially be implemented in DLSU-Manila. It will be later deployed to DLSU-CSB and then to the other campuses. The ongoing Project is expected to be completed by the end of the school year. It is still at the early stage of implementation.

The Project Steering Committee members are Dr. Carmelita I. Quebengco, Mrs Teresita B. Farrell, and Dr. Wyona Patalinghug. The Project Manager is Sammy Mallare, while the Assistant Project Manager is James Sy. Ms Cynthia Lim (Internal Auditor), Ms Gloria de Leon (Controller, DLSU-Manila), and Ms Marietta Langga (Controller, DLSU-CSB) are also members of the Project Team.

At present, controllers, internal auditors, some Accounting Office staff, and IT center staff from the 5 campuses are undergoing training on the different modules.

Samuel P. Mallare 07/05/1999

Use of the Zip Codes

Last May, ISTC was surprised by an email of Ms. Sally Nicolas announcing the strict implementation of the zip codes by the Philippine Post Office. According to her, mails will not be sent by the Post Office to the addressee without the appropriate zip codes. Several people reacted violently. They said there was no news announcements in the TV, radio, newspaper or any form of mass media. The Registrar’s Office staff were surprised because the Grade Report to Parents were not accepted by the Post Office so they had to determine the zip code of each student and type this on the report. It’s not easy to do this for 12,000 students.

Sympathizing with our predicament, I asked a friend who was working at the Post Office why they did this. She said that the use of the zip codes has long been being reminded in the media but it fell on deaf ears. Now they implemented a new system of sorting mails by zip codes so they had to put their foot down!

How about you? Do you know your zip code? Fortunately for those who don’t, we have it on the DLSU web page. Click on the Philippines icon on the main page, then select General Information. Click on the listed second "Zip Codes" or you can go directly to the URL http://ourworld.compuserve.com/homepage/Chris_Blunt/zipcodes.htm.

Celine V. Rivera 07/02/1999.
