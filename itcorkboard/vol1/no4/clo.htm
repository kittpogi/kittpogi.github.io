Computer Laboratories Upgraded

Recently, ISTC upgraded the computer units in several computer laboratories.  The Pentium 200 computer units of L229 and V103 were upgraded to Pentium III last December 1999.  This makes the number of upgraded computer laboratories to four (4) since September 1999 when computer units in L212 and G304 were upgraded to Pentium Celeron 400 from Pentium 100.  By summer, this year, the computer units in the other laboratories, namely, L335, J212 and G404, are scheduled to be upgraded.

This move is in line with the mission-vision of DLSU to provide state-of-the-art technology to students and faculty in pursuit of excellence.

Celine Rivera, 02/11/2000

SA Reunion held last  December 4 at CSB-Angelo King

The Event
The night was a celebration, not only of the much-awaited reunion of the Student Assistants but also of the 15 years existence of the Student Assistantship of the Information Systems and Technology Center.

The Organizing Body
The organizing committee, aside from the Coordinator, was composed of Central Committee Members headed by the Special Projects Assistant and some other Student Assistants who volunteered to help make this event successful.  And of course our distinguished guests and fellow alumnus, for without them, this event would have not been made possible.

It was a momentous occasion for the organizing body. After a grueling series of planning, countless meetings and a number of phone calls to possible donors and participants, this was the ultimate conclusion. No more turning back as they say, and as the organizers have it, “it was the end of a real big challenge."

The Performers
The set of numbers presented that night were actually the product of the constant practice by the talented Student Assistants under the mentoring of the Special Projects Assistant.

We are proud to say that in all of the numbers the members, be it in the song, dance or live band performance, were all  SAs. This just goes to show what kind of people we have—a multi-talented bunch of extra-ordinary students.

Glen Bertulfo, 02/11/2000
