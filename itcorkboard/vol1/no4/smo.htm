Y2K Compliance of Systems

The year 2000 came to pass just as peacefully and spectacularly as a full-length live coverage of the countdown presentations from around the globe, as seen on TV.  Unlike the earlier negative speculations and apprehensions about the overrated Y2K bug and system compliance, no current issue  spoke of the chaos that the new millennium was projected to bring.  DLS-PSI ISTC stands witness today, like the rest of the organizations which have gone through some painstaking preparations and testing.  The following activities were done to ensure Y2K compliance of the systems being maintained:

An inventory of all programs was conducted and each program was checked for Y2K compliance. Some Faculty Information System (FIS) reports were revised; Student Records Management (SRM) in VAX was migrated to Oracle; Faculty Attendance Monitoring (FAM) in Clipper was rewritten in Visual FoxPro.

On January 2, James Sy, DBA, came to the office to check on the enrollment programs but was not able to check the DLSFAST server which was not up at Accounting Office that day.

January 3, first day of classes, DLSFAST server was just re-dated. No problems so far.

Lichelle B Legutan 02/11/2000

FAM Implementation

For Y2K compliance, a new Faculty Attendance Monitoring System (FAM) was developed to replace the old one.  The Attendance Section of the Office of the University Registrar is now using it.

The system is installed in Netware6, drive M, which means those with authorized access can view or print the reports as needed.  The system automatically uses the Netware6 user ID during log-on, but it needs a separate password.  Data entry and processing of records are accessible only to the attendance checkers.  The EVP, VPA and Registrar have access to all reports, while the Deans of the different colleges have access to the reports of all departments under their colleges. The chairs can only browse on screen the reports for their department.

The new FAM has three new reports, which are the attendance of the borrowed faculty, report on exceptional cases and report required for a faculty member’s promotion. The usual reports will still be distributed.  These reports include the daily reports, which are the Faculty Notice of Absence, Attendance Report (for the Department Chairs, Dean, VPA, EVP, and Registrar), and Report of Early Dismissal and Tardiness (for the Department Chairs).  The other reports are summary reports for the month, for the trimester, and for the year.

Catalina M Quiogue 02/11/2000
