ISTC Christmas Party 1999

December 17, 1999 was the night ISTC held its annual Christmas Party. It will be a fun-filled affair, the organizers have promised and it turned out well, full of fun… and more.

The party kicked-off with the organizers giving away prizes to the winners of the Parol Contest and the Dartsfest. CFO bagged the Parol Contest plum edging out the pre-judging favorite DAO/DOCU office. Mr. Ricarte Galang of CFO bested other participants to win the Dartsfest (Advanced Level). Parlor games were played next and I must say these parlor games were wild, but sure did have its share of great moments and graphic scenes. After the games, a seven-course dinner was served complete with lechon courtesy of Optimum.

Four choral groups vied for the singing contest, which was won by Group 3. The winning group pulled off a surprise comic rendition of popular Christmas carols that made the audience roll out in laughter and join them in singing. Now that’s audience impact!

ISTC was lucky enough to have a band called Rubberband perform for them for free during the Christmas Party. The band belted out a mixed repertoire of alternative, disco and new wave music. That "Dancing Queen" number must have registered a 10 on the Richter scale. The SITE students also treated us with musical numbers; a choral rendition of "Pasko Na, Sinta Ko" and an R&B song. Too bad they did not joining the singing contest (as if we’d let them!). The final musical number was from the Christmas party organizers jamming to the song "Fruitcake". The traditional exchange of gifts and revelation for the Kris Kringle was the culminating activity of the party followed by the raffling of Christmas goodies from various suppliers.

ISTC Christmas Party 1999 was so much fun that we are already looking forward to next year’s Christmas Party. A job well done for the organizers.

 

Francis Mark D. Santos 02/10/1999
