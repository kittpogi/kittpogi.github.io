    OD Updates


Performance Incentive Awardees

After months of deliberating, the Excellence Awards Committee have come up with the decision as to how the incentive fund will be allocated for this School Year.  The incentives are for:

Excellence (P10,000.00 and plaque for one winner, and P5,000.00 for the two runners-up)

  • Term-based Perfect Attendance (P5,000.00 every term)

  • Term-based ISTP Assembly Attendance (P4,000.00 every term)

  • Best Idea (P3,000.00 annually)

The conditions and other details for each of the awards are available in the network (at f:office\istc\istp\awards).

ISTC has qualified for the ISTP assembly attendance incentive for the first and second terms of School Year 1999-2000.  The average number of late frequency for the whole center for the first term is 2.89 while that for the second term is 2.72.

Congratulations to Francis Santos and Ronald Warner for maintaining a perfect attendance record for the first term of this school year and Jhun Galang, Ricky Ponce, and Francis Santos (again!) for the second term.

For the other awards, the awardees will be determined at the end of this school year.

Sammy Mallare, our Director, thanks everyone for the improvement of attendance.  There has been a big decrease in tardiness and a lot more people now received the certificates for Perfect Attendance.  Among those who were added to the regular monthly list last January 2000 were Brian Co, Harold Paulo Bautista, Johnny Benitez, Patricia Antonio and Eileen Manabat.

CAP transfer to PSI

The Co-Academic Personnel (formerly non-academic staff) seconded to the Professional Schools, Inc. (PSI) will now be transferred from the Human Resource Development Office (HRDO).  This is for autonomy and to facilitate control.  The CAP will be included as another employee category to teaching faculty, ASP and ISTPs.  The EVP Office will now issue the ID numbers and forms for CAP transactions.  So our very own Sally Nicolas will now be part of PSI.

DLSU disconnecting from PHNet

DLSU has disconnected from the PHNet and has transferred to MosCom to save money.   The MosCom link satisfies DLSU requirements.  However, DLSU will remain in PHNet’s Board as one of the founding members.

ISTC Director participates in AUN Conference

Mr. Samuel Mallare is participating in the preparation for the Asean Universities Network (AUN) Conference together with the EVP-PSI, CCS Dean, Academic Linkages Director, MCO Director, among others.  The theme of the conference is “Towards a Virtual University."

Information Systems and Technology Center to become IT Center

Starting School Year 2000-2001, ISTC will be renamed as IT Center.  This is in line with the objective to have a common name for all the IT centers in the DLSU system schools.  All the other system schools have adopted the name.  The IT Committee is studying the ISTP Manual for a possible single manual and the logo design for the IT Center is being drafted for all the system schools.

Because of the change, the ISTP will be renamed to Information Technology Professionals (ITP).

New Bulletin Board for ISTP matters

The Office of the Director is having a new bulletin board constructed to post ISTP concerns.  These include perfect attendance awardees, completed projects, customer satisfaction rating, team-building activities and ad hoc committee activities.  The new bulletin board will facilitate information dissemination within ISTC.

The existing bulletin board will be used for announcements, e.g., newsletters, memos, among others.

CFO up for renovation

There is a plan to have the Computer Facilities and Operations Office renovated.  This is primarily to have a better “atmosphere” for our clients consulting/visiting our help desks.

Recollection to be conducted for ISTPs

ISTC is planning a one-day recollection on February 29, 2000.  The theme is "Practicing Spirituality in the Workplace." The Teambuilding Committee is still finalizing the venue and speaker availability.  The recollection is a respite from our busy work schedules and is expected to refresh our spiritual life.

PSI plans for expansion

To accommodate the increasing number of students and to offer more space for facilities and services, the Professional Schools, Inc. is planning to expand to RCBC Plaza (Ayala corner Gil Puyat avenues) or to Filinvest Cybercity in Alabang (near Festival Mall and Ayala Alabang).  A study is being conducted to determine which of the planned areas is feasible based on accessibility to faculty and students’ workplace and residence.

Samuel Mallare & Celine Rivera, 02/11/2000
