Transmeta Crusoe Processor

Transmeta has announced the smart microprocessor, Crusoe, last January 19. The new chip will be primarily used for ultralight (less than four pounds) mobile pcs and internet devices and boasts a power consumption of as low as 10 to 20 milliwats and as high as 1 to 2 watts for heavy duty usage such as multimedia. This can be contributed to its use of the following:

VLIW (Very Long Instruction Word) processing, "Code Morphing" software translation and LongRun power management.

Because of this, it runs as cooler than today's processors without the need for a fan or a heat sink even when running at its peak, has the lowest power consumption (even when in sleep mode), and power consumption even changes depending on the demands of the application software.

These are the two chips they have developed: TM3120 (333 to 400 MHz) and the TM5400 (500-700 MHz). These two chips are compatible with the complete range of x86-based operating systems and applications that run on versions of Linux, Windows 95, Windows 98, Windows NT, and Windows 2000. But since Linux Torvalds is also developer of the chip, Mobile Linux is out to also support these chips.

For more information, visit www.transmeta.com, search for Transmeta articles at www.linuxjournal.com and/or follow the url http://www.sharkyextreme.com
