Let us hear something from PRIO's new babies

YEAR 2000

This new year - New Millennium is a fresh start for me. New working environment, new people and organization. I believe -- and hope that I'm starting my new year right by moving here. So far the acceptance has exceeded my expectations. I could say talagang cool and mga tao dito. Hindi yon chika ha!

Well, I hope you could appreciate my contribution. I'd like to share a poem given to me by a classmate in college. She gave this to me before graduation. So here goes...

A Creed to Live By

Don't undermine your worth by comparing yourself with others.
It is because we are different that each of us is special.
Don't set your goals by what other people deem important.
Only you know what is best for you.
Don't take for granted the things closest to your heart.
Cling to them as you would your life, for without them life is meaningless.
Don't let your life slip through your fingers by living in the past or in the future.
By living your life one day at a time, you live all the days of your life.
Don't give up when you still have something to give.
Nothing is really over until the moment you stop trying.
Don't be afraid that you are less than perfect.
It is this fragile thread that binds us to each other.
Don't be afraid to encounter risks.
It is by taking chances that we learn how to be brave.
Don't shut love out of your life by saying it's impossible to find.
The quickest way to receive love is to give love, the fastest way to lose love is to hold it too tightly, and the best way to keep love is to give it wings.
Don't dismiss your dreams.
To be without dreams is to be without hope.
To be without hope is to be without purpose.
Don't run though life so fast that you forget not only where you've been, but also where you're going.
Life is not a race, but a journey to be savored each step of the way.

From Joel G. Kisteria

ADJUSTMENT

First day at work, I was wondering what the day would be like… I knew that there was a lot of adjusting to do… since everything (the people, the culture etc.) will surely be totally different from my previous job (I used to work for a Bank). One thing was for sure --- I cannot escape from the first day jitters. Luckily, when I entered room 308 of the Gokongwei building, I was greeted and welcome by two "young and bubbly" ladies… (they know who they are) who really made me feel so at ease and relaxed…it’s like knowing them my whole life (hey, no tears please…). These ladies were kind enough to introduce me to all my co-workers using a giant parol! (care of PRIO - cute no?) I thought –"Paano kung hindi photogenic?" 

Anyway, I thought I could relax already because everything was going okay until I was informed that we were going to conduct a time study on "Adjustment" that same day… Actually, I was already informed that I would be "working" on my first day… I just didn’t take it so seriously…(tsk., tsk, tsk, - my fault…)

So there I was -- my first tour of De La Salle… It was quite overwhelming… but I survived… with the help of my "new found friend." Actually, she made work that day so much fun!

In case you want to know, I had my formal orientation of De La Salle on my third day… In fairness to PRIO, pinasyal rin naman nila ako around the campus.

From Patricia B. Antonio

Process Review & Improvement Office, 02/11/2000
