As a review, 5S stands for the Japanese words: seiri, seiton, seiso, seiketsu, and shitsuke. To fully understand what they mean in Filipino or English, refer to the table below:

Japanese English Filipino Practical Application
Seiri Sort Suriin Separate the necessary from the unnecessary. Discard the unnecessary.
Seiton Systematize Sinupin There is a big gap between limited resources to unlimited wants. So, conserve time and energy, focus on the important, not just the urgent.
Seiso Sweep Simutin Make immediate repairs and constant checking and cleaning.
Seiketsu Sanitize Siguruhin Make time for preventive maintenance. Safety first with a clean environment.
Shitsuke Self-discipline Sariling kusa Change is the only constant thing in this world and it takes a firm decision to change for the better. Habits are learned and bad habits are unlearned.

 

Francis Mark D Santos, 02/11/2000
