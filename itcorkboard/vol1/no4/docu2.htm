DLSU-Manila Web Site Nominated in the Philippine Webby Awards 1999

The DLSU Web Site was nominated in the Children/Education category of the 1999 Philippine Webby Awards.  However, the web site “The Beggar’s Hand: A Flight from Poverty”, won the coveted award.  In the People’s Choice Award, where anybody can vote for their preferred site per category, DLSU-Manila was leading with 493 votes until the deadline last November 15.  Unfortunately, the deadline was extended until the day of the awards on November 17, and the web site of Informatics Philippines eventually took the lead.

Being nominated gave us quite an experience of excitement and challenge.  And winning the award would have given the Web Development Team a deep sense of fulfillment.  But we take pride in being able to land on the 6th place among the other nominees for the People’s Choice Award.  A reason we hold on to, to work harder in keeping our web site more updated, more complete and more interesting for people to access.  Better luck next time, DLSU!

Celine V Rivera, 02/11/2000

DLSU web site nettled by the Y2K bug

The infamous Y2K bug hit the unsuspecting Documentation Office through the Internet and Intranet web sites.  The java script used to display the current date on all the pages of the DLSU web sites was overlooked and showed not being Y2K compliant - it displayed the year 2000 as 192000.  The incident was discovered on the second day of the New Year by the ISTC Director who quickly tried to remedy the error by fixing the script used to generate the date.  Unfortunately, the remedy done only worked for Internet Explorer browser, while the Netscape browser still displays the year 2000 erroneously as year 100.  So instead of trying to find the correct formula, the Web Development Team decided to download another script which can display the date correctly.  It took the Web Development Team a week to fully correct the error. 

Joie Alavarez, 02/11/2000
