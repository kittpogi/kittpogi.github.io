ISTC Involves CCS Students in its Projects

 Since the summer of School year 1998-1999, ISTC has been farming out some of its projects for CCS students.  The SITE students were assigned projects for development as practicum projects for two (2) terms.  After these, they entered into the Extended Thesis Program (ETP) wherein some students opted to use these projects for their thesis.  Three (3) groups are presently working on three (3) projects, namely:    1) Employment Monitoring Information (EMI) – to support the Office of Career Services, 2) Conference Monitoring System – to facilitate registration and management of conferences, and 3) Course Approval System – a web-based system to facilitate course advising.  The first project is nearing completion while the last two projects are in the design stage.

With the move, DLSU will benefit a lot because the projects will help automate the operations and processes of some offices.  Under the guidance of the Systems Management Office, the development of the systems according to ISTC standards and user requirements will be ensured.  In the future, when users start implementing these systems, maintenance by ISTC will be easier.

Celine V Rivera, 02/11/2000

DLSU-Manila Implements Oracle Financials

In the dates when there was supposed to be a "Live feed", the implementation of the Oracle Financials is now being viewed on a slightly-delayed basis. The personnel doing the production runs have hit a few snags along the way that caused the delays.

According to Mr. Manuel Santiago of the Internal Audit, the Accounts Payable module is already up and running, and that DLSU is doing a parallel run with the old accounting system, DLS-FAST. With the Accounts Receivable (A/R) module, they're currently encountering problems with the Official Receipt.

They're also currently entering the transactions for the General Ledger (GL) for the month of November. The mapping of accounts from DLS-FAST to Oracle financials is being checked, and the Financial Statements Generator is being compared if it matches with the reports from DLS-FAST.

The Oracle support staff, consisting of Ms. Grace Ogena and Ms. Jenny Santos, visit DLSU at least once a week and check on the progress of the Oracle Financials implementation. The team hopes to finish the A/R and GL modules by the end of January 2000.

Harold Paulo D Bautista, 02/11/2000
