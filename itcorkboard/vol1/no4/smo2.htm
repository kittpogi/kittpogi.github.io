ID Access (IDA) System update

As we may have all noticed by now, bar code scanners have been placed in selected gates of DLSU.  These scanners either emit a short beep or a strained noise when you place your ID in front of it for examination.  This is the ID Access (IDA) System in action.

According to Ms. Rowena Santos, project leader for IDA, program testing will continue until the latter part of this trimester.  After a meeting held last January 13 with Ms. Agnes Yuhico, DLSU’s Assistant Vice President for Academic Services, Mr. Enrico Cordero, the Assistant Vice President for Administrative Services, Dr. Richard Gonzales, the University Registrar, Mr. Dionisio Escarez of the Security Office, and other school officials including our very own Samuel Mallare and Vivian Sy, it was agreed that only ISTC, the Discipline Office (DO), and the Registrar’s Office (RO) will have access to IDA.

For the meantime, the system’s dry run will continue in the South, North, Velasco, and Gokongwei gates of the university.  Among the numerous features of IDA, its capability to generate statistics on entry volumes and the interconnection between the RO, DO, and ISTC stand out.  This interconnection enables the Discipline Office to automatically update IDA’s database on lost IDs preventing their misuse as well as allowing the Registrar’s Office to identify those students with delinquent accounts.

The actual implementation date of the system has yet to be identified but Ms. Santos states that the system should be fully functional by the start of the next school year. 

Standard Flowcharting Guidelines for ISTC

Guidelines on the use of flowcharts and their appearance will be finalized soon.  These standards will be the result of earnest cooperation between SMO and PRIO.  Ms. Amalia Salcedo of PRIO and Mr. Brian Co of SMO have been working together gathering and analyzing data from the world wide web and other references in order to update ISTC’s flowcharting rules and to be at par with international standards as recommended by the American National Standards Institute (ANSI).

Among the more notable changes suggested are the use of a standardized VISIO template and the employment of a prescribed flowcharting report format.  This means that all flowcharts generated by ISTC will have a uniform look because all flowcharting symbols used (as well as the overall layout) will come from only one source.

The proposed guidelines will initially be tested for a period of one month before it is revised (if necessary) and finalized.  After which, a short talk will be given in order to orient ISTC personnel on the use of the new standards.  Office-wide implementation of these guidelines has been scheduled on the third week of February.

Brian Co 02/11/2000
