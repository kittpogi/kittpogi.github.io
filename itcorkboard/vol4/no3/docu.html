The Redesigned DLSU-Manila

We want to join the bandwagon and write about the newest thing that has evoked much reaction from the La Sallian community, the new DLSU-Manila website. I hope we are in unison in saying that this is the direction we want to steer our projects to – utilize the best technology that we can benefit most from, and introduce it to our public so they will know where we stand in technology today.

This is the outcome on our use of active server pages (ASP) and Server Side Includes (SSI) in redesigning our website. ASP allows the web designer and developer to format the design theme, generate HTML, automatically detect changes, dynamically compile needed files and store compiled results for subsequent requests.

With SSI, we can execute conditional logical comparisons, query or update our database with no programming or Common Gateway Interface (CGI) scripts, in real-time. We also employed dynamic formatting using cascading stylesheets (CSS). This means that we can easily manipulate content and layout separately should there be a need to enhance some details.

Our current website has dynamic and simplified navigational menus, accessible search engines in most pages and up-to-date images.

In the bottom portion, categorized announcements and postings are classified into “Bulletin”, “What’s New” and “Today”. In the bulletin, you can find the latest postings from the registrar’s office, information for faculty, alumni and the DLS System Schools. The different conferences are enumerated in the “What’s New” column, and events, quality quotes/Lasallian thoughts, online broadcasts and employment opportunities are found in the “Today” column.

The new web site contains updated and comprehensive information on the colleges and frequently asked questions. Duplicate pages were also deleted. Flash animation is also one of its newest features.

Web Design Feedback Form
by: Precious Anne L. Taliwaga

As if we don’t get enough feedback on the new DLSU-Manila site, we are formulating a rated response questionnaire to determine how user-friendly our newly-designed web site is. The objective is to improve the design and usability of web sites across different perspectives. We classified our audience into freshmen, students (undergraduate and graduate), alumni, faculty (internal and external), administrators, employees (internal and prospective) and researchers.

We want to document how they find navigating the site, and their common mistakes when accessing the site. We would also like to know what features they find most helpful, the average time they need to find the information they are looking for and, if the images used were relevant and served their purpose, among others. Answers in the form will serve as our guidelines in improving the site.

Ideally, we planned to do this before launching but the form was not ready yet. Well, it’s not yet late to do it. We’ll be looking for willing samples from every user type.


My.LaSalle Portal Update
by: Precious Anne L. Taliwaga

The new interface of My.LaSalle portal is finally taking shape. Using ASP technology for the new template, it is now easier to maintain multiple static web pages. This gives us more room to enhance its features as the need arises.

In the old portal, features were limited to clearance information, course schedule, e-mail, subject actual count and extra-curricular information system. With a more powerful server, the University Clearance system, online enlistment and enrolment and grades query will be part of the services offered. The portal also allows faculty members to submit the final grading sheets for the courses they taught.

We wanted to give it a distinctively new look and played with the banner. The DLS System logo will be used but the copyright remains with DLSU-Manila. The Login page will be made accessible to College of St. Benilde (CSB) students through their web site. We were able to devise a way wherein the information on the main page changes corresponding to the campus where the student comes from.

DLSU - Canlbuang Website
by: Precious Anne L. Taliwaga

Talk about information in real-time. The <DLSU-Canlubang web site> is already accessible for those who would like to get acquainted or know more about the newest member of the DLS System.

In the homepage you can find a description of the physical atmosphere in the campus, the course offerings, the services and instructional materials.

In its left side, you can find the quick links, integrated schools and the degrees that will be offered for the school year 2003-2004. Below the banner which shows the school building artwork, is the search engine localized within the Canlubang site.

In the quick links, the overview, vision-mission, maps (location and site development), contact information and admission requirements are enumerated. The integrated schools link is where you will find the main features of DLSU-Canlubang. It contains the bulleted list of the main thrust of their curriculum, the instructional strategies that will be used, the qualifications of the faculty, the available facilities that are essential in molding holistic individuals and the library media programs that are within reach to support the academic curricula.

A brief description of the different degrees that are offered in each school is provided in its quick link.

Slated to open for school year 2003-2004, it will offer five degree programs in the fields of Information Technology and computer, Engineering and, Management and Entrepreneurship. The classes will start late June following the trimestral system.
For the moment, the web site will be hosted in DLSU-Manila. As more development arise, the web site will be expanded to facilitate in information dissemination and marketing efforts of the new school.

Multimedia Bank at Last... Now Online
by: Precious Anne L. Taliwaga

After more than a year since it was started, the Multimedia Bank (MMB) will now be made available to the public. The primary purpose of this site is to provide access to a database of images, audio, video and presentation files that can be searched by faculty and students alike.

Files are categorized into stock photography, artworks, illustrations, sounds, videos, animations and presentations.

For the image files, most have been saved in the Joint Photographic Expert’s Group (JPEG) format and in the Graphics Interchange Format (GIF). Files of both kinds usually occupy small memory space. Images are shown with thumbnails and come with captions.

The most widely used types of files for the audio, video and presentation are available in the database. The audio files come in the WAV, MP3 and AIFF formats. Videos available are in MPEG, AVI, MOV, WMV and ASF formats while presentations are in powerpoint, flash, SWF and PDF files.

So that a user can download/upload files, and suggest/comment on the site, he/she should have an account. Enrolling is easy. The usual information regarding the user are asked. These are the user name, password and e-mail address.

Anyone can contribute to the multimedia bank. All he/she has to do is register to the site, then upload his/her file. The administrator will evaluate the files sent according to the terms and conditions, and if the files are added to the bank, an e-mail notification will automatically be sent to the file sender. Files that are not added to the bank are deleted and the sender is also informed. Limit to the file size per transaction is in place so as not to adversely affect our bandwidth or eat up too much space.

For those who download from the bank, they will be asked to supply their accounts information on the manner and purpose of using the files. This will give us an idea of how useful the site is and the type of customer we have. visit it here

 

PINAS SITE
by: Precious Anne L. Taliwaga

“Your gateway to Philippine Information” will finally be launched. At last, we are in the final stages of completing our newest web site.

This Philippine site has a lot to offer. We chose to highlight information regarding general information, culture, tourism, business and economy, information technology, sports and recreation, history and government.

If you just want to refresh your memory about our language, weather and culture, this site is worth the time. If you are looking for the places to visit this summer, the tourism link will provide you with the best sites to check out, where to stay, and the activities (e.g., fiestas, tourist sites, etc.) you can indulge in while staying there. Even information on the place to spend your night-outs can be found here.

The Business and Economy section will provide you with links to the hottest e-commerce issues, the top 100 corporations and the latest information on how our currency is doing in the exchange rates scenario. We have also provided you with links to selected e-banking companies.

The government link is worth browsing too. Its sub-topics include agriculture, labor and welfare, justice, health, education and government e-services.

For all those sports buffs out there, check out the latest in Pinoy sports and recreation. As for “techies”, listings on local search engines, ISPs, chat sites and e-mail providers are here for you to visit.
