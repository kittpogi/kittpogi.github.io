PRIO Launches TQM Training
 By: Janet O. Chua

As a trainer, you always have to do a lot of preparation. You think of the topic, what mediums to use and how you will deliver to create an atmosphere conducive to learning. But what is exciting about conducting training programs is when you felt in your own way your participants have exhibited new skills, saw new opportunities, had greater confidence and a sense of direction to contribute to the company and are motivated to action.

PRIO, in coordination with the Institute for Faculty Development and Human Resource Development Office conducted TQM trainings last March in DLSU Manila. Participants were allowed to choose from 3 modules - these are: TQM Education and Concepts of Improvement, 5S: The Practice of Good Housekeeping, Customer Service: Customer is Always the Customer. Participants were engaged in activities involving practical applications of principles and concepts. As expected, speakers and attendees exchange thoughts and opinions on certain issues.

The succeeding month, PRIO conducted another training in DLSU-Dasmarinas. This time, participants came from the different IT Centers of the system schools. ITC Directors were also present. The two-day training seminar not only allowed the participants to capture and understand TQM but also gave them the opportunity to meet the other ITPs. The ITPs from DLSU-Dasmarinas and DLSU-HSC gave a tour and assisted the participants around the campus. They visited the historical museum, the library, and the chapel. Everyone had a great time.

PRIO is just starting to make a difference by allowing the academic community to embrace and practice TQM. TQM training may serve as a refresher or an eye opener - but definitely it works.
