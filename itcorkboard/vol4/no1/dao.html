Data Administration Office : Updates

Cost Estimation Model
 By: Florinda D. Tamonan

One of the directions of DAO is to be able to migrate old data systems into one central database, standardize the table and field convention, and create a central warehouse for all these data to be able to hasten the reporting of substantial information to most decision-maker.

Cost Estimation Model (CEM) report is one of the vital reports generated yearly. This year, the CEM for SY2000-2001 was released to the management. Presented in the report were different methods used in allocating the expenses or cost. There are nine sets of module attached to it. Each component leads to essential information like effectiveness of each department in terms of class size, the per unit cost per department and the efficiency of each department based on faculty manpower.

At this point, the greatest challenge in the CEM report is deciding and selecting the right allocation bases and the right allocation method that should be used. It is now the management discretion on what best approach to be employed.

For all its intents and purposes, the CEM is basically the yardstick that the management can use to judge the performance of each responsibility center composing the institution. It will enable them to measure the effectiveness and efficiency of their decision from a cost-wise perspective.

Standards for Position Codes
By: Florinda D. Tamonan and Celina V. Rivera

The Position File is one important reference files for a number of application systems, the Faculty Information System, Staff Information System and Students Records Management (graduate studies). Since the start of DLSU, a number of positions have been created and there was no standard procedure how these should be coded in the databases.

Research on position titles in the different industries as well as in DLSU was undertaken to come up with the appropriate codes. The position codes were likewise grouped by level such as management, staff and professionals. The old codes were mapped with the new codes. The new Position reference file is now being used by the System Management Office to update the respective field in the databases of the systems identified above.
