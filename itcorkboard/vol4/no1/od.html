Office of the Director : Updates

This school year, ITC has a number of major projects that are expected to be completed assuming all of them are approved by administration. These are:

ORACLE Financials/Payroll
by: Samuel P. Mallare

This is expected to be in full implementation before the end of 2002. For us, the next challenges are to ensure the security of the system and to increase our knowledge to support our customers. For our customers, the next challenge is to increase their knowledge so they may fully utilize its functionalities. This includes turnover activities when there are changes in job responsibilities.

University Portal
by: Samuel P. Mallare

About a decade ago, we provided our customers a character-based menu system where they can choose an option and execute a program. It included access to SRM-related programs, VAX Mail, VAX Phone (for chat), VAX Notes (for discussion boards), and Internet services - e.g., Telnet, FTP, Gopher, WAIS, newsgroups, and Lynx (text-based web browser). In today's lingo, it was a University portal. Then we moved on developing information systems with graphical user-interface (GUI). These were either client/server-based or web-based. Our objective was to provide more intuitive systems so our customers would be more comfortable in using our programs.

After developing our own web-based portal, we will now purchase a portal package so we can focus on developing University-related applications. The portal is to be the system that will integrate all our web-based information systems. This one-stop shop for our information services will enable us to push information to our customers. It will also enable our customers to pull information that they need. Our objective is to enable our customers to access needed information "at the click of a mouse."

We have seen the following portal packages - Cleverpath from Computer Associates and Oracle9iAS from Oracle. We have yet to see Sharepoint Server from Microsoft and Websphere from IBM.

We hope that the portal package could be used in our Internet and Intranet web sites. It should have a built-in search engine and web-based discussion board. This will enable our web development team to focus on the content and spend less time on the design maintenance activities.

The University portal could bring our knowledge management initiatives closer to what the University needs. We hope to get the approval of the administration on the purchase of a portal package and implement it on or before October 2002.

Data Warehouse
by: Samuel P. Mallare

This project was identified as early as 1998. However, it was not a good time for us to start because we have urgent projects on Y2K, web-based development, and process automation especially related to back office operations - e.g., accounting, purchasing, and warehouse. It was also time to build expertise on data warehousing technology.

With all the transactional information systems that we have developed over the years, we need to gather relevant data and store them in a "data warehouse." This will give us better focused to develop decision support systems. We could even provide tools to specific customers to empower them to retrieve needed data and generate reports.

After this, we will automate the generation of Cost Estimation Model (CEM) reports. This is to enable our administrators to get information and make timely business decisions. We can even attempt to go to the extent of simulation with hypothetical figures for projection purposes or for assessing what-if scenarios. The simulation could also be used when determining cut-offs for incoming freshmen so financial impact could be quantified right away.
