Knocking Down and Putting Up the ITC Walls
 By: Joie B. Alvarez

During ITC's early years of service in the University, one would go to the Miguel Building (then Benilde Building) to knock on the then Computer Services Center's door. But when I joined the IT Center in 1996, it was already holding office in Gokongwei Building and SMO, PRIO, DAO and Docu offices were all housed at G308 while CFO occupied the present conference room; the conference room then was just a small area also in G308. During that time I can still remember one of my first tasks, which I did not welcome with much enthusiasm, was to go to G309 and have an inventory of pieces of office furniture to be retained and to forego. G309 would then be our (DAO and Docu) future office.

That time, G309 was a cluttered room, covered with dusts, and lined with cobwebs. It was in fact a big room partitioned into three small areas and the main room. The first small room was being used by Optimum technicians that time (Telectron, who replaced Optimum still uses that small room at present). The second small partition was like a receiving area and a passageway to the main room and the third small room and it was also used by the Student Assistants as their headquarters. The third small area was like a storeroom for CFO. The main room, on the other hand, was like a warehouse for shelves, steel cabinets, and the other office furniture.

After clearing the room and putting up the cubicles, the room was already a cross between an office and a mini-warehouse cum library. The SA's area and the main room was used as the DAO and Docu Offices and the third small room served as a mini-library and storeroom as well. Stuffs whose destiny could not be decided yet were usually confined there. As a matter of fact, several shelves of old manuals, boxes and all sorts of things (from obsolete diskettes, video tapes, to spoon, plates, and broken acoustic guitar) can be found in that room. A total of eight cubicles were put up in G309 that time although only half were occupied. But not too long after, the room ended up with almost all cubicles occupied and even needed to set up another one to accommodate all the staff.

Not very long when the DAO and Docu staff moved from G308 to the newly refurbished G309, the CFO people left the present conference room area and transferred to their present office at G306. Meanwhile in G308, the mini-conference room area was dismantled to provide extra space for the SMO and PRIO offices and additional cubicles were put up. The former CFO area (G307) became the present ITC Conference Room.

Almost six years has transpired and the ITC offices once again underwent a major reconstruction to accommodate its growing population. Partitions in G309 were removed except for the small room for the Telectron technicians. A total of 12 cubicles were set up, nine were occupied leaving one extra cubicle each for DAO and Docu offices. One cubicle was dedicated for the multimedia station of the Docu office. Also, a mini-conference area was built to augment the conference room reservations. The DAO and Docu people are still waiting for the completion of the renovation as the wall cabinets were not yet in place and other minor aesthetic repairs were not yet done. At the same time, the cubicles in G308 were replaced and rearranged. The ITC Secretary's area was also renovated into a counter-type nook and the furniture in the Director's office was changed into modular-type to match with the cubicles of other ITC units. To date, SMO and PRIO people are waiting for the delivery of their cubicle cabinets and setting up of wall cabinets. Now, all units of the Center sport the same light gray and bottle green color of cubicles. Each cubicle has a keyboard drawer, small hanging cabinets, mobile drawers, and an open case for the CPUs.

These renovations are expected to accommodate the growing number of ITC people at least in the next 2-3 years. The new office set up is also expected to provide the ITPs a suitable environment for productive work and it leaves each ITP no excuse to practice the 5S principles.

Passing the Key
by: Joie B. Alvarez

After three years of heading the very first ITP Assembly, the former officers decided that it is high time to bequeath their positions and let a new batch take charge in time for the start of the new school year. The ITP Assembly is a group that represents the ITPs against issues on employment, employee benefits, working conditions, policies and procedures, and complaints or grievances. During the first ITC Staff Meeting for school year 2002-2003, the election was held.

Joie B. Alvarez

Several days before the election, the outgoing officers formulated a simple guideline for the conduct of the election. During that meeting, it was deliberated that the new officers should be a sectoral representative of the units (i.e., one representative each from DAO, Docu, and PRIO, and two each from SMO and CFO because of their large number). That equates to each ITP voting seven persons.

After the election, results were announced and not one of the seven elected representatives is a Coordinator, as if addressing the ambivalence of the Coordinators to be voted in any position since to a certain degree, they are the more senior ITPs and may also be looked upon as representing the Administration. Also, the newly voted officers were a mix of junior ITPs and those not so senior ones. They had their first meeting last June 14 where they voted among themselves who would fill-in the key positions of president, vice-president, secretary, treasurer, grievance officer, and ITP representatives (2).

During their first meeting, the new set of officers discussed the completion of any pending issues as far as the ITP Assembly is concerned, and determined the office policies with gray areas or undefined implementing guidelines.

On the other hand, before relinquishing their positions, the outgoing officers came out with by-laws, duties and responsibilities of officers, and grievance procedure that could be used by the incoming officers in the performance of their duties. And with this, it is expected that the incumbent officers would be able to deliver more for the welfare of the IT Professionals.
