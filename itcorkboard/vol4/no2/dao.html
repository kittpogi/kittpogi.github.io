New masterfile for religion?
 By JC Ibay & Celine Rivera

With the many encoding errors we've encountered for religion data, DAO decided to create a standard for the different 'religions' that are in the student file. First, we cleaned the religion field in the student data (you wouldn't believe it but we saw a religion "Makati", "Gohonzon", "Metaphysician", "Single", and the list goes on….). Secondly, we did a research on the Internet on the different religions. Thirdly, we compared these with the religion data to determine if what we find in our files are considered religion or beliefs. We have such listings as Christian-Buddhist, Non-Sectarian-Christians, and Orthodox-Catholic among others. Then we listed down the religion data which we couldn't compare with those from the Internet. There were still unidentified religion but we've decided that we will consider these. For statistics reporting, we'll classify them under 'other Christian religion'.

During this activity, we've discovered that there are a lot of beliefs or religion that we rarely here about, religions/beliefs such as Zoroastrianism, Eckankar and Nazarene to name a few. There was even a discussion of how we'll store the data, religion or what the person is called (e.g. catholic or Catholicism? islam or muslim?) or if a Jew is the same as a person whose belief is Judaism or a national of Israel. We'll still create the codes for these and do more research to iron out the issues. Maybe, we should consult a theologist….calling a religion expert!!!


An Integrated Department File in the Making
 By JC Ibay

We are now working at integrating the Department file for the use of all existing applications we have in the University. Department files are used by systems such as Student Records Management, Faculty Information System, Cost Estimation Model, Staff Information System, ID Access System, Faculty Evaluation System , Faculty Attendance, Payroll, and Clientele Application used by the Helpdesk for handling service requests.

We are in the process of consolidating all department files used by the said application systems looking at discrepancies in codes being used as well as consistency in names and abbreviation of each department.

After having checked all these, we will come up with a much improved classification of units operating under Departments such as the Student Personnel Services, Library and other non-academic units in the University. We will also be identifying which departments are retired or inactive.

Upon completing this process, we will be able to have a more consistent department file or unit file used by application systems across the University as well as avoid duplication of task in terms of updating or maintaining the different versions of department files. The unit file will also contain new data that concern a department, e.g., local number, generic email address of unit head.
