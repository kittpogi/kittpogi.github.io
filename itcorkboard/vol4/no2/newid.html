DLSU-Manila ID sports a new look
 by:Lemuel Cabia


Once again, ITC came up with a new design for the new digital ID card system. Our identification badges now aren't just for visual and barcode identification, but in combination with smart chip (smart card), it can store data and facilitate payment for transactions internal to DLSU.

Unlike in our previous ID designs, there is only one design to be used for the IDs of all university employees and students, differentiated by color codes. Proposed color codes are red for full-time faculty, aqua blue for part-time faculty, chrome-yellow for administrative service personnel (ASP), brown for co-academic personnel (CAP), violet for both academic service faculty (ASF) and information technology professional (ITP), and green for students. Colors will appear as a transparent stripe on the lower portion of the design.

New design studies depict the facade of the newly constructed DLSU Administration Building. Different angles or shots of its towering cylindrical pillars or columns have been considered. A play of colors was devised to make it more artistic. Other design considered is the top view of DLSU-Manila by night.

The new design will be printed using a new printer, the EdiGuard Re-Transfer Printer Sub-system. The image quality output from this technology is fairly superior to our previous typical plastic card printer (the EPISuite). The 'contact-less' card does not need to be taken out from your wallet or case when being scanned. The card material is also of a higher quality that will last for at least three (3) years.

ITC is still negotiating for a different design for PSI employees and students. Take a peek of our proposed design and the one which was approved for DLSU-Manila
