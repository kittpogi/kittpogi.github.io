Data Administration Office : Updates

DAO Generates the Cost Estimation Model
by JC Ibay & Flor Tamonan

Data Administration is currently working on the Cost Estimation Model (CEM) as requested by Bro. Andrew of the VPAR Office.

CEM, presents data on the expenses incurred by each college. The report is used by the EVP, VPA and Finance Director. It gives the administrators an estimate on how each college performed for a given period. In the past, the System Management Office generated this model.

DAO is now in the process of validating the collected data to ensure data integrity. In the process, extensive research is also being made on Cost Estimation process and principles to provide the top management a more comprehensive set of data for analysis and for decision-making. The process is also being documented to provide a reference for future requirements.

The structure of the model is being designed following the structure that is required in datawarehousing. With these, data in the past years can also be reconstructed to provide information in the datawarehouse.

Creation of coding standards for industry positions and area of specialization
by JC Ibay

The Data Administration Office recently made a research on Positions that can be found in different industries. This was used as basis for our Position Masterfile. Inclusion of the DLS positions has been made and the proposed coding standard is now being evaluated.

Similar activities are also being done for our Specialization Masterfile. An updated file of the Library of Congress will be used as a basis in building our Specialization file. DAO is currently evaluating the coding method.

Evaluation of software for database analysis and report generation
by JC Ibay and Celine Rivera

Data Administration Office is currently evaluating decision support applications and we have looked closely at Crystal solutions, namely, the Seagate Info and the Crystal Enterprise; and Microsoft SQL..

The Seagate Info is a complete suite of analytical, reporting and distributions technologies. It has integrated ad hoc query, multi- dimensional OLAP analysis and report design and generation. The software can design cubes or information views which can facilitated ad hoc report generation and datawarehouse design. This was evaluated by JC and Flor to determine if it can be used for accessing all types of databases at ITC, namely: Oracle, Microsoft SQL and Foxpro. The software passed our evaluation but the cost will still be evaluated.

Microsoft SQL which we already have, can also do what Seagate Info can. However, it cannot do publishing of reports on the web. It is hoped that a newer version that has web capabilities will be out in the market soon.

On the other hand, a demo on Crystal Enterprise V8, an enterprise solution for complete report designing and data analysis and information delivery via web, was conducted by Crystal Solutions, Inc. It include Crystal Reports v8.5 and Crystal Analysis v8.0. Crystal Analysis is used to navigate information rich reports through powerful analysis and visualization to identify trends and patterns for superior interactive analysis of your OLAP (Online Analytical Processing) data. And with the entirety of the solution, Crystal Enterprise, you can get interactive analytic reports out to your named users over the web. The software will be further evaluated if it meets our requirements.
