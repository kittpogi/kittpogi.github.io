Search Engine Optimization
By: Precious Anne L. Taliwaga

Meta tags are words or phrases that help search engines classify and rank a web page. They instruct web browsers to perform specific tasks, and identify keywords and site definitions, among others. The DLSU-Manila web site has almost 3,000 pages. Though information is organized well, some people find it easier to use its Search function extensively. For this reason, the DOCU Office reviewed the major folders and selected the appropriate keywords. The DOCU Office also took into account statistics reported by Atomz and Awstats.

To date, "mylasalle" which used to be the most frequently searched word, is the first item that will be included in the report when it is searched. This is just one keyword among many. The process of updating the meta tags is being done weekly.

CHED-ZRC Web Site
By: Precious Anne L. Taliwaga

As DLSU-M aims to be the top research university in the Asia-Pacific region, ITC is proud to be part of the move to work towards this vision. Recently, the Commission on Higher Education (CHED) chose DLSU-M as one of its Zonal Research Centers (ZRC) (http://www.dlsu.edu.ph/research/centers/ched_zrc/default.asp). As a result, the CHED-ZRC web site can now be found in the DLSU-M web site. The web site contains the National Higher Education Research Agenda, priority research areas, overview of the different grants it offers, downloadable portable document format (PDF) forms and the CHED research framework.

New WISe Web Site
By: Lemuel B. Cabia

To maximize the potentials of wireless computing, the DLSU Wireless Information Services (WISe) is now on its re-design stage. DLSU's IT Center has previously ventured in developing practical real world applications for such devices. This is to strengthen WISe’s purpose of informing and guiding the academic community about the benefits of wireless information services.

Now, the new WISe web site includes not only includes guidelines & procedures about cellular phone's Short Messaging System (or SMS, popularly known as text messaging) and Personal Digital Assistant (PDA or handheld devices), but also information which typically gives a detailed account on wireless services and mobile computing. The objective here is not only to showcase applications that extend university information wirelessly, but to educate the academic community about mobile or wireless computing, i.e., what are the options and technologies, what are needed, and what or how it can be done.

The new site covers items such as Wi-Fi or wireless networks (WLAN) and portable devices such as notebooks, PDAs or Pocket PCs, flash memory modules (e.g., memory stick & CF cards), standard cellphones and other PDA-phone gadgets that are capable of desktop-like performance and features.
