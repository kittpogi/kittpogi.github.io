We're back in Manila!
By: Samuel P. Mallare

It was in 1996 when we spun off from DLSU-Manila and became part of DLS-PSI. That was eight years ago! We're now about 52 here in ITC. Less than 10 of us have already been in ITC before PSI was formed. (Does that sound like a good trivia question?) All the events that happened in Manila and PSI were unprecedented. They're really for the (history) books. There were good ones. There were those that we just want to forget. Our life in PSI is now history.

It seems that very little has changed. We're still in the same office. We still service the same offices. This is because the bigger changes have yet to come. DLSU-Manila is a dynamic organization. Procedures and polices change - e.g., our promotions (et al) are handled by the VPAR. This school year is a negotiations year. We can expect more changes next school year. Administrators change - e.g., we have a new System AVP Finance. New offices are formed - e.g., we will have a graduate school admissions office sooner or later. Let's just make the best out of what we have and where we are now. We're back in Manila!

Teambuilding Themes
By: Samuel P. Mallare

The Teambuilding Committee met on June 11, 2004. The Committee discussed "themes" for teambuilding activities. These are:

  • Getting to know
  • Involvement
  • Goal-Setting
  • Maximize Performance
  • Motivation
  • Commitment
  • Delegation
  • Recognition
  • Leadership
  • Resolving Conflict
  • Time Management
  • Creativity

The success of our teambuilding activities depends on us. Try answering these questions.

  • Who are the members of the team?
  • Who are responsible for building the team?
  • Who will benefit from the building the team?
  • Why do the members need to build the team?
  • What are the members willing to commit for the team and for themselves?
  • What behaviors/attitudes/values should the members nurture to continuously build the team?
  • How do the members build the team?

If all our answers are similar, we have made a great achievement already. Don't you think so?

mm, come to think of it, what if we consider all our activities/work as teambuilding activities? I think this is our ultimate challenge. The awareness on building the team should go way beyond our "TGIH" activities. So, do we really want to build our team? If your answer is yes, we'll look forward to have more fun, more participation (not just presence), and a more cohesive team!
