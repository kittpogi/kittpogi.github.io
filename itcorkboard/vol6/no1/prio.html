Still ITC... Still PRIO...
By: Joel G. Kisteria

In cooperation with offices in the Center, PRIO tackles the improvement of the Performance Objective Measurement. The Center has already incorporated this move in the planning sessions. At the start of SY 2004-2005, we are closely monitoring our set goals and objectives.

The Performance Objective Measurement PRIO proposes, traces its reference to the “Hoshin Kanri”, another term for policy deployment. It provides step-by-step planning, implementation, and process review for managed change.

To summarize, Hoshin Kanri is: Hoshin = Target + Means

There are many books as well as some software that can be referred to in observing and following the Hoshin Kanri approach. (http://www.hoshinplanning.com/hoshin_fairly.htm, http://www.iqd.com/hoshin.htm, http://www.tqe.com/hoshin.html)

PRIO tried to introduce some conventions of this approach in the planning sessions of the Center. From the existing planning methodology, certain conventions were introduced. This customized the process to our needs.

Example:

Services Offered

Develop new product/system.

  
Performance Measure Performance Objective Checkpoints/Means
Decrease : New product development cycle From 8 months to 4 months by December
  • Establish an effective development process
  • Develop documentation for the product development
  • Implement Quality Function Deployment (QFD) within the quality assurance system


Although the Hoshin Kanri approach may be tedious at first, this can be really effective especially if done with commitment. As an advice, here are some environmental scanning activities that can be done in pursuing the Hoshin Kanri for the organizations:

  1. Assess the need for change
  2. Evaluate the business environmental situation (i.e., political, economic, social, technological, and cultural)
  3. Understand competitive trends by benchmarking, conducting industry analysis, and evaluating competitive intelligence
  4. Project trend information to develop alternative future scenarios
  5. Build mutual consensus among the management teams regarding implications, or observed and projected indicators
  6. Develop business objectives that address possible alternatives
  7. Identify core objectives and gain shared commitment within the business

Remember P-D-C-A? Your Plan-Do-Check-Act cycle? Well, it is an integral component of the Hoshin Kanri. This is important in the CONTROL step of all of the ongoing within the organization.

We are all looking forward to better management of our activities and processes. Now it's time to see, if we can make this one work for us. Let's do our share!
