Single Forever?
By: Arfeli B. Balanza

When I was asked a week ago to contribute in the next issue of our Corkboard, I said yes hoping that I could make it before the deadline. Unfortunately, I couldn't think of anything to write about until I had a late night chikahan with a friend. Maybe I could write something about me so I decided to give my viewpoint on being single. on being ME. Hopefully, not all ITPs will be interested to read this.

It is very often that I'm asked why, at my age, I am still single. Through the years, I have mastered the art of answering question with wit, honesty, and sometimes... sarcasm. I can get away with the prudent "Wala pa siya eh" or the mataray "Wala kang 'K' magtanong, di tayo close". And the comments I get - you cite one and I'm sure I've already heard it. Some would say the caring "It is okay, as long as you're happy"; a few would say the pathetic "It is sad to be alone" Others would say the amusing "Sayang naman lahi mo." (I like this one. Ha-ha-ha!)

My view of life is simple. I know that life is sometimes rewarding and sometimes frustrating. Life has its intricacies and when faced with such, I get by with the help of God up there and those who are around me. I believe that the key to happiness and contentment is up to me. I decide how to stay contented. I just have to love being who I am (with all my imperfections), and everything in the world is wonderful. I only have one life to live and I want to make the most of it. (Now, I'm talking sense.)

So, what if I stay single forever? There's nothing wrong with that. There's nothing to be afraid of. It's a matter of choice and I choose to age gracefully (hopefully).

I guess I'm learning to live with what I have and what I don't have. But, the child in me will always and forever believe in fairy tales and in magic.

Need I say more?
