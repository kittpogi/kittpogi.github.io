Plone is a generic platform for web applications with ready content management systems useful in managing web content. It focuses on solving business problems by making processes more effective. It enables organizations to gather all information and resources in one central web-accessible area. This fosters significant synergies though one common web platform with unified content management and publishing for intranet, internet and extranet.

Plone also allows publication between different net parts while retaining the individual differences in look and feel for its many parts even though the content is the same.

Plone works with Zope, a widely-used Open Source web application server and development system written in Python. It allows products and techniques to share information with other sources such as relational databases and filesystem files.

It is ideal for project groups, communities and intranets. It runs on the platforms Windows, Linux, Mac OS X, among others.

RFID: Radio Frequency Identification

I have always watched Batman track down his criminals using some sort of chip device and find its location with his computer.

RFID is the technology that includes tags and reader devices using radio waves to identify living (thats you and me) and non living things (there goes the consumer privacy list). Wal Mart and Seven Eleven are already looking into this kind of technology. It will help them improve process efficiency, inventory control and also cost savings!

So how does RFID work? A basic RFID only needs 3 things, an Antenna or coil, a transceiver with decoder and a transponder otherwise known as the RF tag programmed with unique information.

Some RF tags have the battery life of up to 10 years.

If you have watched the movie "Along came Polly" there is a scene there where Jennifer Aniston uses the key finder to find her keys, RFID key rings are now in the U.S.market.
