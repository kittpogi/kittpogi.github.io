Systems Management Office : Updates

Short Course System
by: Ryan Q. Salvanera

The Short Course System (SCS) is envisioned to be a miniature enrollment system. Specifically geared towards courses offered by offices like College of Computer Sctudies Consulting and Education Center (CCS CEC) and CISCO, the system is expected to have a huge impact as it encompasses all processes from preparation of Statement of Accounts to ID printing down to the preparation of Certificate of Completion.

The system is still being designed by the Support Systems Team of SMO, and will be implemented and deployed by next term.

Faculty Scholarly Output Module
by: Agnes Bulay

Are you up for promotion, reclassification or permanency? Do you need to recall publication details?

Then this one is for you.

The Systems Management Office (SMO) created a Web Based Faculty Scholarly Output Module that captures the scholarly outputs of the DLSU faculty. This system was developed in accordance with the requirements of the Office of the Vice-President for Academics and Research (VPAR). It will serve as an on-line storage system where faculty members are required to update personal scholarly data prior to promotion, reclassification or permanency.

Eventually, the VPAR Office can prepare descriptive reports for strategic planning with ease and assess each faculty member's strengths and capabilities.

Short-Term Course Registration System (STC) Deployed Soon
by: Brian Co

The Office of the University Registrar is scheduled to deploy the newly-developed Short-Term Course Registration System (STC) this coming third term. STC is an online registration system for use by the Computer Education Center (CEC), Center for Language Learning (CeLL), and CISCO Networking Academy for their short-term course offerings. It will mimic the current manual process flow in that the system will cover a participant’s registration, assessment, grade evaluation, all the way to the printing of certificates.

The system also aims to streamline the short-term course registration system as well to make it more efficient, secure, and reliable.

The reports were required for the BIR audit on the Accounting Department. The group who worked on these reports included Robert de Jesus, Joel Santiago, Brian Co, Arfeli Balanza, and Vivian Sy.

 Resource Management System
by: Roberto de Jesus

At the start of the new year the Resource Management System is expected to be deployed. The system would enable Department Chairs and their secretaries, as well as student organizations, to make classroom reservations for their department/organization online. It involves usage tracking of resources in different locations, the date and time of the reservation, and the department / organization which made the reservation. It also monitors the delinquency of a reserving department for the effective use of the classrooms.

StSOT Meetings
by: Vivian Sy

In line with the thrust of SMO to improve its operations, the Student Systems Operational Team (StSOT) has finally been able to meet regularly, i.e. once a week. The team meets to discuss updates on the Calendar of Operations, problems encountered, as well as activities that need to be done based on meetings of the MIS, Operations Council, OUR-ITC, Enrollment Committees, and others. With the weekly meeting, the team is able to monitor activities that need to done for the week, to document activities already done (as well as reasons for delays in processing), to address concerns raised in committee meetings, and to refine procedures to better service the users.

The StSOT members include Arfeli Balanza, Rhaniel Bingcang, Loveleah Mallari, Lorna Acorda, and Vivian Sy. The Team meets every Wednesday, 10:00-12:00, at the G308 Conference Area.

If there are any concerns you would like to raise with the Team, please feel free to e-mail any member, or to join us during our weekly meeting.

My.Lasalle Parent
by Rhaniel Bingcang

MLS-Parent is a system proposed by the Office of the University Registrar (OUR) which aims to provide up-to-date information to parents regarding the academic and non-academic status of their children. The parents will be given usernames and passwords so they will be able to access the facility which will be available via the Internet. They will be able to use this account anytime and anywhere.

The system will provide the following options: (1) View Class Schedule; (2) View Assessment; (3) View Academic Status; (4) View Discipline Records; and, (5) .View Final Grades


The system will be implemented with the parents of the incoming Freshman of Term 1, SY 2005-2006. The parents of the current students will be informed about the facility and will be given until next year to start using the facility. After the one-year period, the Report of Grades to Parents will no longer be printed and mailed to the parents.

QA Team
by Vivian Sy

The QA Team has been formed to implement Quality Assurance in the development of applications. So far, the team has scheduled trainings on the use of Visual SourceSafe (VSS) for source control, and on the implementation of QA during program implementation. The team has started discussing schedules for the drafting of the UML Standards, and Programming Conventions for PL/SQL. The team has gone into the design of the Software Development Monitoring Form, and the Implementation Monitoring Form, e.g. for monitoring the use of the handbook by the Software Architects, the implementation of the .Net programming conventions, and others. The Team is also coordinating with the CFO regarding the set-up of servers for source control and test environments.

The VSS and QA trainings that have been scheduled for December 1 will be from 10-12, at the ITC Conference Room. After the trainings, the entire SMO team will be undergoing workshops in the afternoon to ensure that the concepts taught during the lecture have been understood by the participants. Immediately after the training, the different system teams -- Student, Employee and Support -- will be expected to submit their programs for safekeeping by the Software Librarian, Joel Santiago.

The QA Team is composed of Ryan Salvanera, Lorna Acorda, Harold Bautista, Rowena Santos, Joel Santiago, and Vivian Sy. The Team meets every Thursday, 1:30-3:30, at the G308 Conference area.

If you have any suggestions on what areas of concern the Team should work on, please feel free to e-mail any member. If you prefer, you could also join the Team during its weekly meeting.
