Marikina Boys Town Outreach : February 14, 2004

Heart for the Boys
by: Harold Paulo D. Bautista

The 14th of February - a special day. Yes, we all know the rituals: flowers, chocolates, gifts, candlelit dinners, movie dates, and what-have-you's. The point of the matter is to show how much you care for someone. Heck, even our favorite resident Santa had something to do during this occasion.

Birthdays. that time of year. You get to celebrate the day you popped out of your mother's womb; the day the world first heard your voice; the day you became a statistic in the government records; the day you start making your mark on the world. It's a time for celebration!

So what happens when ITC mixed the 14th of February with the birthday celebrants of the month?

That's what around 45 kids were treated to when ITP volunteers went to Boys Town Mahay in Marikina during The Day of Hearts for the annual ITC Outreach. Donations were given in the form of toys, toothbrushes, face towels, crayons, and pencils. To top it all, a Jollibee kiddie party was held at the Boys Town Mahay grounds. There were party hats, giveaways, games, food, and even a personal appearance by Brian's twin - The Bee himself - who the kids enthusiastically greeted.

The ITP volunteers gamely participated in the games conducted by the Jollibee personnel wherein the kids showed hyperactive tendencies beyond the realm of eating chocolates for lunch. The outreach lasted for about two hours, after which the volunteers proceeded to another venue for an eat-all-you-can event care of Ryan's better half.

Part of the funding for the outreach came from the fundraising activity organized by the teambuilding committee. Items new and old were donated by ITPs, and theses items were displayed in the conference room. A silent bidding was conducted wherein bidders wrote their bids on a piece of paper and dropped them in a box. The committee was able to raise a significant amount for the outreach activities.

But after all were said and done, the outreach wouldn't have been possible if not for the generous deeds of the February celebrants. Instead of celebrating at the office, they decided to pool their resources for the outreach benefactors. Hats off to all of you!
