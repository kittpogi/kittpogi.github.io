  • Office Related Injuries & Illness

  • Office Electrical Safety

Office Related Illness Injuries

  • Falls:
    • tripping over desks, open drawers, elec cords, bending from an unstable chair, etc.
  • Prevention:
    • walk on a clear pathway
    • close drawers after use
    • avoid excessive bending, twisting or leaning back around while seated
    • secure elec cords & wires away from walkways
    • use step ladder & not chairs for overhead reaching
    • Clean up mess & spills immediately
    • Report loose carpeting/damaged flooring
    • When falling- roll don't reach!
  • Strains & Overexertion:
    • improper way of carrying reaching/lifting materials
  • Prevention:
    • balance self when lifting items
    • never carry loads that block your vision
    • try arranging tables/items to avoid twisting
    • store materials at knee level to avoid too much bending
    • when carrying materials/loads some distance, try getting support or hand truck or cart to transport it.
  • Struck By or Striking Objects:
    • bumping or striking against office machines, hit by dropped items/equipments
  • Caught In-Between Objects:
    • fingers or articles of clothing caught in or in-between objects, windows, doors, office machines, etc.
  • Material Storage:
    • improper piling of stocks leading to falling objects, poor visibility/obstruction, etc.

Workstation Ergonomics

  • Common Complains:
    • neck pains, also in shoulders, back eyes, arms due to positioning & design of workstation
  • Prevention:
    • Arrange workstation to fit you by adjusting chair height. Be comfortable with arm & back rest
    • Apply good work practice by adjusting drapers/blinds, moving the monitor away from light to minimize glare. Proper handling of telephone. Moving from different position regularly. Keep regularly used items within reach. Take mini-breaks to rest eyes & muscles. Maintain a comfortable workplace

Indoor Air Quality & Ventilation

  • These are associated w/ chemical contaminants from inside or outside the office, which may come from paints, sealants and the likes
  • Other sources: dusts, smokes, office re-modeling & maintenance, emissions of some equipments,
  • Prevention:
    • Adequate ventilation of office, including design & maintenance
    • Periodic cleaning of air-conditioning system
    • Special attention (isolation) of operation that may generate contaminants (painting, spraying & heavy cleaning)

Noise

  • Unwanted sound from materials that may include printers, telephones, machines & human voices. These can lead to interference w/ speech & communication, annoyance and distraction from mental activities.
  • Prevention:
    • Selection of quietest equipment possible.
    • Proper maintenance of equipment (lubrication & tightening)
    • Relocation of loud equipment in areas where its effect is less disturbing.
    • Use barrier/walls/dividers to isolate noise sources
    • Schedule noisy tasks at time when it will have less effect on the other tasks in the office.

Office Electrical Safety

  • Ungrounded equipment, overloaded outlets, unsafe/un-approved equipment, defective and improper installation of cords, working on "live" equipment, pulling of plugs to shut off power, etc.
  • Prevention:
    • Inspection of outlets, cables, wirings & electrical connections of equipment
    • Practice safety first in working with electrical connections.

One last thing. Practice Safety First!
