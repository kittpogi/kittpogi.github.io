Are you left brained, right brained, or whole brained?
 by: Johnny Perpetua Benitez Jr.

Find out if you are left brained, right brained, or whole brained! Please write down the number of A, B and C answers, and then scroll down the page to find out what your score means.

  1. I remember best... A) names B) faces C) both names and faces
  2. I prefer to have things explained to me... A) with words B) by showing them to me C) both ways
  3. I prefer classes... A) with one assignment at a time B) where I work on many things at once C) both ways
  4. I prefer... A) multiple choice tests B) essay tests C) both types of tests
  5. I am... A) not good at body language, I prefer to listen to what people say. B) good at body language C) sometimes good, sometimes not so good
  6. I am... A) not much of a comedian B) funnier than Robin Williams C) sometimes funny, sometimes not
  7. I decide what I think about things... A) by looking at the facts B) based on my experience C) both ways
  8. I like my classes to be... A) planned so I know exactly what to do B) open with opportunities for change as I go along C) both planned and open for changes
  9. I am... A) never inventive B) very inventive C) occasionally inventive
  10. I like to express feelings and ideas... A) in plain language B) in poetry, song, dance, etc. C) both ways
  11. I respond more to people when... A) they appeal to my intellect B) they appeal to my emotional side C) both ways
  12. I... A) prefer total quiet while studying B) prefer music while reading or studying C) listen to music when reading for enjoyment, not studying
  13. I prefer... A) dogs B) cats C) both equally
  14. I would like to write... A) non-fiction books B) fiction books C) no preference
  15. I have... A) almost no mood changes B) frequent mood changes C) few mood changes
  16. I have the memory of... A) an elephant (good!) B) what was the question? C) an ordinary teenager
  17. I prefer... A) verbal instructions B) demonstrations C) no preference
  18. I... A) use time management to organize my life B) have difficulty organizing my time C) organize my time sometimes
  19. I dream or think in... A) words B) pictures C) both equally
  20. I am... A) easily lost while finding directions B) good at finding directions C) not bad in finding directions, but not good either

HOW TO CALCULATE YOUR SCORE

Count Lefts (A), Rights (B) and Wholes (C). Take B score and subtract A score. This number can be minus or plus. Call it your MAGIC number.

If your C score is 6 or high, divide your MAGIC number by 3. Round our score to the nearest number. This answer will be your score.

If your C score is from 3-5, divide your MAGIC number by 2. This is your score.

If your C score is less than 3, do not divide at all. Your MAGIC NUMBER is your score.

SCORE

  • A score of 0 = Whole brain dominance A score of -1 to -3 = Whole brain dominance, favouring the left
  • A score of 1 to 3 = Whole brain dominance, favouring the right
  • A score of -4 or lower = Left brain dominance
  • A score of 4 or higher = Right brain dominance

THE LEFT BRAIN. This is the typically logical and critical side of the brain. It deals with reason, mathematical skills and analysis. The reading writing and language centres are located on this side of the brain. Other characteristics of the left brain are:

  • Intellectual
  • Remembers names
  • Planned and structured
  • Prefer talking and writing
  • Controlled Emotions
  • Responsive to structure of environment
  • Not good at interpreting body language
  • Prefers multiple choice tests

THE RIGHT BRAIN. This side of the brain is more abstract and creative. It is strong at visual imagery, and it controls dreams. It also controls emotions, and artistic talent. A right-brained person is strong in the arts and in abstract thinking. The dominant characteristics of a right brained person include:

  • Intuitive
  • Remembers faces
  • Fluid and Spontaneous
  • Prefers drawing and manipulating objects
  • More free with feelings
  • Essentially self-acting
  • Good at interpreting body language
  • Remembers faces

THE WHOLE BRAIN. Whole-brained people have both the characteristics of the right and left brain. They can use the side of the brain which is best to deal with a situation. Whole brain favouring either side means that the person is generally whole brained, but stronger at one side.
