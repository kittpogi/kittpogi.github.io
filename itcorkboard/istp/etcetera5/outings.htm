    Summer Outing 2000 (and other happenings)
 

Summer Outing 2000 (Apr 28-30)

Summer isn't summer if one doesn't get a taste of the sun, sea and sand of the beach. Definitely, for those who went to the summer outing, their summer is more than enough, just overflowing.

Capping off the school year, the summer outing gives us a breather from the usual work routine and a chance to bond with our officemates and enjoy the scenery of a place very different from the Taft Avenue landscape. The destination this year was Villa del Mar, a pristine beach way up in Pagudpud, Ilocos Norte, a good 560 km away from Manila.

(Apr 28) The 12-hour trip didn't dampen the high spirits of the ITPs a bit as they looked forward to swimming on the beach and doing all the "demented" things they can do during their stay. The first night was a real "ice breaker". It seems we can't get enough of the Videoke sessions during our Friday night activities so we did try to belt out the high notes in faraway Pagudpud where no one knows us. We also did "Charades" and as usual, the guys beat the girls although I think they ate most of our Flat Tops.

(Apr 29) It's a brand new day for an adventure and most of us went touring. First stop was the Bangui View Deck, which is like Mines View Park without the mines, the houses below and the Baguio chill. Then we went to an old church in Bacarra. Well, Ilocos is famed for its old churches especially the one in Paoay. Next in the itinerary is Laoag City where we get to visit Fort Ilocandia and the local market for some "pasalubong-shopping" and to satisfy Brian's RDA (Recommended Daily Allowance). About two hours later, we're back in Pagudpud for our lunch and some rest.

After dinner that evening, we played Limbo Rock and a Personality Trivia game of sorts over the bonfire. This night was also an Awards Night as awards with out-of-this world categories like "Tulo Laway King" and "Plakda Queen" among others were handed out to the "deserving" people who were a bit surprised about it.

(Apr 30) Time to go back to Manila but first, it's time to hear mass and thank the Lord for a very wonderful and enjoyable outing. But it's not over yet for we still have to visit and frolic around the streets of Vigan that afternoon.

(May 1) After a grueling 16 hour road trip, finally we're back in Manila. We're way behind the schedule but it's alright. The Summer Outing was a "success". And we have the hardworking people from the Outing Committee to thank for.

Tagaytay Picnic (January 29, 2000)

In one of the spur-of-the-moment "gimmicks" the Center pulled off, this picnic may well be considered the most successful. The Tagaytay Picnic Grove was the venue for the picnic (kaya nga Picnic Grove, eh!) where the view was simply beautiful and the wind was, well, parang si Rod Navarro. What made this event special was the kids of the ISTPs who came to the picnic. It's very rare to be with people with the same age as I am. Kailan kaya ang kasunod?
