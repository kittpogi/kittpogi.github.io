Creative Takes on Exercise
Contributed by: Julio Ceres R. Ibay

  • Use stairs instead of the elevator or escalator. When at home, climb to the second floor as often as possible. For variety, take the stairs two at a time, or step up the pace.

  • Walk when you can. Park an extra block from your destination or at the rear of the parking lot. Look for the longest, rather than the shortest route.

  • Get up from your chair (permanently lose the TV remote) or get out of your car more often (avoid drive-through windows).

  • When unloading groceries from your car, carry one bag at a time into the house.

  • Start a hobby that makes you move, e.g., gardening.

  • View chores like lawn mowing, dusting, and vaccuming as opportunities to exercise.

  • Turn off the TV and play with the children. This will tax you as much as you allow.

  • Try dancing. There are styles to suit any preference.

  • Learn a new sport. Take golf, tennis, or racquetball lessons.

  • Do double duty. When you shop for groceries, move quickly down the aisles. You may miss some items and have to backtrack, but that only adds to your workout.

  • Join groups that are active, like mall walkers, hikers, or bicyclists.

This article is extracted from sports medicine website written by Dr Stamford.

Dr Bryant Stamford is director of the Health Promotion and Wellness Center and professor of exercise physiology in the School of Education at the University of Louisville, Kentucky. He is also an editorial board member of The Physician and Sportsmedicine. Remember: This information is not intended as a substitute for medical treatment. Before starting an exercise program, consult a physician.
