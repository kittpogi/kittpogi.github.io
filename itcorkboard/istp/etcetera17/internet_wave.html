Racing Into the Internet Wave
By: Irwin S. Jurilla

What indeed is the Internet? The Internet is a technology, a tool and a way of life to millions of its enthusiasts.

It is a distributed and open technology which has no central location. Its operating codes are no secrets. Everyone can contribute to the design and development of the overall system. Even an elementary grade Internet fan can make its own web site.

The Internet has dropped out of the sky like a bomb and blew up into the growing modern society as it increases in size yearly. Many have been attracted to the Internet with great passion since the introduction of the personal computer over two decades ago. This is the first technology of its kind to achieve global accolades.

The technology that led to the invention of the Internet has been designed as a communication system that can withstand or survive nuclear holocausts for the military. In 1968, Computer Engineer Ray Tomlinson worked as a computer engineer for Bolt Beranek and Newman (BBN), the company hired by the United States Defense Department to build the first Internet. Towards the end of 1971, he invented the Internet-based electronic mail (e-mail). The first e-mail was sent between two computers that were actually sitting besides each other. The first email message was "QWERTYUIOP"

The Internet was up and running by 1983. In its complex history of innovation flowing to and from the Internet, starting from sending a simple e-mail, it evolved into a subversive virtual society. Over the decades, the Internet has demonstrated that it is not subject to privatization, centralization or control. Anything under the sun can be held captive by this invention.

From then on, the Information Age has begun in earnest. Information has been the primary commodity of free enterprise. The business, economy and lifestyle trends, among others, have been transformed with the structural shift of information. In the past, information was crude, based on paper, unorganized, and mostly isolated. Today with the new technology, information is networked, digitized and efficiently propagated.

The Internet levels the playing field of international markets. In the growing wired and digital information, communication reaches the consumers worldwide and is accessible in an insignificant cost through the Internet.

The Internet is the one of the most significant new tools for business in small to large enterprises, for scientific or military purposes, even for a beginner trying to send an e-mail across the globe. The Internet is so intense and rich in information as it has the ability to communicate with a global audience in tens of millions at the same time. Everyone is racing into the Internet wave; the window to the world.
