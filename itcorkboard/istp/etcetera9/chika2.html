Creatures of the Night, Halloween has come once again, And we filipinos also have our own myths and legends, Read on below:

THE MANANANGGAL is a headless being who roams about all night frightening people whom he meets, but who at daybreak resumes his human form. And like the aswang, the manananggal removes the entrails of his victims. ("Manananggal" is derived from the Tagalog word "tanggal" which means "to remove".)

THE ASWANG is the most dreaded of supernatural beings and is a person who assumes other forms, like that of a dog, pig, horse, or carabao, when he or she roams about at night in search of prey, particularly sick persons or expectant mothers. The form an aswang takes is usually extraordinarily large and of completely black color. In addition, the aswang can fly at night but only their head and intestines do so leaving the rest of their body behind on their bed. People are often warned against sleeping with their bellies exposed because an aswang might steal their intestines as they sleep. When the intended victim is not asleep, the aswang would stand upside-down and then emit a strong odor which will make the victim incapable of moving. The aswang will then eat the victim's internal organs starting from the heart down to the intestines.

THE TIKBALANG is a harmless being, but when he took a fancy to someone, he would lure him to his haunts and tease him with frightening looks and antics.

The tikbalang is sort of like a "reverse-centaur" -- its lower body is human while its upper body is that of a horse.

MANGKUKULAM is the Filipino name given for the being called Witch .
In the past decades, they are represented as deformed human beings who have evil mystical powers with the ability to hurt or kill anyone for very different reasons.
Some believe that they can also transform themselves into any other formations or shape, both in animate or inanimate. In performing their rituals, they use weird ingredients such as frog intestines, flies, human parts, and the like. To be able to curse a specific person, they would get anything possessed by that individual, ranging from material items to body parts like hair. Then they execute their rituals for that person by representing him with a doll...anything they do to that doll, it happens to the person being cursed.

KAPRE The Kapre are gigantic creatures to terrify persons or harrass them with nightmares, diseases and similar misfortunes. They live in caves, woods, large trees, ravines, stony places and deserted buildings.

The smoke that comes out from the acacia tree in your backyard might be the smoke from a Kapre , a mammoth of a man who sits alone on balete and other large trees and likes to terrify people by changing its size and shape. When fired at, this black demon would swiftly turn into a banana trunk. A person brave enough to approach a Kapre should lasso it with a rope and tie it to the tree. The next morning, he will find that the rope has fallen empty on the ground and he should dig the same spot to find a jar of gold. He can only claim the treasures if no other person catches him digging the ground or carrying the jar of gold home.
Kapres are said to be afraid of fire or anything that ressembles its color such that Filipinos always have matches ready or wear things like gold watches and other gold items to avoid being taken as victims.
