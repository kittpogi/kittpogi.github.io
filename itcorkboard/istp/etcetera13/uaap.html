DLSU - ATENEO FINALS

Finally, after 14 years, Ateneo has won the UAAP Basketball Championship. Fourteen years when basketball players were wearing short shorts and Nike air was just around the corner. 14 years, that was way back in 1988, I was grade five then. All I knew was that Ateneo was La Salle's rival.

It was a battle of the best of the best! DLSU with a record of 13 - 1, and Ateneo the underdog which had to beat University of the East twice just to make it into the finals, heroics of Jec Chia made them get there and face the DLSU green archers.

I could not be more impressed with that… Jec Chia had heart and was able to sink that 2 pointer to beat UE. Yes Ateneo had heart but so did La Salle. La Salle has its own set of heroes… the likes of Mike Cortez…fans were cheering MVP for Mike. MVP … MVP…MVP.

What makes the UAAP much more spectacular than the PBA? Its because it's a rivalry of schools, a time when you had to line up at 5:00 am just to get lower box tickets. You get such a hard time getting tickets, it gets you frustrated not getting one! For all you know, everyone would be watching it, alumni, politicians, businessmen, college students, high school students, even toddlers of alumni.

Personally, I like meeting all my old college friends, who are there to watch the UAAP games, to exchange high fives wearing green shirts. That's the only time I get to see old friends, classmates, familiar faces, die hard alumni, the pretty cheer dancers, the coaches, the staff.
the players…where else do you see players jumping for the ball?

In the last 2 minutes of the game, it was bound to happen… It was…Ateneo's time.
I could not feel more numb, dumfounded, as the Ateneo crowd was at its ever crowning glory. Ateneo fans were cheering… ONE BIG FIGHT. Yup! Ateneo really felt victorious.
Silver confettis scattered from the ceiling, blue balloons fell down, and Villanueva was waving like an eagle, a glorious one. I could see the disappointed faces of Cardona and Cortez and other alumni. There is always a next time. Come to think of it, when will another La Salle - Ateneo championship happen again?…maybe next year, or 10 years from now. Still, my friends and I, were still keeping it alive… Hail, Hail Alma Matter, Hail to De La Salle…
There's a game we still have to keep on playing, the game called life. And we have to accept that we can't be always on the winning end.

Well. it was one heck of a season….Season 65! A showdown of talent, personality, grace, athleticism, courage, heart. Cheers to all the teams that played their best this season.

ANIMO LA SALLE!
