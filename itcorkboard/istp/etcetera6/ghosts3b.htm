    You be the judge


It all started while I was living in New Port R.I. A place rich in history and beautiful mansions and darn it I didn't get to live in one. I did however live in an old victorian house that was quite large and converted into two apartments. Our's was downstairs with an entrance way that ran the whole length of the house. As you came in the front door, off to your right were pocket doors to the living room. Usually they were left open except for when I was in there sleeping, because that is where I slept on a daybed. The front of the house had a large front window with large thick wooden shutters which were used during north easterners or hurricanes. My day bed was right under the window. I liked it because I could open the window, look at the stars, and hear the ocean.

To give you a better layout of the room, the pocket doors were to the left, a large fireplace to the right and another set of pocket doors facing north to the diningroom. It was rather a large room and quite comfortable.

Two days before, a Friday night to be exact, we had gone to the wharf to get fresh seafood to eat and look around because I had never seen one before. There were all kinds of fish, clams and a lot of poor lobsters. I felt so bad for them. I'm sure you are wondering why, right? Well back then they would drive wooden pegs into their claws so they couldn't grab you. That was so cruel, or so I thought, so I went around and took all the pegs out. There was this one lobster who had to have weighed at least twenty pounds. He or she was about three feet long with claws so big that I swear they could cut your arm off if it got a hold of you. I wanted to buy it but my boyfriend wouldn't pay thirty dollars for it and then I wanted to sneek it outside and drop it into the ocean but the old party poop said no, I would go to jail if I got caught. Darn, well I tried.

We got our food and took it outside to the tables they had to eat it, then went home. Later I started to get terrible stomach pains and started to throw up. I was so sick I thought I was going to die and then I was hopping I would. The next day all I did was stay in bed, sick as a dog.

Sunday morning rolled around and I slept late. When I did wake up I just laid there with my eyes shut enjoying the feeling of not being sick anymore. I heard the doors open, thinking it was my boyfriend coming to check on me and just laid there pretending to be asleep. I heard no movement, it was so quiet, then I got this terrible feeling something was not right. It became so very cold and the air was hard to breath. I was absolutely frozen with fear. Then I felt it....some 'thing' sat on my bed and then laid down beside me. As it put it's arm around me I could feel this terrible cold through the covers and on the back of my neck. I wanted to scream but I couldn't, I couldn't move. My mind was screaming for help but my mouth wouldn't move. Then the front door opened and my boyfriend and his parents came in. It vanished, just like that, gone. What a relief! I'm not ashamed to say that after that, it wasn't long before I left for home.

I don't know what or who it was and don't care. I'll let you be the judge.






This last story is one that is from the most difficult period of my life, one I have trouble talking about. So please bear with me on this.

The parts I'm going to tell you about all started on oct.28, 1981. It was in the middle of the afternoon while I was sitting in the living room watching tv when everything around me faded away and I heard a loud humming my ears. Before my eyes I could see a car accident and had a strong feeling of death. It only lasted a few moments but it was long enough to leave me shaking with dread. I've had premonitions before but never of this magnitude. I could only surmise that it had to do with my youngest son Chris because he walked to school and had to cross a busy street.

The next morning I walked him to school and as we were about to cross the street a car pulled out of a side street and was broadsided by a van. The car was shoved in our direction and almost hit us, but we jumped out of it's way in time. My premonition was right, I saved Chris.

Later that day my oldest son Richie came home from school and I told him that a friend had been calling him and that he wanted him to return his call. He did, and then said he was going over there for a little while but would be home soon. That was the last time I saw him alive.

The next night I was laying in bed not able to sleep and I looked at the clock , it said three a.m. when I heard someone or something come up on the porch. I absolutely froze. You've heard the expression ice in your vains? Well that's what it felt like. Whatever was there knocked on the door three times. Knock......knock.....knock, I knew that what was there was not human but I didn't know what it wanted except that it wasn't good. Then I heard it leave. Thank God!

Later I was told by someone that I had cheated death and it had come to get who it was suppose to. If that were true than why did my other son die? Was he a replacement? You be the judge.
