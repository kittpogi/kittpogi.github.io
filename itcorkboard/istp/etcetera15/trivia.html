Firsts in Philippine Sports

Contributed by: Francis Mark Santos

  • Benjie Paras is the first player to win both Rookie of Year award and Most Valuable Player Award in one season in the PBA
  • Robert Jaworski, now a senator was the first Pinoy sports figure to make it to the Guiness Book of World Record. His feat, being the oldest active basketball player
  • Ramon Fernandez is the PBA's all-time leading scorer followed by Abet Guidaben.
  • The most fouled player in Philippine Basketball Association history is Ramon Fernandez.
  • The first PBA player to score over 100 points in a single game is Michael Hackett.
  • La Salle argueably is the only team to win 2 grand slam titles in the UAAP (1989-1991, 1998-2001).
  • Francis Arnaiz - the only Toyota player to be included in PBA's first Mythical 5 selection in 1975.
  • Bogs Adornado is the 1st PBA MVP, 1975-1976 Season.
  • Gil Cortez of Toyota was the first recipient of PBA's Rookie of the Year Award (1976).
  • The first ever international Grandmaster from Asia was Eugenio Torre who won at the Chess Olympiad in Nice, France in 1974.
  • The first Filipino boxing champion to defend his title in Manila was Pancho Villa in 1925.
  • Lope "Papa" Sarreal produced 22 Filipino world champions in boxing.
  • Dodie "Boy" Penalosa was the first Filipino boxer to win world titles in two different weight divisions.
  • Pancho Villa was the first Filipino world-boxing champion.
  • Former senator Lorenzo Tanada was the country's top soccer player in the 1920's.
  • The team from Zamboanga City won the 1992 Little League World Series and then lost it by disqualification.
  • Jennifer Rosales at 15 became the youngest winner of the National Ladies Golf Open (1994)
  • Antonio Arzala was the first winner of the lung-busting Tour of Luzon.
  • The first ever Surfing Tournament in the Philippines was held in Daet, Camarines Norte on September 1-2, 1995.
  • The first Filipino golfer to play in the US Masters was Ben Arda.
  • Paeng Nepumuceno is the Filipino to win the World Cup for Bowling 4 times. He managed to put himself in the Guiness Book of World Records for this feat.
  • The first Filipina olympians are track runners Francisca Sanopal and Manolita Cinco together with swimmers Gertrudes Lozada at Jocelyn von Giese at the 1956 Melbourne Olympics
  • The First Filipino Olympian is David Nepomuceno. He alone put up the first Philippine Athletic Contingent during 1924 in Paris. He joined the 100- and 200-meter track sprints but did not qualify.
