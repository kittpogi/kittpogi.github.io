    Spot the Mistakes...
 

Here are the answers...

  1. Not paying attention
    Looking away while someone else is talking to you is bad manners. Paying attention is one of the great keys of etiquette and the greatest compliment to pay another person. For best results: also respond with appropriate facial expressions and body movements, and show interest even if you must fake it!
  2. Limp handshake
    A limp handshake suggests you are insecure and unsure of yourself. Make yours firm. This shows confidence, warmth, openness and sincerity. But don't be a bone-crusher! This pegs you as dominating and insensitive.
  3. Body turned away
    Your body language can give you away! If you're not facing your companion squarely, you might be showing a distinct lack of interest. This can be insulting and trigger negative, even hostile feelings towards you.
  4. Smoking
    Today, most public meetings are strictly nonsmoking. Light up and you're instantly disliked by those who don't like smoke and by those who do, but obey the rules. Even if cigarettes are allowed, often cigars are not. And never, ever let ashes drop on the floor - always find an ashtray.
  5. Holding drink in wrong hand
    Always hold your drink in your left hand. Then you can shake someone's hand with your right hand easily, without fuss and without spilling your drink over both of you. Also, this helps your right hand stay warm and dry - holding an iced drink can make your hand cold and clammy.
  6. Not wearing jacket
    Never take off any clothing, such as your jacket, or loosen your tie unless the host or hostess does first and specifically says you can, too. It is bad form to "dress down" at a social gathering without the permission, or the lead, of those who are hosting it.
  7. Too much alcohol
    Control your drinking! You might think you're having a great time but those around you will think less of you from that moment on. If someone overindulges and becomes loud and abusive, even a top executive, that person should have his or her car keys confiscated and be escorted home. Otherwise that person can ruin the enjoyment of everyone else and destroy their reputation at the same time.
  8. Standing too close
    We each have a "comfort zone" around us of about three feet. We don't like others to invade this space without being invited to. When someone does, we try to back away - and we dislike the transgressor for being so intimidating and insensitive.
  9. Talking loudly
    Don't talk with a loud voice. It makes you seem over-bearing and self-important. Your voice should be calm and even, and your tone and volume should suit the time and situation.
  10. It's 9:00 p.m.!
    A cocktail party should never run to more than two hours. Start at 6:00 p.m. and end at 8:00 p.m. at the latest. Stop the bar 30 minutes after the party was supposed to end to discourage late drinking.
  11. Hand on shoulder
    Unless you are old friends who always hug when meeting each other, the only safe place to touch another person is the hand, when you shake hands. It is dangerous to touch any other part of the body without permission. You might think your gesture is innocent, but others might see it as much more suggestive.
  12. Talking at the same time
    If someone else is talking, it is the worst kind of rudeness to interrupt. Let him finish before responding. You'll appear polite and he can better hear and understand what you're saying. Also, the secret to becoming a secret conversationalist is to listen well.
