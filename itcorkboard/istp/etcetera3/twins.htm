    Logic Puzzles
 

Twin Criminals

Three well-known criminals Rey, Rob, and Rex were rounded up for questioning regarding a robbery that happened in Quiapo. Now, Rey and Rex happened to be identical twins and few people could tell them apart. All three suspects had elaborate records, and a good deal was known about their personalities and habits. In particular, the twins were quite timid, and neither one has ever dared nor will dare to pull a job without an accomplice. Rob, on the other hand, was quite bold and despised ever using an accomplice. Also, several witnesses testified that at the time of the robbery, one of two twins was seen drinking at a bar in Malate, but it was not known which twin.

Again, assuming that no one other than Rey, Rob, and Rex was involved in the robbery, which ones are innocent and which ones guilty?

 

Juan's Children

Juan and Pedro meet and while chatting, their talk also leads to their families. Questioned for the ages of his three children, Juan answers: "The product of the ages of my three children is 72."

Pedro thinks for a moment and then states that this is not enough information. Juan therefore says: "Well, have a look at the building number outside the entrance door... that's the sum of their ages."

This having done Pedro comes back shaking his head... "I still don't know..."

"Alright", Juan says, "I'll give you one more hint: my youngest child is named Ann." How old are the three children?

Clue: Pedro and Juan are both logicians.
