Systems Management Office : Updates

Online Faculty Information Update Form (FIUF)
By: Ryan Salvanera

FIUF is a web-based application developed using ASP (Active Server Pages) technology with a FoxPro database. It was designed to complement the manual submission of update forms to the EVP office. A report will then be generated and verified by the EVP office. These verified data would then be used for the promotion of the faculty members. The application is scheduled for integration with My.LaSalle as soon as faculty members are given access to the portal. Data from the application will also be downloaded into the Faculty Information System (FIS).


Online Enrollment
By: Rhaniel Bingcang

ITC developed an online enrollment program to lessen the crowd of students at the Registrar's Office during enrollment. The program was implemented during the First Term of SY 2001-2002. With this program, students can now enroll by themselves at the school's laboratories, at home, at any cyber cafés, or at any place where there is Internet access. They can even view their assessment of fees online. The online enrollment program allows only students who are currently enrolled, those who were able to enlist duringonline enlistment, those who do not have any clearance problem, and has an MLS account.

Helpdesk for Physical Facilities Office (PFO)
by: Agnes Bulay

Tired of broken keyboard drawers? Malfunctioning aircons? Twinkling fluorescent lamps? Who you gonna call? … The PFO fighters!

Yes! That is, the PFO, the main function of which is to plan, organize and supervise all changes, improvements and renovations of the University's physical facilities . Specifically it supervises the planning, installation, maintenance, repair and improvement not only of the equipment, furniture and fixtures but also the technical and system needs of the University. It also supervises and monitors all contracts of in-house and outside companies insofar as the maintenance of elevators, water coolers, fire fixtures, typewriters, and other equipments are concerned.

With these functions, the Help Desk System of ITC is being evaluated, whether it can also be used by PFO personnel to facilitate the organization and supervision of service or work orders being handled by PFO . PFO's Help Desk System should be able to speed up the procedures of handling and monitoring work orders by computerization. By computerization, we mean access to accurate information and faster generation of significant reports and information.

Currently, the University's IT Center - Systems Management Office is in the process of defining the system requirements of the would-be-new PFO Help Desk System. A thorough investigation of their current operations, specifically of the two divisions -- the Mechanical/Electrical Division and the Civil Works Division, took place so that the flow of their procedures could be documented, and areas of improvement could be identified, items which should be incorporated into the PFO Help Desk System.

Employment Information (EmI) System
By: Agnes Bulay

Are you a graduating student? Wanna be listed in the top 500 corporations in the world? Then check this out! Be cool! Be part of the graduate listings generated by EmI! What's EmI? EmI stands for Employment Information - a system developed by the programmers of the University's IT Center-Systems Management Office.

One of EmI's function is to assist the University's Office of Career Services (OCS) in the supervision and maintenance of the student graduates' information. As the office title implies, it helps graduating students in job hunting and other related activities. EmI enables students to enlist and be part of the graduate listing which in turn are sent to companies who then subscribed for these graduate listings. Thus, it also maintains information on subscribing companies.

Apart from that, a computerized survey is also a part of the system EmI which is GEIS - Graduate Employment Information System. This survey system aims to collect the latest information on the alumni of the University providing it with the current career developments of its graduates.

Presently, revisions are being done to the system in order to address the changing and growing needs of OCS. The revised system will be due for implementation this mid-October.
