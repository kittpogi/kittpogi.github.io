Office of the Director : Updates

Subscription to MosCom
 by: Samuel P. Mallare

The use of the 2.4-meter satellite dish was able to conserve DLSU some Php 2,821,500 and another substantial savings amounting to US$841,550 due to our subscription to MosCom. Despite the big savings, ITC is still negotiating with MosCom to lower their pricing since DLSU is already requiring high Internet bandwidth. Moreover, the Office of the Director is coordinating with some telecommunication companies and the Legal Counsel in looking for cheaper options for the Piltel E1 line in order to save about Php28,000 a month.

PREGINET project
by: Samuel P. Mallare

The ITC Director is set to evaluate the PREGINET project of DOST-ASTI. In 1994, DOST also spearheaded the PHnet project, wherein some big Universities, including DLSU, were able to acquire leased line to connect to the Internet.

Expansion of Internet services
by: Samuel P. Mallare

Mr. Federico Gonzalez, EVP-PSI, wants ITC to provide Internet Services to EGI Taft Tower where the EVP Office will consequently be housed. On the other hand, SPM is currently coordinating with telecommunication companies and other IT Centers for DLSU-Dasmarinas' connection to PSI and DLS-Zobel's link upgrade.

IT System Committee activities
by: Samuel P. Mallare

IT Committee will hold a workshop on November 13 and 20 focusing on the long-term plan for the DLSU-System.


ITC Partnering with the Library

 ITC has partnered with the Library to provide network cards which the Library can lend out to students for wireless access to the campus network and internet. Wireless access is currently available in the 2nd and 4th floor of the Library, Conservatory, Gokongwei Lobby, ground floor and "tambayan area" of Velasco Hall, "tambayan area" beside the Miguel Hall, and ground floor of St. Joseph Building.

In addition, ITC also provided the University Library with PC cameras that could provide PC-based video conferencing to the campus. This tool is good for video conferencing between small groups of 1 to 4 people, or maybe 5 if they can all squeeze in. The PC cameras are a much cheaper alternative. These are stationed at the EDRC and Ortigas Room of the Library. Smaller versions of the video conferencing facility is also available at M306 e-classroom.
