Learn Quality through PRIO Workshops
By: Rina U. Alano

We learned to crawl before we began to walk, to walk before we could run. Quality movements benefit from similar progression and can require similar amounts of time to grow from the first hesitant steps to true coordination and stamina.

Understanding quality means understanding that everything is a series, a series of identifiable, manageable steps that combine to make a product or deliver a service.

PRIO team will assist the staff and personnel of the university in the series of steps of knowing the quality framework in different stages. So where do we start? The most basic step is through education and training. Our group has a series of courses in store for the community that is related to work standards and quality related concepts. Trainings to watch out for are:

Total Quality Management

The consistent, structured application of management commitment, leadership, customer focus, total participation, and systematic analysis to an organization's processes in order to achieve customer satisfaction.

Quality conscious people are created not found. The objective of this course is to give the participants basic grounding of ideas, concepts and tools before they can begin the concepts of continuous improvement, empowerment and effective problem solving.

Customer is Always the Customer

The customer is the lifeblood of a business. To make customers loyal to us we must provide them an excellent service. Customer service is anything we do for the customer that enhances the customer experience.

The main objective of this course is to learn customer needs personally and how to attain customer satisfaction at all cost.

Quality in Education

The course will cover comprehensive plans, strong TQM leadership at every level of the University that has "bought" TQM as a way of life and discrete metrics that accurately reflect process health and provide the basis for continuous improvement.
