DE LA SALLE UNIVERSITY BAGS ITS 4TH TITLE THIS SEASON

Defense and poise keyed defending champion La Salle's claim on its fourth straight title as it held archrival Ateneo scoreless for nearly two minutes in the fourth quarter to tally a thrilling 93-88 win in front of an overflow crowd at the Araneta Coliseum.

Finally exhuming the ghost of 1988, when they lost 70-76 to the Blue Eagle squad, the Archers grabbed their sixth title overall since joining the league in 1986.

The two-week-long delay seemed to affect La Salle's bid as it let Ateneo control the tempo of the match in the first half before finally edging the Eagles in the last period to finish the series 2-1.

Mike Cortez, given the sportsmanship award this year, scored 12 of his 23 points in the second half including six points in the third period when La Salle made their comeback from an 11-point halftime deficit and closed the gap to two 62-64 at the end of the quarter. He also had seven rebounds, seven assists and one block.

Tied at 83-all after Magnum Membrere's layup off Ren Ren Ritualo's defense, rookie Carlo Sharma scored on a three-point play at the other end, while drawing the fifth foul from the season's Most Valuable Player (MVP) Rich Alvarez in the process, to put La Salle up 86-83, with 1:51 left in the match.

Ateneo finally scored after almost a two-minute drought courtesy of a split free throw by Membrere time down to 59 seconds. Ritualo, who was judged Finals MVP, then missed on his triple try in the return play as LA Tenorio grabbed the defensive board, but BJ Manalo was waylaid by a foul from Fonacier as he stole a pass intended for Membrere.

Manalo then made one of his charities for an 89-84 count. Ritualo and Cortez made it 93-84, to the delight of the huge green and white gallery, after successive misses by Membrere and Rainier Sison from the three-point area with 14 ticks left. Tenorio then made four straight points from the free throw line for the final tally.

Playing perfect basketball in the first half, Ateneo scored 13 points off La Salle's six miscues at the same time limiting their turnovers to just two. They also shot 56 percent from the field to take a 50-39 halftime advantage.

Villanueva was the missing factor for the Eagles as he was limited to five points by Sharma's solid defense. He managed to grab nine boards but failed to control the 6'6" Sharma who had a monstrous 22-point and 14-rebound production.

Ritualo, playing his last year as an Archer, went scoreless in the first half but made 10 of his 15 points in the pivotal third quarter. Manalo chipped in 11 points while Willy Wilson scored eight markers off the bench, all in the deciding period.

Tenorio had a game-high 30 points, five rebounds, two assists and two steals and was backed up by Membrere's 14 points and Sison's 12 markers. Alvarez was the only other Eagle who ended up in double digits with 10 points and 10 caroms.
