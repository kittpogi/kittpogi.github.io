Another ITC Day Next Year? Let's Hear What the Survey Says...
by: Joie Alvarez

Fun, cooperation, tiring, team spirit, exciting, challenging, bonding, good food, bloopers, learning experience. Those are the words I can link with the recently concluded ITC Day. Since it was the first time the IT Center hosted such event, the ITPs were quite uncertain of what to expect from the visitors; many had a degree of apprehension whether or not the visitors will appreciate the activities and exhibits. Having a good foresight, the Team Building Committee prepared an online survey to be accomplished by the visitors after viewing the whole exhibit. The survey aimed to gather the visitors' opinion on the ITC Day. I guess, the result of this survey will most probably determine whether the ITPs should look forward to another ITC Day come June 2002 or just consider the whole idea as part of ITC's history. Well, let's see what the survey said…

First, it was a relief to know that only a single soul found the ITC Day boring. Interesting, high-tech and exciting were how the visitors noted the event. A few said it was captivating and enlightening. These adjectives only made certain that all our preparations for that day were not in vain. The 38 visitors who participated in the survey, to a varying degree, appreciated the displays, exhibits, demonstrations, and the event as a whole.

Second, it was saddening to know that more than half of the respondents did not know that ITC developed information systems, acquired and allocated computer facilities, and designed and maintained the DLSU web sites. Maybe this same half were also the ones who were not aware that ITC was the one behind the DLSU Web Sites, WISe, Active Desktop, Online Survey System, IDA, FAM, Help Desk System, and My.Lasalle.

And finally, what the visitors would remember the most during the ITC Day? Do I hear food? Well, if food was included in the survey, it might have taken the highest rating. Please take note, the highest rate went to the PowerPoint presentation on the History of ITC - Our Story, ITC (ehem, ehem… did anyone see how many times I voted? J). Kidding aside, I guess many would agree that it was a real neat presentation and the trivia quiz was quite amusing (I wish I was not the one assigned to write this article… well, too much for that "egotistic" tendencies). Tied on the second spot were the Evolution of DLSU Web Sites and Video Clips of Interviews. Well, well, find something in common from these exhibits? The Docu Office authored them all! What can I say, Docu people have a real sense of artistry! But wait, let's also give a round of applause for the exhibit on Evolution of Computer Media which ranked third. In fairness to other exhibitors, I'm sure everyone did their best and should be given due credit. Unfortunately, the posters were not given much attention. The IDA system was the highest rater in the product demo category. For the pride and joy of the one who performed the demo, other demos did not come close to IDA. On the other hand, I think the video conferencing should have been a big hit that day only if those assigned to that demo were a little more zealous in presenting the technology. So next time, try to learn some marketing tricks, and it really helps having a mascot J.

To round it all up, I think the ITC Day was a good idea to boost up the consciousness of the DLSU community on what the Center really does. That we don't just sit here pounding our keyboards for nothing. The event was a modest way of showing that we are doing a great deal of clever work. If many are not aware of our good products and services, where do the credits go? A student even asked me if our web pages are being paid per page. Obviously, he doesn't know that it was ITC's talented Web Development Team who creates and maintains the DLSU websites. We should not allow ourselves to accept only blames (for a slow internet connection, unavailable site, and crashing systems). Once in a while, we should also show them that there are a lot of good cooking going on here in ITC. Way to go ITC!
