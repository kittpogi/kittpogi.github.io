Process Review and Improvement Office : Updates

PRIO Busy Buddies
by: Teodoro Soliva, Jr.

Any PRIO updates? Well, after several days of brainstorming during our planning sessions last January 2001, we have collaborated in completing and standardizing the PRIO Business Process and quality manual.

The inclusions of MS. Rina U. Alano, and MS. Janet O. Chua to PRIO and their inputs were instrumental to standardize PRIO Document System Structure.

The new Document System Structure is integrated into six (6) parts, these are:

  1. MISSION, VISION, QUALITY POLICY, CORE VALUES, QUALITY SERVICE OBJECTIVES
    1. This part provides the basis and guiding direction on how the quality management system was designed and installed.
  2. OPERATIONAL POLICIES
    1. The operational policies are found in the Quality Manual. It covers Quality Management System Description, Administrative and Business Process policies.
  3. QUALITY SYSTEM MAPS (Business Process Maps, Quality Plan, Core Competency Table, Document and Data Map, Compliance Map).
    1. These maps show where relevant sources are deployed.
  4. PROCEDURES
    1. Procedures describe the various activities that take place in the company and who is responsible for them.
    2. This covers Administrative and Business Process procedures.
  5. WORK INSTRUCTIONS, STANDARDS, GUIDES, REFERENCES
    1. Details how to do specific task and provide more information in quality management system execution.
  6. FORMS OF QUALITY RECORDS
    1. These are objective evidences that policies, procedures and work instructions are being implemented.

With this being the PRIO's guiding structure in streamlining and documenting processes, we, as a group expect more projects to come and extend a helping hand to ITC as an efficient entity in providing support to all offices in the university and possibly to all La Salle system (haneeeeeep!).

PRIO Rejuvenated
by: Teodoro Soliva, Jr.

Life must go on…they say, if something unfortunate happens. We feel sad, when someone close to our heart has to leave, every reason behind must be understood and respected. But, we must not feel left out, we have to move on and continue to do what is expected of us and do whatever we can to satisfy ourselves and those who rely on us.

PRIO let go of MS. Eileen D. Manabat, a Research Assistant for three years. One of the surviving PRIO pioneers so to speak. A great loss, I may say, but any reason she had was for her own good and maybe a good career decision she had to make.

Lose some, win some…PRIO hired the services of Rina U. Alano, previously employed in a consulting firm as an associate consultant and Janet O. Chua, previously employed as a project assistant also from a consulting firm.

Rina U. Alano, immediately was assigned as the 5S Committee Head to spearhead housekeeping programs and was tasked also to collaborate with me in documenting the PRIO Document System Structure, in which, it is on it's approval stage and was presented during the ITC Day last June 22, 2001.

Janet O. Chua was hired just before the ITC Day celebration and was tasked to prepare the invitation form and signages. For the said event, she also prepared and presented the Quality Evolution during the ITC Day.

For a neophyte like them, an overwhelming effort that is.

Another milestone for PRIO was the successful implementation of the modified procedure of the Adjustment process. This was attained with the collaborative efforts of Tricia, Joel and Eileen through the guidance of the acting Coordinator, Amy.

With the implementation of the PRIO Document System Structure, the inclusion of Rina and Janet to the group and the continuing effort of Tricia, Joel and myself through the guidance of Amy, we as a group expect more projects to come and to extend a helping hand to ITC to be an efficient entity in providing support to all offices in the university and possibly to all La Salle system members.

First Day at ITC
by: Rina Alano

Our firsts in our lives usually leaves big impression on our minds, whether it is a first kiss, first crush or first love. My first kiss is sweet, my first crush is Tom Cruise, my first love is romantic and my first day at ITC is very warm (Summer kasi, mainit!). ITC Staff welcomes me as if I'm a newborn babe (oink!) in the family. They were all friendly and nice. I had a grand tour at DLSU campus with the dashing and debonair JGK (feeling cute, a.k.a. Piolo Pascual, as his fans fondly called him). New members of ITC staff (I'm there, of course) had a meeting with ITC Director, Samuel Mallare, to give us an inspirational thought and share our views on our respective jobs. I spent the rest of the day reading the Faculty Manual, browsing DLSU website and asking PRIO staff about the group functions, role of ITC in the campus and other rules and regulations of DLSU. Like my other first in my life, my first day at ITC is exciting and educational, a well remembered day in my adult life.
