5S Philosophy: Guide for Continuous Improvement
by: Rina Alano

As in most other programs developed in Japan, there are a number of underlying philosophies inherent in the 5-S (Seiri-Sort, Seiton-Systematize, Seiso-Sweep, Seiketsu-Sanitize, Shitsuke-Self Discipline) program. It is always best to establish a common understanding of the philosophy before going into the implementation of the program. According to the article of "5S: The First Step Toward World Class Management" by Prof. Etsu Inaba (currently the Institute's Don Manuel Elizalde Professor of Business Management), these philosophies are:

Productivity comes from the elimination of waste.

Whatever you do in the office or factory, there is a cost associated with it. The time spent looking for a piece of information is costing you and your company.

It is necessary to attack the root cause of a problem, not just the symptoms.

Once you start the 5S program, you will feel the urge to keep the workplace clean and neat because the time and effort to clean it is going to increase if you don't take care of it regularly.

Participation of everyone.

If you leave the cleaning to a specialist, a janitor for example, you will miss the important task of checking the process. Or, if you make exceptions for top management, the ITC Director for example, the rules and regulations you will set for others will soon be ignored. It is important; therefore, that everyone's participation is solicited for the 5S program.

Acknowledge that the human being is not infallible.

People forget things. People sometimes improvise. People often neglect rules and regulations. A certain structure therefore is needed to maintain a 5S program and constant reminders and trainings are important.


The program should not be taken lightly, as many companies have not been able to practice 5S, even though they want to. Top management understanding, commitment and positive involvement in the program are required. Without these, the true scientific nature of the program will not be realized. So, let these simple and positive thinking helps us implement the 5S program in our workplace.
