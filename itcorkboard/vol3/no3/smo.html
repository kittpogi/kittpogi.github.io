Systems Management Office : Updates

D L S U   L E A P
by Johnny Perpetuo Benitez

Last January 22, 2002 the ITC assisted the Office of Student Activities (OSAc) and the DLSU Student Council (SC) in conducting the Lasallian Enrichment Alternative Program (LEAP) using web and wireless technologies.

LEAP is an activity organized by the SC and OSAc wherein the students are allowed to enroll in alternative classes such as mountaineering, tarot card reading, palmistry, and the like, in lieu of their regular classes for a day. Due to the difficulty in the registration process that the SC and OSAc experienced the previous year, ITC’s assistance was requested to find an alternative solution to manual registration.

Using the My.LaSalle Portal, the Systems Management Office (SMO) developed a program which allowed students to browse and register in the alternative class of their choice. The program also allowed the students to view information about the subject such as the venue, time, speaker and the number of slots remaining. The students were also provided a facility to verify their registration and view the subject information thru their cellular phone using DLSU WISe.

Campus Events Organizer Update
by Ryan Q. Salvanera

The Campus Events Organizer (CEO) system, originally developed by OJT students, is currently undergoing revision. The system will help events organizer to manage the different activities in an event. Though most of the modules are working perfectly, the revisions are necessary for the system to comply with SMO conventions. The said system is scheduled to be up and running by Valentine's Day.

The CEO Registration, a module within the CEO system, will be next in line. This stand-alone system, also developed by OJT students, will also undergo minor modification. As an extension of the module, an online registration will be developed to allow participants to register via the web.

Oracle Modules Kick Off
by Brian Co

The Inventory, Fixed Assets, and Purchasing modules of Oracle financials are scheduled for implementation this year. The target date for both the Inventory and Fixed Asset modules is June 2002 and July 2002 for the Purchasing module. Oracle has assigned a new project consultant for DLSU in the person of Andrew Chong of Oracle Singapore.

The Inventory and Fixed Assets requirements definition meetings already started mid-January and Purchasing is scheduled to begin early February. Meanwhile, consultants from Oracle are continuing to help us implement or integrate the remaining modules of Oracle Financials.

The Oracle Financials Project involves people from ITC, the accounting office, purchasing, warehouse, and even internal audit working together. And it does not only involve DLSU-Manila and PSI but also the other system schools as well. With everybody's cooperation, we hope to accomplish what we set out to do and implement all relevant and needed Oracle Financials modules at all system schools.
